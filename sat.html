SAT (Scholastic Assessment Test)

SAT is a standardized entrance examination administered by the College Board for admission to undergraduate programs at universities and colleges, primarily in the United States and many other countries.

Exam Focus: The examination measures critical reading, writing, language, and mathematical reasoning skills.

Important: SAT scores are accepted by thousands of universities and colleges worldwide, including institutions in the United States, Canada, United Kingdom, Singapore, and many other countries.

Some private universities in India also accept SAT scores for undergraduate admissions.

Conducted by: College Board (USA).

📌 Quick Facts

Eligibility:

There are no official academic or age requirements set by the College Board.

SAT is primarily taken by students in Class 11 or Class 12.

Exam Pattern: The SAT consists of two sections: Reading & Writing and Mathematics.

Mode: Digital Computer-Based Test (administered via the Bluebook application).

Total Questions: 98 Questions.

Total Score: 400–1600 Points.

Duration: 2 Hours 14 Minutes (plus a 10-minute break between sections).

Frequency: Conducted multiple times per year (typically seven or eight times per academic cycle).

Qualification:

  • There are no official educational requirements set by the College Board.
  • The SAT is primarily taken by students in Class 11 or Class 12 who plan to apply for undergraduate courses.
  • Students from any stream (Science, Commerce, or Arts/Humanities) can take the SAT.

Age:

  • There is no minimum or maximum age limit for taking the SAT.

Minimum Marks:

  • There is no minimum percentage requirement in school examinations to register for the SAT.

Attempts:

  • There is no limit on the number of times a student can take the SAT.
  • Students may take the exam multiple times to improve their scores.

Nationality:

  • Students of any nationality can take the SAT.
  • The exam is offered in many countries around the world.

Mode: Digital Computer-Based Test conducted through the Bluebook application.

Frequency: Conducted multiple times per year.

Duration: 2 Hours 14 Minutes (plus a 10-minute break between sections).

Total Questions: 98 Questions.

Total Score: 400–1600 Points.

Question Type: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) and Student-Produced Responses in the Mathematics section.

Section Breakdown:

  • Reading & Writing: 54 Questions, 64 Minutes.
  • Mathematics: 44 Questions, 70 Minutes.

Exam Structure:

  • Each section is divided into two modules.
  • The test uses an adaptive format, where the difficulty of the second module depends on performance in the first module.
  • A calculator is allowed throughout the Mathematics section.

Scoring:

  • Reading & Writing: 200–800 Points.
  • Mathematics: 200–800 Points.
  • Total Score: 400–1600 Points.

Negative Marking: No negative marking.

Important: Universities consider SAT scores along with academic records, essays, recommendation letters, and other admission requirements.

Students with SAT scores can apply for a wide range of undergraduate programs at universities in the United States, Canada, the United Kingdom, Singapore, and several private universities in India.
