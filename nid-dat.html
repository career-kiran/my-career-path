NID DAT (Design Aptitude Test)

NID DAT is a national-level entrance examination conducted for admission to Bachelor of Design (B.Des) and Master of Design (M.Des) programmes.

A student interested in design appears for the NID DAT examination and qualifies the selection process.

Courses may include: B.Des, M.Des, Product Design, Communication Design, Animation Film Design, Textile Design, Furniture Design, and other design-related programmes.

Conducted by: National Institute of Design (NID).

📌 Quick Facts

Who Can Apply? Students who have completed or are appearing for Class 12 (for B.Des) or a bachelor's degree (for M.Des).

Eligibility: varies depending on the programme

Mode: Primarily offline assessments, including written, studio, and interview-based evaluations depending on the stage and programme.

Frequency: Usually conducted once a year(usually in January).

  • Qualification:
    • Must have passed Class XII (10+2) or equivalent qualifying examination from a recognized board.
    • Candidates currently appearing for their Class 12 final exams are also fully eligible to apply.
    • Open to students from all academic streams (Science, Commerce, Arts, and Humanities).
  • Age:
    • General / General-EWS: Must be under 20 years of age
    • OBC-NCL / SC / ST: Granted a 3-year relaxation
    • PwD: Granted a 5-year relaxation
  • Min mark:
    • There is no minimum aggregate percentage or cutoff mark required in your Class 12 boards.
    • You only need a simple "Pass" certificate from your educational board.
  • Attempts:
    • There is no official restriction on the number of attempts for the NID DAT.
    • You can appear for the exam across multiple years as long as you continue to meet the upper age limit criteria.

Mode of Examination: Round 1: Written Test (Prelims) | Round 2: Practical Studio Test (Mains)

Duration: 3 Hours for Prelims + 3 Hours for Mains

Medium: English

Total Marks: 100 Marks for Prelims + 100 Marks for Mains

Question Types: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs), Sketching, Storyboarding, and 3D Model Making

  • MCQ (Multiple Choice Questions): Choose one correct answer from the given options.
  • MSQ (Multiple Select Questions): More than one option may be correct. You must select all correct answers.
  • NAT (Numerical Answer Type Questions): No options are given. You must calculate and enter the numerical answer.

Marking Scheme:

  • Part 1 — GK & Aptitude (GAT): 1-mark and 2-mark multiple-choice questions testing logic and general knowledge. No negative marking.
  • Part 2 — Drawing & Creativity (CAT): Subjective drawing and design questions that make up the largest part of the Prelims score. No negative marking.
  • Round 2 — Studio Test (Mains): A practical exam involving hands-on model making with physical materials and observational exercises.

Important: You must pass the Round 1 (Prelims) cutoff to qualify for the Round 2 (Mains) exam.
