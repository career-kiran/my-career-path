🌐 Website Designer

🧠 Imagine this…

"A new bakery is opening in your area.

They want a website to show their cakes and menu online.

They ask you to design their website.

You decide how the homepage should look.

You choose colors, images, and arrange everything neatly.

When the website goes live, customers start visiting it."

If this feels exciting to you, you may enjoy being a Website Designer.

NOTE:

Website Designer → Designs how a website looks. (Focus on looks)

UI / UX Designer → Makes apps and websites easy to use. (Focus on usage)

🛡️ What does a Website Designer do?

  • Designs how a website looks.
  • Arranges text, images, and buttons on web pages.
  • Makes sure the website looks neat and easy to use.
  • Updates and improves the website when needed.

💖 You may enjoy this path if you...

  • Like creating things on a computer.
  • Enjoy arranging images, text, and colors neatly.
  • Like designing websites.

🎓 Courses to Become a Website Designer

Diploma Courses

  • Diploma in Web Design
  • Diploma in Web Development

Duration: 6 months – 2 years

Eligibility: 10th or 12th pass

Minimum Marks: Not strict

Exam to Pass: No entrance exam (direct admission)

Bachelor’s Degree Courses

  • B.Des - Bachelor of Design
  • B.Sc in Web Design
  • BCA (Bachelor of Computer Applications)

Duration: 3–4 years

Eligibility: 12th pass (any stream)

Minimum Marks: Around 50% (varies)

Entrance Exam: Some colleges may require exams like CUET. Others offer admission based on 12th marks.

Master’s Degree Courses

  • M.Des - Master of Design
  • MCA (Master of Computer Applications)

Duration: 2 years

Eligibility: Bachelor’s degree

Exam to Pass: Entrance exam or interview (in some colleges)

Short-Term Certification Courses

  • Web Design Certification
  • HTML, CSS & JavaScript Courses

Duration: Few weeks – 6 months

Eligibility: Open to anyone

Exam to Pass: No entrance exam

Note:

Many Website Designers also learn through online courses and by practicing their skills.

Building your own websites and projects helps improve your design skills.

⚠️ Important

Short certificate courses can help you learn web design skills quickly.

However, a Bachelor’s degree can give you deeper knowledge and better job opportunities in the long run.

✏️ Exam Details

  • CUET – Common University Entrance Test:

    Conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) for admission to many central universities.

  • University Entrance Exams:

    Some universities conduct their own entrance exams for courses like BCA or B.Sc in Web Design.

🏢 Where do Website Designers work?

  • IT Companies
    Design and maintain websites for businesses and organizations.
  • Web Development Agencies
    Create websites for different clients and companies.
  • Digital Marketing Companies
    Design websites and landing pages for online promotions.
  • Businesses and Startups
    Work with companies to design and improve their websites.
  • Work Independently (Freelancing)
    Take website design projects from different people and companies.

🌍 Global Opportunities

  • International Companies
    Work for companies in other countries.
  • Work From Home (Online Jobs)
    Work online from home for International companies.
  • Freelance Platforms (International Projects)
    Take website design work from people and companies in different countries.
  • Overseas Employment
    Get full-time jobs in countries like USA, UK, Canada, or Australia.

⭐ Career Insight

Website design is a skill-based career.

The more websites you build and practice, the better your opportunities.

Your skills and your creativity are very important in this field.
