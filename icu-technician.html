ICU Technician

An ICU Technician is a healthcare professional who supports doctors and nurses in the Intensive Care Unit (ICU). They help monitor critically ill patients and manage ICU equipment.

What does an ICU Technician usually do?

  • Monitor vital signs like heart rate, blood pressure, and oxygen levels
  • Assist nurses and doctors during emergency procedures
  • Operate and maintain ICU equipment such as monitors and ventilators
  • Help in shifting and positioning critically ill patients
  • Ensure ICU equipment is clean and ready for use

You may enjoy this career if you:

  • Can stay calm and focused in emergency situations
  • Like working in a hospital environment
  • Can follow strict instructions and safety protocols
  • Are comfortable working with critically ill patients
  • Want a technical role in healthcare
Real-life example:
In an ICU, a patient is connected to monitors and oxygen support. The ICU technician regularly checks the monitor readings, informs the nurse if anything changes, and assists doctors during urgent treatment.

Courses Available

Diploma in ICU Technology

  • Duration: 1 – 2 years
  • Eligibility: 12th pass (Science preferred)
  • Entrance exam: Usually not required
  • Admission: Merit-based or direct

BSc in Critical Care / ICU Technology

  • Duration: 3 years
  • Eligibility: 12th with Physics, Chemistry, Biology
  • Entrance exam: Varies by college (CUET / state-level exams)
  • Admission: Merit-based or entrance-based

Where do ICU Technicians work?

  • Hospitals
  • Intensive Care Units (ICU)
  • Emergency and trauma centers
  • Multi-specialty hospitals
Important note:
ICU Technicians work under the supervision of doctors and nurses. They support patient care but do not make treatment decisions independently.
