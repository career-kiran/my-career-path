State-Level Law Entrance Exams

State-Level Law Entrance Exams are entrance examinations conducted by individual state governments or state authorities for admission to law colleges within their respective states.

These exams are mainly used for admission to government, government-aided, and private law colleges in that state.

Examples: MH CET Law (Maharashtra), KLEE (Kerala), AP LAWCET (Andhra Pradesh), TG LAWCET (Telangana), and other state-level law entrance examinations.

Conducted by:Individual state governments or state authorities

Note:

Every state does not conduct its own law entrance examination.

Some states admit students through national-level examinations such as CLAT or CUET, while others offer admission based on Class 12 or graduation marks.

Always check the official website of the state or university you wish to apply.
