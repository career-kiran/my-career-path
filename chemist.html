Chemist

Who is a Chemist?

A chemist is a person who studies substances, how they are made, how they react, and how they can be changed to create useful products.

They work in laboratories and industries to develop medicines, materials, food products, and many everyday items we use.

You may enjoy this career if:

  • You enjoy science experiments
  • You are curious about how medicines, soaps, fuels, or materials are made
  • You like working in a laboratory
  • You are comfortable following safety rules
  • You like to focus and do things properly

Real Life Examples (India)

  • A chemist in a pharmaceutical company helps develop and test new medicines.
  • A chemist in a food testing lab checks products to make sure safety standards are followed.

Courses to Become a Chemist

BSc in Chemistry (3 years)

Eligibility: 12th with Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics / Biology

Minimum Marks: Usually 50–60% (varies by college)

Admission: Merit-based or CUET / State-level entrance exams

Integrated BS–MS in Chemistry (5 years)

Focus: Research-oriented program

Eligibility: 12th with Science stream

Entrance Exams: IISER Aptitude Test, JEE Advanced (varies by institute)

MSc in Chemistry (2 years)

Eligibility: BSc in Chemistry or related subject

Entrance Exams: CUET-PG, IIT JAM, University-level exams

PhD in Chemistry (3–5 years)

Eligibility: MSc in Chemistry

Entrance Exams: CSIR-NET, UGC-NET, GATE (varies by institute)

Where Do Chemists Work?

  • Pharmaceutical companies
  • Chemical industries
  • Food and beverage industries
  • Government laboratories
  • Research institutes
  • Universities and colleges
Important Note: To work as a research scientist in national laboratories or higher research positions, postgraduate studies (MSc) and often a PhD are usually required.
