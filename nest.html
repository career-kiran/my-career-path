NEET(National Eligibility cum Entrance Test)

NEST (National Entrance Screening Test) is a national-level entrance examination conducted for admission to integrated science programmes.

Integrated Science Programme is a course that combines undergraduate and postgraduate studies in science into a single programme.

Example: Instead of studying an undergraduate science degree 3 years and a postgraduate science degree 2 years separately, students can complete both through a single 5-year Integrated M.Sc programme.

Students can use their NEST score to apply for science programmes in participating institutions.

Conducted by: NISER Bhubaneswar and CEBS Mumbai.

📌 Quick Facts

Eligibility: 12th Pass or Appearing

Mode: Computer Based Test (CBT)

Questions: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

Frequency: Conducted once a year(usually June)

Qualification: Passed or appearing in Class 12 from a recognised board.

Appearing = currently in Class 12 (writing exams this year)

Subjects: Candidates should have studied at least three of the following subjects in Classes 11 and 12:

  • Physics
  • Chemistry
  • Mathematics
  • Biology

Age: No upper age limit.

Minimum Marks:

  • 60% aggregate marks for General category
  • 55% aggregate marks for SC/ST/PwD categories

(Eligibility requirements may change. Please check the official NEST website for the latest information.)

Mode: Computer Based Test (CBT)

Question Type: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

Exam Frequency: Conducted once a year (usually June)

Duration: 3 Hours

Subjects: Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics and Biology

Total Questions: 80

Total Marks: 240 Marks

Marking Scheme:

  • +3 - for every correct answer
  • -1 - for every wrong answer
  • 0 - for unattempted questions
  • Physics: 20 Questions (60 Marks)
  • Chemistry: 20 Questions (60 Marks)
  • Mathematics: 20 Questions (60 Marks)
  • Biology: 20 Questions (60 Marks)

Total: 80 Questions | 240 Marks

NEST scores are used for admission to Integrated M.Sc programmes offered by participating institutions.

Courses may include:
  • Integrated M.Sc in Biological Sciences
  • Integrated M.Sc in Chemical Sciences
  • Integrated M.Sc in Mathematical Sciences
  • Integrated M.Sc in Physical Sciences
