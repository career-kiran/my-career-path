State Teacher Eligibility Test (State TET)

State Teacher Eligibility Test (State TET) is a state-level examination for candidates who want to become teachers in State Government schools.

Each state in India conducts its own Teacher Eligibility Test (TET).

The eligibility criteria, exam pattern, and syllabus may be different for each state.

Candidates who qualify the State TET become eligible to apply for teaching positions in State Government schools and other schools that accept the respective State TET certificate.

Conducted by: The respective State Government or the State Education Department.

📌 Quick Facts

Eligibility: Class 12 or Graduation (varies by state). See the Eligibility section below for details.

Exam Pattern: Offline or Online examination consisting of objective-type Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs).

Mode: Offline (Pen-and-Paper Test) or Online (Computer-Based Test), depending on the state.

Total Papers: Usually 2 Papers (Paper I and Paper II).

Total Questions: Varies by state.

Total Marks: Varies by state.

Duration: Usually 2½ to 3 hours (varies by state).

Frequency: Varies by state.

Official Websites: State-wise official website links are provided below.

Qualification:

  • Paper I (Classes I–V)
    • Usually requires Senior Secondary (Class 12).
    • A recognized teacher training qualification is also required.
  • Paper II (Classes VI–VIII)
    • Usually requires a Bachelor's degree.
    • A recognized teacher training qualification is also required.
  • Both Papers
    • Candidates who meet the eligibility criteria for both papers can appear for both Paper I and Paper II.
  • Final Year Students
    • Some states allow candidates studying in the final year of the required teacher training course to apply.

Age Limit:

  • The age limit varies by state.
  • Candidates should check the official notification of their respective state.

Minimum Marks:

  • The minimum marks required vary by state.
  • Reserved categories may receive relaxation as per the rules of the respective state.

Attempts:

  • The number of attempts varies by state.
  • Candidates should check the official notification of their respective state.

Mode: Offline (Pen-and-Paper Test) or Online (Computer-Based Test), depending on the state.

Frequency: Varies by state.

Papers: The examination usually consists of two papers.

  • Paper I: For candidates who wish to teach Classes I to V.
  • Paper II: For candidates who wish to teach Classes VI to VIII.
  • Both Papers: Candidates eligible for both levels can appear for both papers.

Question Type: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs).

Marking Scheme:

  • The number of questions and total marks vary by state.
  • Some states have negative marking, while others do not.
  • Candidates should check the official notification of their respective state for the latest exam pattern.

Important:

The exam pattern may differ slightly from one state to another.

Candidates should always refer to their respective State TET official notification for the latest details.

Candidates who qualify the State Teacher Eligibility Test (State TET) become eligible to apply for teaching positions in State Government schools and other schools that accept the respective State TET certificate.

Teaching opportunities may include:

  • Primary Teacher (PRT)
    • Teach Classes I to V.
  • Upper Primary Teacher (TGT)
    • Teach Classes VI to VIII.

Schools that may accept State TET scores:

  • State Government Schools.
  • Government-Aided Schools.
  • Private Schools that accept the respective State TET certificate.

Note:

Qualifying the State TET makes a candidate eligible to apply for teaching positions.

It does not guarantee a job. Candidates must also satisfy the recruitment rules of the respective state or school.
