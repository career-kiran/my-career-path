Physiotherapy Assistant

A Physiotherapy Assistant works under a physiotherapist to help patients recover movement and strength. They support therapy sessions and help patients follow exercises correctly.

What does a Physiotherapy Assistant usually do?

  • Assist the physiotherapist during therapy sessions
  • Help patients perform exercises safely
  • Prepare therapy equipment and treatment areas
  • Guide patients during basic movements and stretches
  • Maintain cleanliness of therapy rooms and equipment

You may enjoy this career if you:

  • Like helping people recover from injuries or illness
  • Are comfortable working closely with patients
  • Enjoy practical, hands-on work
  • Are okay with following instructions from a physiotherapist
  • Have interest in physical activity and rehabilitation
Real-life example:
In a physiotherapy clinic, the assistant helps patients do simple exercises shown by the physiotherapist and prepares the treatment area for the next patient.

Courses Available

Certificate Course in Physiotherapy Assistant

  • Duration: 6 months – 1 year
  • Eligibility: 10th or 12th pass
  • Entrance exam: Not required
  • Admission: Direct or merit-based

Diploma in Physiotherapy Assistant

  • Duration: 1 – 2 years
  • Eligibility: 12th pass (science preferred in some institutes)
  • Entrance exam: Usually not required
  • Admission: Merit-based or direct admission

Where do Physiotherapy Assistants work?

  • Physiotherapy clinics
  • Hospitals
  • Rehabilitation centers
  • Sports injury and fitness centers
  • Old-age homes and care centers
Important note:
Physiotherapy Assistants work under the supervision of a physiotherapist and do not independently plan or prescribe treatment.
