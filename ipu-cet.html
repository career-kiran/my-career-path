IPU CET (Indraprastha University Common Entrance Test)

IPU CET is an entrance examination conducted by Guru Gobind Singh Indraprastha University (GGSIPU), Delhi for admission to various undergraduate, postgraduate, and professional courses.

The examination is used for admission to selected courses offered by GGSIPU and its affiliated colleges.

For some programmes, the university also accepts national-level entrance examinations such as JEE Main, NEET, CLAT, NIMCET, and CUET.

Conducted by: Guru Gobind Singh Indraprastha University (GGSIPU), Delhi.

📌 Quick Facts

Eligibility:

UG courses - Class 12 (10+2)pass (often with 50% aggregate marks and English as a compulsory subject)

PG courses - Bachelor's degree

(Requirements vary by course.)

Exam Pattern: Generally consists of 100 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs).(vary by course)

Mode: Mostly offline, pen-and-paper-based test using OMR sheets. Some courses may use a Computer-Based Test (CBT) format.

Total Questions: Most papers consist of 100 questions (may vary by course).

Total Marks: Most papers are conducted for 400 marks (may vary by course).

Frequency: Conducted annually. (usually in April or May)

Important: Admission requirements vary by course. Some programmes use national-level entrance exams instead of IPU CET.

Qualification:

  • (Eligibility varies depending on the course.)
  • Undergraduate courses - candidates must have passed Class 12 (10+2) from a recognized board.
  • Postgraduate courses - candidates must hold a Bachelor's degree from a recognized university.
  • Many courses have specific subject requirements (e.g., Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics for B.Tech; English is a compulsory subject for many programmes).

Age:

  • Undergraduate programmes - upper age limit, typically 21 years.
  • Postgraduate programmes - upper age limit of 35 years.
  • Some professional or specialized courses may have specific age requirements or relaxations for reserved categories.

Minimum Marks:

  • Undergraduate coursess - a minimum of 50% aggregate marks in Class 12.
  • Some technical programmes (such as B.Tech) - require 55% aggregate marks in specific subjects (e.g., PCM).
  • Postgraduate courses require at least 50% aggregate marks in the qualifying degree.
  • Reserved categories (e.g., SC/ST/OBC) - may be eligible for marks relaxation as per government and university norms.

Attempts:

  • There is generally no restriction on the number of attempts.
  • Candidates can apply in future admission cycles if they continue to meet the eligibility requirements.

Mode: Primarily Offline (OMR-Based Test). Some courses may use a Computer-Based Test (CBT) format.

Frequency: Conducted once a year.

Duration: 2 Hours 30 Minutes (150 Minutes).

Total Questions: 100 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) (may vary by course).

Total Marks: 400 Marks (may vary by course).

Question Type: Objective-Type Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs).

Question Paper Structure:

  • English Language: Grammar, vocabulary, comprehension, and language skills.
  • Logical & Analytical Reasoning: Reasoning and problem-solving questions.
  • Quantitative Aptitude: Numerical ability and basic mathematics.
  • General Awareness: Current affairs and general knowledge.
  • Course-Specific Subjects: Subjects vary depending on the programme applied for.

Marking Scheme:

  • Correct Answer: +4 Marks
  • Incorrect Answer: -1 Mark
  • Unattempted Question: 0 Marks

Important:

The exact exam pattern, subjects, and admission process may vary depending on the course.

Few courses admissions are based on national-level entrance examinations such as JEE Main, NEET, CUET, CLAT, or NIMCET instead of IPU CET.

Based on the current admission process, IPU CET is primarily used for admission to certain undergraduate, postgraduate, and lateral entry courses offered by GGSIPU.

Courses Commonly Admitting Through IPU CET:

  • B.Tech (Biotechnology)
  • B.Tech Lateral Entry (for Diploma Holders)
  • BHMCT (Bachelor of Hotel Management & Catering Technology)
  • B.Sc. (Medical Radiology & Imaging Technology)
  • Bachelor of Medical Record Information Technology (BMRIT)
  • M.Sc. (Packaging Technology)

Important:

  • Many popular GGSIPU courses now use national-level entrance examinations such as JEE Main, NEET, CUET, CLAT, and NIMCET instead of IPU CET.
  • The list of courses using IPU CET may change from year to year based on university admission policies.

Always check the official GGSIPU admission brochure to confirm whether your chosen course currently uses IPU CET or a national-level entrance examination.
