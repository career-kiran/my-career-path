Political Scientist

Who is a Political Scientist?

A political scientist is a person who studies how governments work, how laws are made, and how decisions affect people and society.

What does a Political Scientist do?

  • Studies government policies and public issues
  • Analyzes political systems and elections
  • Researches social and national problems
  • Suggests ideas to improve policies and governance

You may enjoy this career if:

  • You are interested in current affairs and politics
  • You like discussing social and national issues
  • You enjoy reading, writing, and analyzing ideas

Real Life Examples

  • A political scientist studies election results to understand why people voted in a certain way.
  • They may help the government design better policies for education or employment.

Courses & Eligibility

BA in Political Science (3 Years)

Eligibility: 10+2 in any stream with minimum 50% marks (varies by college)

Entrance Exams: CUET-UG (Common University Entrance Test), State university exams, or Merit-based admission

MA in Political Science (2 Years)

Eligibility: Bachelor’s degree in Political Science or related field with minimum 50–55% marks

Entrance Exams: CUET-PG, University-specific entrance exams, or Merit-based admission

PhD in Political Science (3–5 Years)

Eligibility: Master’s degree in Political Science with minimum 55% marks

Entrance Exams: UGC-NET, University-level PhD entrance exams

Where do Political Scientists work?

  • Policy Analyst – In government departments and think tanks
  • Research Officer – In research institutes and universities
  • Civil Servant – Through UPSC Civil Services Examination (IAS, IPS, IFS)
  • Political Consultant – With political parties and campaign teams
  • Professor or Lecturer – In colleges and universities

🌍 Global Opportunities

  • Policy Analyst – In international organizations like United Nations (UN), World Bank, and International Monetary Fund (IMF)
  • Research Fellow – In global think tanks and universities abroad
  • Political Risk Analyst – In multinational companies and consulting firms
  • Diplomatic Services – Through Foreign Service examinations

Important Note

If you aim to join Civil Services like IAS, IPS, or IFS, you must clear the UPSC Civil Services Examination after graduation.
