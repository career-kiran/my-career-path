🌾 Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR)

🌾 What is ICAR?

The Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR) is India’s top agricultural research organization.

It works under the Ministry of Agriculture and focuses on improving farming, crops, animal husbandry, fisheries, and food production.

🛡️ What Do They Do?

  • Develop better crop varieties and farming techniques.
  • Conduct research in agriculture, animal science, and fisheries.
  • Help improve food production and farmer income.

💖 You May Enjoy This Path If You

  • Are interested in agriculture and food production.
  • Like research related to crops, soil, or animals.
  • Want to improve farming and rural development.

📍 Real Life Examples

  • A scientist develops a crop that grows well even in dry conditions.
  • A researcher finds ways to protect crops from pests,insects,etc.

🎯 How to get a job in ICAR

🎓 Jobs After 10th (Technical Support Roles)

Technician (T-1)

  • Eligibility: Matriculation (10th pass) from a recognised board.
  • Minimum Marks: No fixed percentage; final merit is based on exam performance.
  • Age Limit: 18 to 27 years (may vary as per notification).
  • Selection: Computer Based Test (CBT) followed by a qualifying Trade/Skill Test (if required).

🎓 Jobs After 12th (ICAR)

Lower Division Clerk (LDC) / Junior Secretariat Assistant (JSA)

  • Type: Permanent Central Government Post.
  • Eligibility: 10+2 / Class 12th pass from a recognised board.
  • Minimum Marks: No fixed percentage (selection based on exam performance).
  • Age Limit: Usually 18–27 years (as per notification).
  • Selection: Computer Based Test (CBT) + Typing Test (qualifying).

Field Assistant (Project Based)

  • Type: Temporary / Contract Post.
  • Eligibility: 12th pass (Science preferred).
  • Minimum Marks: Usually 50% (varies by project).
  • Age Limit: As per project notification.
  • Selection: Shortlisting + Interview.

Laboratory Assistant (Project Based)

  • Type: Temporary / Contract Post.
  • Eligibility: 12th with Science (PCM/PCB).
  • Minimum Marks: Usually 50–60% (varies by project).
  • Age Limit: As per notification.
  • Selection: Interview / Skill Assessment.

Data Entry Operator (Project Based)

  • Type: Temporary / Contract Post.
  • Eligibility: 12th pass with basic computer knowledge.
  • Minimum Marks: As per notification.
  • Age Limit: As per project requirement.
  • Selection: Written Test / Skill Test.

NOTE:

    Project-based posts depend on ongoing research projects.

    Vacancies, eligibility and duration may vary each year. Always check the official ICAR website for the latest notification.

🛠 Roles & Responsibilities

Technician (T-1)

  • Maintain and operate laboratory or field equipment(Testing done at the site).
  • Assist scientists during experiments.
  • Handle tools, instruments, and technical systems.

Lower Division Clerk (LDC) / Junior Secretariat Assistant (JSA)

  • Handle office files, documents, and official records.
  • Prepare letters, reports, and administrative documents.
  • Assist in daily office and administrative work.

Field Assistant (Project Based)

  • Collect samples and data from farms or research fields.
  • Support scientists during outdoor research work.
  • Record important details and observations during field work.

Laboratory Assistant (Project Based)

  • Help in conducting laboratory experiments.
  • Prepare chemicals and taking care of lab equipments and materials.
  • Record experiment results.

Data Entry Operator (Project Based)

  • Enter research data into computer systems.
  • Maintain digital records and reports.
  • Support in keeping project records properly.

📘 Exam Details

For Technician (T-1)

  • CBT – Computer Based Test: An online exam with multiple choice questions(MCQ)covering subjects like General Knowledge, Science, Mathematics, and reasoning.
  • Trade / Skill Test: A practical test to check technical or hands-on skills (qualifying in nature).

For LDC / JSA

  • CBT – Computer Based Test: Online exam with Multiple Choice Questions(MCQ)) questions in General Intelligence, Reasoning, English, General Awareness, and Mathematics.
  • Typing Test: A qualifying test to check typing speed in English or Hindi as per required standards.

For Project-Based Posts

  • Written Test (if conducted): Basic subject-related or aptitude-based exam.
  • Interview: Personal interaction to assess knowledge and suitability.
  • Skill Assessment: Practical test depending on the role (lab work, data handling, etc.).

📌 Selection Process

For Technician (T-1)

Step 1 Apply through the official ICAR recruitment portal when notification is released.
Step 2 Appear for the Computer Based Test (CBT – MCQ format).
Step 3 Candidates are shortlisted based on CBT marks.
Step 4 Attend Trade / Skill Test (if applicable).
Step 5 Final merit list prepared based on overall exam performance.

For LDC / JSA

Step 1 Apply online through the official ICAR website.
Step 2 Appear for the Computer Based Test (CBT – MCQ format).
Step 3 Candidates qualifying the CBT are called for the Typing Test.
Step 4 Final selection based on CBT marks (Typing test is qualifying in nature).

For Project-Based Posts

Step 1 Check project vacancy notifications on the ICAR institute website.
Step 2 Submit application online or offline as mentioned in the notification.
Step 3 Shortlisted candidates are called for Written Test or Interview.
Step 4 Final selection based on interview or skill performance.

🎓 Jobs After Graduation (ICAR)

Technical Assistant (T-3) – Group B

  • Eligibility: Bachelor’s Degree in Agriculture / relevant Science subject (Horticulture, Fisheries, Veterinary Science, etc.) OR 3-year Diploma in Engineering.
  • Minimum Marks: Generally 60% aggregate marks (First Class).
  • Age Limit: 18 to 30 years (relaxation as per government rules).
  • Selection: Computer Based Test (CBT) followed by Document Verification. No interview for most technical posts.

Assistant (Administrative) – Group B

  • Eligibility: Bachelor’s Degree in any discipline from a recognised university.
  • Minimum Marks: No fixed percentage specified; selection is competitive.
  • Age Limit: 20 to 30 years (relaxation as per rules).
  • Selection: Preliminary Exam + Mains Exam + Computer Proficiency Test (CPT).

Agricultural Research Service (ARS) – Scientist (Group A)

  • Eligibility: Master’s Degree in relevant agricultural or allied science subject.
  • Minimum Marks: As specified in ARS notification.
  • Age Limit: Usually up to 32 years (relaxation applicable).
  • Selection: ARS National Examination (NET-based) followed by Interview.

Young Professional (Project Based)

  • Type: Temporary / Contract Position (Not Permanent).
  • Eligibility: Graduate or Postgraduate in relevant agricultural / science subject.
  • Minimum Marks: As per project requirement.
  • Age Limit: Usually up to 35 years (may vary).
  • Selection: Shortlisting based on qualification + Interview.

NOTE:

    Eligibility, age limit, and selection process may vary depending on the official recruitment notification.

    Always check the official ICAR website for the latest updates.

🛠 Roles & Responsibilities

Technical Assistant (T-3)

  • Support scientists in laboratory and field experiments.
  • Maintain technical equipment and research instruments.
  • Collect, record, and manage research data.

Assistant (Administrative)

  • Handle office files, reports, and official communication.
  • Manage records, accounts, and administrative tasks.
  • Support day-to-day functioning of ICAR institutes.

ARS Scientist (Group A)

  • Conduct advanced research in agriculture and allied sciences.
  • Develop new farming technologies, crop varieties, or research methods.
  • Publish research findings and guide research projects.

Young Professional (Project Based)

  • Assist in specific research projects under senior scientists.
  • Collect field data and prepare project reports.
  • Support short-term research activities and documentation.

📘 Exam Details

Technical Assistant (T-3)

  • CBT – Computer Based Test: Online exam with Multiple Choice Questions(MCQ) covering General Knowledge, Science, Mathematics, and subject-related topics.
  • Document Verification: Verification of educational and eligibility documents after qualifying the exam.

Assistant (Administrative – Group B)

  • Preliminary Exam: Online exam with Multiple Choice Questions(MCQ) covering Reasoning, English, General Awareness, and Quantitative Aptitude.
  • Mains Exam: Advanced level written examination.
  • CPT – Computer Proficiency Test: Test to assess basic computer skills (qualifying in nature).

ARS Scientist (Group A)

  • ARS – Agricultural Research Service Examination: National-level exam conducted for recruitment of scientists in ICAR.
  • NET – National Eligibility Test: Qualifying exam to determine eligibility for ARS and agricultural research posts.
  • Interview: Personal interview conducted after qualifying the written examination.

Young Professional (Project Based)

  • Shortlisting: Candidates are shortlisted based on qualification and experience.
  • Interview: Personal interview conducted by the project selection committee.

📌 Selection Process

Technical Assistant (T-3)

Step 1 Apply online through the official ICAR recruitment portal when notification is released.
Step 2 Appear for the Computer Based Test (CBT – MCQ format).
Step 3 Candidates are shortlisted based on CBT marks.
Step 4 Document verification.
Step 5 Final merit list prepared based on exam performance.

Assistant (Administrative – Group B)

Step 1 Apply online through the ICAR recruitment portal.
Step 2 Appear for the Preliminary Examination.
Step 3 Qualified candidates attend the Mains Examination.
Step 4 Appear for the Computer Proficiency Test (CPT).
Step 5 Final selection based on overall performance and document verification.

ARS Scientist (Group A)

Step 1 Apply for the ARS Examination through the official notification.
Step 2 Appear for the ARS Written Examination.
Step 3 Candidates qualifying the written exam are called for the Interview.
Step 4 Final selection based on written exam and interview performance.

Young Professional (Project Based)

Step 1 Check project vacancy notifications on the ICAR institute website.
Step 2 Submit application as mentioned in the notification (online/email/offline).
Step 3 Shortlisted candidates are called for the Interview.
Step 4 Final selection based on interview performance.

⚠ Important

  • Technical Assistant and Assistant posts are permanent central government positions under ICAR institutes.
  • ARS Scientist recruitment requires a Master’s degree and is conducted through a national-level examination.
  • Young Professional positions are temporary and project-based, not permanent government jobs.
  • Eligibility, age limit, and number of vacancies may vary as per official recruitment notification.
  • Age relaxation is provided as per Government of India rules for reserved categories.
