Environmental Scientist

An environmental scientist is a person who studies the environment and works to protect nature, air, water, soil, and living things. They try to find solutions to environmental problems like pollution and climate change.

What does an Environmental Scientist do?

  • Studies air, water, and soil to check pollution.
  • Finds ways to protect nature and reduce environmental damage.
  • Tests samples and prepares reports.
  • Makes sure companies and projects follow environmental rules.

You may enjoy this career if you:

  • Care about nature and the environment.
  • Like studying science subjects like biology and chemistry.
  • Want to make a positive impact on the planet.

Real Life Example

An environmental scientist may test water from a river to check if it is polluted. If pollution levels are high, they suggest ways to reduce it and protect people and animals who depend on that water.

Courses to Become an Environmental Scientist

🎓 Courses & Eligibility

B.Sc Environmental Science (3 years)

Eligibility:

  • 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, Biology / Mathematics
  • Minimum Marks: Usually 50–60% (varies by college)

Admission:

  • Merit-based in many colleges
  • CUET (Common University Entrance Test) for central universities
  • State-level university entrance exams (varies by state)

M.Sc Environmental Science (2 years)

Eligibility:

  • Bachelor’s degree in Environmental Science / Life Sciences / Chemistry / Geography or related subjects
  • Minimum Marks: Usually 50–60%

Admission:

  • University entrance exams
  • CUET-PG (for some universities)
  • State-level entrance exams (varies by state)

Ph.D in Environmental Science (3–5 years)

Eligibility:

  • Master’s degree in relevant subject
  • Minimum required marks (varies by university)

Entrance Exams:

  • UGC-NET
  • CSIR-NET
  • University-specific Ph.D entrance exams

Where do Environmental Scientists work?

  • Environmental Officers – In Pollution Control Boards (CPCB – Central Pollution Control Board, SPCB – State Pollution Control Boards).
  • Environmental Analysts – In Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change (MoEFCC).
  • Research Scientists – In CSIR (Council of Scientific and Industrial Research) and NEERI (National Environmental Engineering Research Institute).
  • Sustainability Managers – In private companies, making sure their work does not harm the environment.
  • Environmental Consultants – In consulting firms, advising companies on eco-friendly practices.
  • Project Officers – In NGOs like WWF India (World Wide Fund for Nature).

Important Note

Environmental science is a growing field in India and worldwide. With increasing focus on climate change and pollution control, career opportunities are expanding in both government and private sectors.
