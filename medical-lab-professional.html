Medical Lab Professional

A medical lab professional works behind the scenes in healthcare. They test blood, urine, and other body samples to help doctors find diseases and decide the right treatment.

What does a medical lab professional usually do?

  • Tests blood, urine, and tissue samples
  • Helps detect infections, diabetes, anemia, and other diseases
  • Operates laboratory machines and equipment
  • Prepares test reports carefully for doctors
  • Supports doctors by providing lab test information
Real-life example
A person goes to a hospital with fever. A medical lab professional tests the blood sample to check for infection. The report helps the doctor decide the treatment.

You may enjoy this career if you:

  • Like working in a laboratory environment
  • Are comfortable handling samples and machines
  • Enjoy careful and accurate work
  • Prefer less direct interaction with patients
  • Are interested in science and healthcare

Courses to become a Medical Lab Professional (India)

BSc Medical Laboratory Technology (BSc MLT / BMLT)

Duration: 3 years (some colleges include internship)

Eligibility:

  • 12th standard with Physics, Chemistry, Biology
  • Minimum marks: usually 50%

Entrance exams (examples):

  • CUET (for some universities)
  • State health university entrance exams

Entrance exams and admission process vary from state to state and university to university.

Diploma in Medical Laboratory Technology (DMLT)

Duration: 2 years (sometimes + internship)

Eligibility:

  • 10+2 with Science (Biology preferred)
  • Minimum marks vary by institute

Admission: Usually merit-based

Diploma holders usually work under the supervision of senior lab professionals or doctors.

Where do medical lab professionals work?

  • Hospitals and diagnostic laboratories
  • Private pathology labs
  • Medical colleges
  • Blood banks
  • Research laboratories
