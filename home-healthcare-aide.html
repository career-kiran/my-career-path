Home Health Care Aide

A Home Health Care Aide helps patients at their homes with daily activities, basic care, and emotional support, especially elderly or chronically ill patients.

What does a Home Health Care Aide usually do?

  • Assist patients with bathing, dressing, and personal hygiene
  • Help with basic mobility like walking or getting out of bed
  • Support patients in taking medicines as advised
  • Help with simple household tasks related to patient care
  • Observe patient condition and inform family or healthcare staff

You may enjoy this career if you:

  • Like caring for elderly or sick people
  • Are patient, kind, and supportive
  • Are comfortable working in people’s homes
  • Prefer practical, hands-on caregiving work
  • Want a healthcare role without long academic courses
Real-life example:
An elderly person living at home needs help with daily activities. A home health care aide visits daily to assist with bathing, meals, basic exercises, and ensures the person is safe and comfortable.

Courses Available

Certificate Course in Home Health Care / Caregiving

  • Duration: 3 – 6 months
  • Eligibility: 10th pass
  • Entrance exam: Not required
  • Admission: Direct admission

Diploma in Home Health Care / Geriatric Care

  • Duration: 1 year
  • Eligibility: 10th or 12th pass
  • Entrance exam: Usually not required
  • Admission: Direct or merit-based

Where do Home Health Care Aides work?

  • Patient homes
  • Home healthcare agencies
  • Old-age homes
  • Palliative care services
  • Rehabilitation and long-term care centers
Important note:
Home Health Care Aides provide basic care and support. They work under the guidance of nurses, doctors, or family members and do not perform medical procedures.
