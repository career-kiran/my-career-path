Sociologist

Who is a Sociologist?

A sociologist is a person who studies how people live, interact, and behave in society. They try to understand social problems, relationships, culture, and changes happening in communities.

What does a Sociologist do?

  • Studies how families, schools, workplaces, and communities function
  • Researches social issues like poverty, unemployment, education, and gender equality
  • Collects data through surveys and interviews
  • Suggests solutions to improve social systems

You may enjoy this career if:

  • You are curious about how society works
  • You like observing people and social patterns
  • You are interested in social change and development

Real Life Examples

  • A sociologist studies why certain communities face more problems and helps plan better solutions for them.
  • A sociologist studies how social media affects teenagers’ behaviour and relationships.

Courses & Eligibility

Bachelor’s Degree (3 years)

Courses:

  • BA Sociology
  • BA Social Sciences (with Sociology)

Eligibility: 10+2 in any stream

Minimum Marks: 45–50% (varies by university)

Entrance Exams: CUET (Common University Entrance Test), university-level entrance exams

Master’s Degree (2 years)

Courses:

  • MA Sociology
  • MA Social Work (related field)

Eligibility: Bachelor’s degree in Sociology or related subject

Minimum Marks: 50–55%

Entrance Exams: CUET-PG, university-level entrance exams

PhD (3–5 years)

Eligibility: Master’s degree in Sociology or related field

Entrance Exams: UGC-NET / JRF (National Eligibility Test / Junior Research Fellowship)

Where do Sociologists work?

  • Research Officer – In organizations like Indian Council of Social Science Research (ICSSR)
  • Policy Analyst – In government departments and public policy institutions
  • Program Coordinator – In NGOs like Pratham or CRY (Child Rights and You)
  • Social Researcher – In market research and survey organizations
  • Lecturer / Professor – In colleges and universities

🌍 Global Opportunities

  • Social Policy Researcher – In international organizations like United Nations (UN)
  • Community Development Specialist – In global NGOs
  • Social Data Analyst – In research institutes abroad

Important Note

Sociology focuses on understanding society and improving social systems. It is a strong career option for students interested in research, policy-making, development work, and academics.
