Biologist

Who is a Biologist?

A biologist is a person who studies living things such as plants, animals, and humans. They try to understand how living organisms grow, function, and interact with their surroundings.

You may enjoy this career if:

  • You are curious about plants, animals, or the human body
  • You enjoy learning about nature and ecosystems
  • You like observing living things closely
  • You are interested in how diseases affect the body
  • You enjoy practical lab work or field work

Real Life Examples

  • A botanist studies why some plants grow better in certain places and helps improve crop production.
  • A zoologist observes animals in forests and helps protect wildlife.
  • A microbiologist studies tiny organisms like bacteria and viruses to understand how diseases spread and how to prevent them.

Courses to Become a Biologist

1️⃣ Bachelor’s Degree – BSc (3 years)

You can choose:

  • BSc in Biology
  • BSc in Zoology
  • BSc in Botany
  • BSc in Microbiology
  • BSc in Biotechnology

Eligibility: 12th with Biology (PCB stream)

Minimum Marks: Usually 50–60% (varies by college)

Admission: Merit-based in many colleges or CUET / State-level entrance exams

BSc (ZBC – Zoology, Botany, Chemistry)

This is a combination course where you study Zoology, Botany, and Chemistry together.

Eligibility: 12th with Biology

Minimum Marks: Usually 50–60%

This option is useful if you want broader knowledge in life sciences.

2️⃣ Master’s Degree – MSc (2 years)

After BSc, you can specialize in:

  • MSc in Zoology
  • MSc in Botany
  • MSc in Microbiology
  • MSc in Biotechnology
  • MSc in Life Sciences

Eligibility: BSc in relevant biological subject

Minimum Marks: Usually 50–60%

Entrance Exams: CUET-PG, IIT JAM (for some institutes), University-level exams

3️⃣ PhD in Biological Sciences (3–5 years)

A PhD is required if you want to become a research scientist or professor.

Eligibility: MSc in relevant biological field

Entrance Exams: CSIR-NET, UGC-NET, GATE (for some institutes), Institute-level exams

Where do Biologists Work?

  • Pharmaceutical Companies – Research, development and quality control in companies like Sun Pharma and Cipla.
  • Food Industry – Food scientists working in companies like Nestle India and ITC Limited.
  • Hospitals & Diagnostic Labs – Clinical research and laboratory testing in hospitals like Apollo and labs like Dr. Lal PathLabs.
  • Research Organizations – Working as research assistants or scientists in organizations like CSIR (Council of Scientific and Industrial Research) and ICMR (Indian Council of Medical Research).
  • Government Agencies – Roles in FSSAI (Food Safety and Standards Authority of India) and CFSL (Central Forensic Science Laboratories) for inspection and research analysis.
  • Environment & Wildlife Organizations – Working in Wildlife Trust of India (WTI), Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change, and Pollution Control Boards.
  • Education Field – Becoming lecturers and professors in colleges and universities.
Important Note: To work as a research scientist in national laboratories or advanced research roles, higher studies such as MSc and often a PhD are usually required.
