ISI Admission Test

ISI Admission Test is an entrance examination conducted by the Indian Statistical Institute (ISI) for admission to its undergraduate, postgraduate, and research programmes.

Students interested in statistics, mathematics, data science, quantitative economics, computer science, and related fields can apply for various programmes offered by ISI.

Conducted by: Indian Statistical Institute (ISI).

📌 Quick Facts

Who Can Apply? Candidates who have passed 10+2 (for UG) or hold a relevant Bachelor's degree in Math/Stats/Engineering (for PG).

Mode: Offline (Pen and Paper-based / OMR & Descriptive)

Questions: 40

Duration: 4 Hours

Frequency: Conducted once a year (Usually in May)

🔹 Undergraduate (B.Stat / B.Math)

Qualification: Passed Class 12 (or equivalent) with Mathematics as a subject.

Age: Mentioned in the official admission notification.

Minimum Marks: Eligibility criteria prescribed by ISI.

Attempts: No specific attempt limit.

🔹 Undergraduate (B.SDS)

Qualification: Passed Class 12 with Mathematics and English.

Age: Age requirements mentioned in the admission notification.

Minimum Marks: Minimum marks criteria prescribed by ISI.

Attempts: As per the latest admission guidelines.

🔹 Postgraduate Programmes (M.Stat / M.Math / MSQE)

Qualification: Bachelor's degree in a relevant discipline with mathematical background.

Age: No specific age limit for most postgraduate programmes.

Minimum Marks: Candidates must satisfy the academic requirements prescribed for the chosen programme.

Attempts: No restriction on the number of attempts.

Mode: Offline (Pen and Paper-based Test)

Question Type: Multiple Choice Questions (UGA) & Subjective/Descriptive Problems (UGB)

Duration: 4 Hours Total (2 Hours for MCQ Session + 2 Hours for Descriptive Session)

Total Questions: Approximately 38 Questions (30 MCQs + 8 Subjective Problems)

Total Marks: 200 Marks (120 Marks for MCQ Section + 80 Marks for Subjective Section)

Negative Marking:

  • MCQ Section: No negative marking (Correct: +4 | Wrong: 0 | Left Blank: +1 Mark)
  • Subjective Section: No negative marking

Frequency: Conducted once a year (Usually in May)

Undergraduate Programs (UG)

These flagship courses are for students who have passed their Class 12 examinations with Mathematics and English.

  • B.Stat (Hons) – Bachelor of Statistics (Kolkata Campus)
  • B.Math (Hons) – Bachelor of Mathematics (Bengaluru Campus)
  • B.SDS (Hons) – Bachelor of Statistical Data Science (Kolkata, Delhi, and Bengaluru Campuses)

Postgraduate Programs (PG)

These advanced programmes are open to graduates holding a relevant Bachelor's degree.

Master's Degree Programs

  • M.Stat – Master of Statistics
  • M.Math – Master of Mathematics
  • MS (QE) – Master of Science in Quantitative Economics
  • MS (QMBA) – Master of Science in Quality Management and Business Analytics
  • MS (LIS) – Master of Science in Library and Information Science

M.Tech Programs

  • M.Tech (CS) – Master of Technology in Computer Science
  • M.Tech (CrS) – Master of Technology in Cryptology and Security
  • M.Tech (QROR) – Master of Technology in Quality, Reliability and Operations Research

Postgraduate Diploma Programs

  • PGDM (BA) – Post Graduate Diploma in Business Analytics
  • PGD (SMA) – Post Graduate Diploma in Statistical Methods and Analytics
  • PGD (ARSMA) – Post Graduate Diploma in Agricultural and Rural Management with Statistical Methods and Analytics

Research & Ph.D. Programs

These programmes are for students who have completed a Master's degree and wish to pursue advanced research leading to a Ph.D.

Core Statistical & Mathematical Sciences

  • JRF in Statistics
  • JRF in Mathematics
  • JRF in Quantitative Economics

Engineering & Information Sciences

  • JRF in Computer Science
  • JRF in Quality, Reliability & Operations Research (QROR)
  • JRF in Library & Information Science

Interdisciplinary Natural & Social Sciences

  • Physics and Applied Mathematics
  • Geology
  • Biological Science
  • Sociology & Linguistics
