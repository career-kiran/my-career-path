State-Level MBA Entrance Exams

State-Level MBA Entrance Exams are entrance examinations conducted by individual state governments, state authorities, or universities for admission to MBA and other postgraduate management programmes within their respective states.

These exams are mainly used for admission to government, government-aided, and private management colleges in that state.

Examples: MAH MBA CET (Maharashtra), TANCET MBA (Tamil Nadu), AP ICET (Andhra Pradesh), TS ICET (Telangana), KMAT Kerala, Karnataka PGCET MBA, and other state-level MBA entrance examinations.

Conducted by: Individual state governments, state entrance examination authorities, or universities.

Note:

Every state does not conduct its own MBA entrance examination.

Some states admit students through national-level examinations such as CAT, CMAT, MAT, XAT, or other accepted exams, while others conduct their own state-level entrance examinations.

Always check the official website of the state or university where you wish to apply.
