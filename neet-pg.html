NEET PG (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test for PostGraduate)

NEET PG is a national-level postgraduate medical entrance examination for students who have completed the MBBS course and wish to join postgraduate medical courses in India.

Conducted by: National Board of Examinations in Medical Sciences (NBEMS)

📌 Quick Facts

Eligibility: MBBS Degree

Mode: Computer Based Test (CBT)

Duration: 3 Hours 30 Minutes

Total Questions: 200

Frequency: Conducted once a year (usually in June)

Qualification:MBBS Degree from a recognized medical university.

Internship:Must have completed the compulsory internship as prescribed by NBEMS.

Registration: Must possess provisional or permanent medical registration.

Age: No upper age limit.

Attempts: No limit on the number of attempts.

Mode: Computer Based Test (CBT)

Duration: 3 Hours 30 Minutes

Total Questions: 200 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

Total Marks: 800 Marks

Marking Scheme:

  • +4 Marks for each correct answer
  • -1 Mark for each incorrect answer
  • 0 Marks for unanswered questions

Questions are asked from various subjects covered during the MBBS course, including both pre-clinical and clinical subjects.

After clearing NEET-PG, students can get admission to various postgraduate medical courses in India.

Courses include:-
  • MD (Doctor of Medicine)
  • MS (Master of Surgery)
  • PG Diploma Courses
  • DNB (Diplomate of National Board)

For registration, notifications and latest updates, visit the official NBEMS website.

For counselling, seat allotment and admission process, visit the MCC website.
