🌟 Discover Yourself

Complete these 5 simple activities before exploring careers.

The purpose of these activities is not to choose a career today.

The purpose is to help you discover your true interests, passions, and aspirations.

There are no right or wrong answers. Be honest with yourself.

🎨 Activity 1: What Makes You Happy?

Take a pen and paper.

Think about those activities that makes you really happy while doing them

Write down as many activities as you can think of.

Examples:

  • Drawing
  • Helping someone
  • Playing a musical instrument
  • Writing stories
  • Coding
  • Teaching others
  • Repairing gadgets
  • Playing sports
  • Designing things

💡 These are just examples. Your interests may be completely different, and that's perfectly okay.

❌ Don't include passive activities like watching TV, scrolling social media, or eating your favorite food.

❤️ Activity 2: What Would You Do in Life?

Take a pen and paper.

Ask this question to yourself:

"If money is not my goal, then what would I do?"

"What job or activity would I happily do even if nobody paid me for it?"

Write down all the ideas that come to your mind.

💡 There are no right or wrong answers. Be honest with yourself.

🔍 Activity 3: What Makes You Curious?

Take a pen and paper.

You might have watched many videos on YouTube, right?

Think about the topics, or subjetcs or any field that you enjoy watching, learning, or reading about again and again.

Write down all the topics that come to your mind.(eg. technology, biology, space..etc)

💡 There are no right or wrong answers. Be honest with yourself.

Think About It:
What topics do you keep coming back to because you genuinely want to know more about them?

🌍 Activity 4: What Would You Love to Do?

Take a pen and paper.

Ask yourself this question:

"If you knew you couldn't fail, what would you want to do?"

Write down all the ideas that come to your mind.

💡 Don't think about marks, money, difficulty, or what others may say. Just write what you genuinely feel.

Think About It:
If fear of failure did not exist, what would you choose to do?

✨ Activity 5: What Would Make Your Life Meaningful?

Take a pen and paper.

Imagine you are 70 years old.

You are saying to yourself:

"I spent my life doing something I truly loved."

"I didn't waste my life doing something I didn't enjoy."

Now think about the jobs, careers, or activities that would make you feel this way.

Write down all the ideas that come to your mind.

💡 There are no right or wrong answers. Be honest with yourself.

Think About It:
What kind of work would make you feel proud, happy, and satisfied when you look back at your life?

📝 Look for Patterns

Review your answers from all 5 activities.

Do you notice any common themes appearing again and again?

For example:

  • I like to help people, i love designing...like this

💡 Interests that are repeatedly appearing will give you an idea about which field you may like.

Note:
Notice any idea repeatedly coming in more than one activity

🌱 Final Suggestion

A career is one of the most important choices in life. Whatever profession you choose, you may spend a large part of your life doing it.

I strongly encourage you to spend at least 3 to 10 days reflecting on these questions before making a final career decision.

Don't choose a career just because it is popular, pays well, or because others expect you to.

Choose something that genuinely interests you, excites you, and gives you a sense of purpose.

Remember:
Understanding yourself is the first step towards choosing a career that you will truly enjoy.

💭 One Final Thought

  • One day, when you look back at your life, you should be able to say:

    "I am glad I chose this path."
