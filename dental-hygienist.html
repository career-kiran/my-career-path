Dental Hygienist

A Dental Hygienist is a healthcare professional who focuses on cleaning teeth, preventing dental problems, and educating patients about oral hygiene. They work under the supervision of a dentist.

What does a Dental Hygienist usually do?

  • Clean teeth by removing plaque and tartar
  • Help prevent gum diseases
  • Educate patients on proper brushing and oral care
  • Assist dentists during dental procedures
  • Maintain cleanliness and hygiene in the dental clinic

You may enjoy this career if you:

  • Like helping people maintain good oral health
  • Enjoy practical, hands-on work
  • Are comfortable working closely with patients
  • Have attention to detail and cleanliness
Real-life example
A patient visits a dental clinic for routine cleaning. The dental hygienist carefully cleans the teeth, removes plaque, explains proper brushing techniques, and helps the patient maintain healthy teeth and gums.

Courses to become a Dental Hygienist

Diploma in Dental Hygiene

Duration: 2 years

Eligibility: 10+2 with Biology

Admission: Merit-based / Entrance-based

BSc Dental Hygiene

Duration: 3 years

Eligibility: 10+2 with Biology

Admission: Merit-based / Entrance-based

Admission note:
Some colleges admit students based on merit, while others conduct university-level or state-level entrance exams. Admission rules may vary by institution and state.

Entrance Exam

  • Some universities may consider CUET (UG)
  • A few colleges may conduct their own entrance test or interview

Important note:

Dental Hygienists mainly focus on cleaning, prevention, and oral health education and work under the supervision of dentists. Admission rules, entrance exams, and course availability may vary between colleges. Always check the official college or university website for the most accurate details.
