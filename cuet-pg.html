CUET PG (Common University Entrance Test - Postgraduate)

CUET PG is a common admission test used by many universities in India for admission to postgraduate courses.

A student writes the CUET PG examination and receives a score.

The student can use this score to apply for various postgraduate courses in universities that accept CUET PG scores.

Courses may include: M.Sc., M.A., M.Com., MBA, MCA, M.Tech., LLM and many other postgraduate programmes.

Conducted by: National Testing Agency (NTA).

📌 Quick Facts

Eligibility: Bachelor's Degree

Mode: Computer Based Test (CBT)

Questions: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

Frequency: Conducted once a year (usually March)

Qualification: Bachelor's degree from a recognised university.

Age: No age limit.

Minimum Marks:

  • Minimum marks requirements may vary according to the course and university.

Attempts: No restriction on the number of attempts.

Note: Candidates must satisfy the eligibility requirements prescribed by the university and course they are applying for.

Mode: Computer Based Test (CBT)

Question Type:

  • MCQ (Multiple Choice Questions): Choose one correct answer from the given options.

Exam Frequency: Conducted once a year (usually March)

Duration: 90 Minutes

Total Questions: 75

Total Marks: 300 Marks

Marking Scheme:

  • +4 - for every correct answer
  • -1 - for every wrong answer
  • 0 - for unattempted questions

CUET PG scores may be used for admission to a wide range of postgraduate programmes offered by participating universities across India.

Courses may include:

Science & Technology Programmes

  • M.Sc. (Master of Science)
  • M.Tech. (Master of Technology)
  • MCA (Master of Computer Applications)
  • M.Arch. (Master of Architecture)
  • M.Pharm. (Master of Pharmacy)
  • MPT (Master of Physiotherapy)

Management, Commerce & Law Programmes

  • MBA (Master of Business Administration)
  • M.Com. (Master of Commerce)
  • LL.M. (Master of Laws)
  • MPH (Master of Public Health)

Arts, Humanities & Education Programmes

  • M.A. (Master of Arts)
  • MSW (Master of Social Work)
  • M.Ed. (Master of Education)
  • M.P.Ed. (Master of Physical Education)
  • MFA (Master of Fine Arts)
  • B.Ed. (Bachelor of Education)
  • B.P.Ed. (Bachelor of Physical Education)

Traditional & Sanskrit Studies

  • Acharya
  • Shiksha Acharya
  • Other Traditional Postgraduate Programmes

Note: The exact courses available through CUET PG may vary from one participating university to another.
