Operation Theatre (OT) Technician

An Operation Theatre Technician is a healthcare professional who assists doctors and nurses during surgeries by preparing the operation theatre, handling instruments, and maintaining a sterile environment.

What does an OT Technician usually do?

  • Prepare the operation theatre before surgery
  • Arrange and sterilize surgical instruments
  • Assist doctors and nurses during operations
  • Maintain cleanliness and infection control in the OT
  • Handle surgical equipment before and after procedures

You may enjoy this career if you:

  • Are comfortable working in hospital and surgical environments
  • Can stay calm and focused under pressure
  • Are careful, disciplined, and follow instructions well
  • Are okay with standing for long hours
  • Have interest in clinical and surgical work
Real-life example:
During a surgery, the OT technician prepares the operation theatre, arranges sterilized instruments, assists the surgical team, and ensures all equipment is ready and functioning properly.

Courses Available

Certificate in Operation Theatre Technology

A short-term course that focuses on basic operation theatre procedures, surgical instruments, sterilization, and assisting surgeons.

  • Duration: 6 months – 1 year
  • Eligibility: 10th or 12th

Diploma in Operation Theatre Technology

A more detailed program covering OT management, anesthesia support, emergency care, and advanced surgical assistance.

  • Duration: 2 years
  • Eligibility: 12th (Science preferred)

BSc Operation Theatre Technology

A degree-level course that provides in-depth knowledge of surgical procedures, anesthesia techniques, patient safety, and OT management.

  • Duration: 3 years
  • Eligibility: 12th with Physics, Chemistry, Biology

Where do Operation Theatre Technicians work?

  • Hospitals
  • Operation theatres
  • Multi-specialty hospitals
  • Trauma and emergency care centers
  • Surgical centers
