X-ray / Radiology Technician

An X-ray technician takes X-ray pictures so doctors can see what is happening inside the body.

What does an X-ray / Radiology Technician usually do?

  • Take X-ray images of bones, chest, and other body parts
  • Position patients correctly before taking images
  • Operate X-ray and imaging machines safely
  • Follow radiation safety rules
  • Provide clear images to doctors for diagnosis

You may enjoy this career if you:

  • Like working with medical machines
  • Are careful and pay attention to details
  • Are comfortable interacting with patients
  • Can follow safety rules strictly
  • Want a technical role in healthcare
Real-life example:
A patient comes to the hospital after a fall. The X-ray technician takes an X-ray of the injured arm so the doctor can check for fractures.

Courses Available

Diploma in X-ray / Radiology Technology

  • Duration: 1 – 2 years
  • Eligibility: 12th pass (Science preferred)
  • Entrance exam: Usually not required
  • Admission: Merit-based or direct

BSc in Medical Imaging / Radiology Technology

  • Duration: 3 years
  • Eligibility: 12th with Physics, Chemistry, Biology
  • Entrance exam: Varies by college (CUET / state-level exams)
  • Admission: Merit-based or entrance-based

Where do X-ray / Radiology Technicians work?

  • Hospitals
  • Diagnostic imaging centers
  • Trauma and emergency departments
  • Multi-specialty hospitals
Important note:
X-ray technicians must follow radiation safety rules strictly and work under doctor supervision.
