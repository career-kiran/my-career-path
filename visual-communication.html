Visual Communication Professional

Who is a Visual Communication Professional?

A Visual Communication professional is someone who uses images, videos, graphics, and design to communicate ideas and tell stories in a clear and creative way.

You may enjoy this career if:

  • You enjoy creative storytelling
  • You like designing posters, videos, or digital content
  • You are interested in film, media, advertising, or social media
  • You like expressing ideas visually instead of only through words

Real Life Examples (India)

  • A Visual Communication graduate works in a film production team and helps edit scenes, design posters, and create promotional videos for a new movie.
  • A Visual Communication professional designs social media campaigns and advertisements for a brand using images, videos, and creative storytelling.

Courses to Become a Visual Communication Professional

1. Bachelor's Degree (3 years)

You can choose:

  • BA in Visual Communication
  • BSc in Visual Communication
  • BA in Digital Filmmaking
  • BSc in Media Production
  • BA or BSc in Electronic Media
  • BSc in Animation and Design
  • BDes (Communication Design)

Eligibility: 12th pass (any stream - Arts / Science / Commerce)

Admission Process:

  • Many colleges give admission based on 12th marks
  • Some universities may conduct entrance exams or interviews
  • Private colleges may conduct aptitude or creative tests

2. Diploma Courses (1-2 years)

If you prefer a shorter course:

  • Diploma in Visual Communication
  • Diploma in Animation
  • Diploma in Film Editing
  • Diploma in Media Production

Eligibility: 12th pass (some institutes accept after 10th)

Admission Process: Mostly merit-based or institute-level entrance

3. Postgraduate Courses (2 years)

After completing your bachelor's degree:

  • MA in Visual Communication
  • MSc in Visual Communication
  • MDes (Communication Design)
  • MFA (Visual Communication)

Eligibility: Bachelor's degree in Visual Communication / Media / Design or related field

Admission Process:

  • University entrance exams
  • Creative work review (showing your work)
  • Interviews

Where Do Visual Communication Professionals Work?

  • Film and television industry
  • Advertising agencies
  • Digital marketing companies
  • Media production houses
  • Animation studios
  • News and media organizations
  • Corporate companies (branding and design teams)
  • Freelancing or starting your own creative studio

Emerging Areas

  • AI and Digital Media
  • Advanced Animation
  • Visual Effects (VFX)
  • Digital Content Creation

Important Note:
This field mainly depends on your creativity. Keep improving your design skills, practice regularly, and build a collection of your best creative work.
