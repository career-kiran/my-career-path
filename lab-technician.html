Lab Technician

A lab technician is a healthcare professional who performs medical tests on blood, urine, and other samples to help doctors find out diseases.

What does a lab technician usually do?

  • Collect blood and other samples from patients
  • Perform laboratory tests using machines and equipment
  • Prepare test reports for doctors
  • Maintain cleanliness and safety in the laboratory

You may enjoy this career if you:

  • Like working with machines and lab equipment
  • Are careful and pay attention to details
  • Prefer lab work rather than direct patient care
  • Can follow procedures and safety rules

Real-life example

A patient gives a blood sample in a hospital. The lab technician tests the sample in the lab and shares the report with the doctor so the doctor can decide the treatment.

Courses to become a Lab Technician (India)

B.Sc Medical Laboratory Technology (BMLT)

Duration: 3 years

Eligibility: 12th with Biology

Minimum marks: Usually 45–50%

Entrance exam: Institute / state / university level

B.Voc in Medical Laboratory Technology

Duration: 3 years

Eligibility: 12th with Science

Minimum marks: Usually 45–50%

Entrance exam: Institute / university based

DMLT (Diploma in Medical Laboratory Technology)

Duration: 2 years

Eligibility: 12th pass (Science preferred)

Minimum marks: Usually 40–45%

Entrance exam: Varies by institute or state

Certificate in Medical Laboratory Technology

Duration: 6 months – 1 year

Eligibility: 10th or 12th pass

Entrance exam: Usually not required

Helps students work as lab assistants under supervision.

Admission process (India)

  • Admission may be merit-based based on 12th marks
  • Some colleges conduct their own entrance exams
  • A few universities use state or university-level entrance exams
  • Rules can vary by state, college, and university

Where do lab technicians work?

  • Hospitals (government and private)
  • Diagnostic laboratories
  • Clinics
  • Blood banks
  • Research laboratories
Important note:
Certificate and diploma holders usually work under supervision. Higher qualifications may offer better responsibilities and growth opportunities. Admission rules and entrance exams can vary by state and institution.
