🏛 Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS)

🏛 What is Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS)?

Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) is the national standards body of India. It sets quality standards for products and services to make sure they are safe and reliable.

BIS also gives the ISI certification mark to products that meet safety and quality requirements.

🛡 What Do They Do?

  • Set quality standards for products in India.
  • Test products to check if they are safe and reliable.
  • Give ISI certification mark to approved products.
  • Conduct inspections and quality checks in factories.

💖 You May Enjoy This Path If You

  • like checking quality and standards.
  • are detail-oriented and careful in your work.
  • are interested in safety and product testing.
  • like inspection and field-based work.

📍 Real Life Examples

  • A BIS officer checks whether electrical appliances meet safety standards before they are sold in the market.
  • A BIS officer checks packaged food products are safe and meet quality before they reach customers.

How To Get Job In BIS

🎓 After 10th / 12th (Technical Trade Posts)

Senior Technician

  • Eligibility: 10th (Matric) pass + ITI certificate in relevant trade (Electrician, Fitter, Carpenter, Plumber, Turner, Welder, etc.).
  • Experience: Usually 2 years practical experience after ITI.
  • Age Limit: Generally 18–27 years (varies by notification).
  • Selection Process: Computer-Based Test (CBT) + Trade / Skill Test + Document Verification + Medical Examination.

ITI Apprentice

  • Eligibility: 10th / 12th pass + ITI certificate in relevant trade.
  • Experience: Freshers can apply (varies by recruitment).
  • Age Limit: Usually 18–27 years (varies by notification).
  • Selection Process: Written Test (if conducted) + Trade / Skill Assessment + Document Verification.

Important:

  • Exact trade requirements and vacancies vary by notification.
  • Students must check the official BIS recruitment website for latest updates.

🛠 Roles & Responsibilities (After 10th / 12th)

Senior Technician

  • Maintain and repair equipment used in BIS laboratories and offices.
  • Assist engineers and scientists in testing and inspection activities.
  • Handle tools, machines, and technical instruments safely.
  • Ensure proper working condition of electrical and mechanical systems.

ITI Apprentice

  • Work under supervision to learn technical trade skills.
  • Assist senior technicians in daily technical tasks.
  • Support maintenance and basic repair work.
  • Gain hands-on experience in BIS technical operations.

📘 Exam Details (After 10th / 12th – Technical Trade Posts)

Senior Technician

  • Computer-Based Test (CBT): Multiple Choice (MCQ) questions covering General Awareness, Reasoning, basic Mathematics, and trade-related knowledge.
  • Trade / Skill Test: Practical test to assess hands-on technical skills in the relevant trade (Electrician, Fitter, etc.).
  • Document Verification: Verification of educational, ITI, and experience certificates.
  • Medical Examination: Basic health check before final appointment.

ITI Apprentice

  • Written Test (if conducted): Multiple Choice (MCQ) questions based on basic trade knowledge and general subjects.
  • Trade / Skill Assessment: Practical evaluation of technical skills in the relevant ITI trade.
  • Document Verification: Verification of educational and ITI certificates.

📌 Selection Process (After 10th / 12th – Technical Trade Posts)

Senior Technician

Step 1 Apply online through the official recruitment portal when notification is released.
Step 2 Appear for the Computer-Based Test (MCQ format).
Step 3 Qualified candidates attend the Trade / Skill Test (practical test).
Step 4 Document Verification of educational, ITI, and experience certificates.
Step 5 Medical Examination before final appointment.
Final Selection Final merit list prepared based on performance in written and skill tests.

ITI Apprentice

Step 1 Apply online when apprenticeship notification is released.
Step 2 Shortlisting based on eligibility and academic marks.
Step 3 Attend Trade / Skill Assessment (if conducted).
Step 4 Document Verification.
Final Selection Selection based on trade performance and eligibility criteria.

🎓 Jobs After Graduation (Group A, B & C)

Scientist-B

  • Eligibility: B.E / B.Tech in relevant engineering fields (Civil, Computer, Electrical, Electronics, etc.) OR M.Sc in Natural Sciences (e.g., Chemistry).
  • Minimum Marks: As per notification.
  • Age Limit: Maximum 30 years.
  • Selection Process: Shortlisting based on valid GATE score (85% weightage) + Personal Interview (15% weightage).

Technical Assistant

  • Eligibility: Bachelor’s Degree in Science (Chemistry / Microbiology) OR 3-year Diploma in Mechanical / Chemical Engineering.
  • Minimum Marks: 60% aggregate (50% for SC/ST candidates).
  • Age Limit: Maximum 30 years.
  • Selection Process: Online Exam + Skill Test (Computer proficiency – qualifying in nature).

Assistant Section Officer (ASO)

  • Eligibility: Bachelor’s Degree in any discipline.
  • Age Limit: Maximum 30 years.
  • Selection Process: Online Written Exam (Reasoning, Quantitative Aptitude, English, General Awareness) + Skill Test (if required).

SSA / JSA / Stenographer

  • Eligibility: Bachelor’s Degree in any discipline.
  • Age Limit: Maximum 27 years.
  • Selection Process: Online Written Exam + Typing Test (for JSA/SSA) OR Shorthand Test (for Stenographer).

Important:

  • Exact eligibility, age limits, and branches vary by recruitment notification.
  • Students must check the official BIS recruitment website for latest updates.
