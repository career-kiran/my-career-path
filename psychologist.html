Counsellor / Psychologist

Counsellors and psychologists both help people with emotional and mental challenges. Counsellors usually focus on guidance and support, while psychologists have advanced training to assess and treat mental health conditions.

A counsellor or psychologist helps people talk about their problems, understand their feelings, and find ways to deal with stress, confusion, or difficult situations in life.

Real-life examples

Counsellor:
If someone feels stressed or anxious due to work, family, or personal issues, a counsellor listens to their problems and supports them in finding ways to handle these challenges.

Psychologist:
If a person keeps feeling sad, anxious, or fearful for many months and finds it hard to study, work, or enjoy daily life, a psychologist helps them understand why this is happening and teaches ways to manage these feelings over time.

You may enjoy this career if you:

  • Like listening to people and understanding their problems
  • Are willing to support others without judging them
  • Enjoy meaningful conversations
  • Want to help people improve their emotional well-being

Course path to become a Counsellor

Bachelor’s Degree

Common courses: BA Psychology, BSc Psychology

Duration: 3 years

Eligibility:

  • 12th standard (any stream in most colleges)
  • Minimum marks: usually 45–50%

Admission process: Mostly merit-based or university-level entrance exam

Master’s Degree (Required)

Common courses: MA Counselling Psychology, MA Applied Psychology

Duration: 2 years

Eligibility:

  • Bachelor’s degree in Psychology or related field
  • Minimum marks: usually 50–55%

Course path to become a Psychologist

Bachelor’s Degree

Common courses: BA Psychology, BSc Psychology

Duration: 3 years

Eligibility:

  • 12th standard (any stream in many colleges)
  • Minimum marks: usually 45–50%

Admission process: Mostly merit-based or university-level entrance exam

Master’s Degree (Mandatory)

Common courses: MA Psychology, MSc Psychology

Duration: 2 years

Eligibility:

  • Bachelor’s degree in Psychology
  • Minimum marks: usually 50–55%
Important
Psychologists may need additional training, licensing, or registration depending on their area of practice.

Where do counsellors and psychologists work?

  • Schools and colleges
  • Hospitals and mental health centres
  • Career guidance centres
  • Rehabilitation centres
  • NGOs and community organisations
  • Private counselling clinics

Important note

This field focuses on understanding people and supporting their mental and emotional well-being. It requires patience, empathy, and continuous learning.
