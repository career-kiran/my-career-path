GNM Nursing Entrance Exams

What are ANM Nursing Entrance Exams?

GNM Nursing Entrance Exams are entrance examinations conducted by various state governments and institutions for admission to the General Nursing and Midwifery (GNM) courses in India.

Conducted by: Various state governments, nursing councils and examination authorities.

There is no single national entrance examination for admission to GNM (General Nursing and Midwifery) courses in India.

In many states, admission is offered based on Class 12 marks. However, some states and institutions may conduct their own entrance examinations for admission.

Admission procedures, eligibility criteria and selection processes may vary from state to state.

📌 Quick Facts

Eligibility: 12th Pass

Mode: Online or Offline (varies by state)

Duration: Varies by State

Questions: Varies by State

Frequency: Usually conducted once a year

Qualification: Passed or appearing in 12th from a recognised board.

Age: Minimum 17 years as on or before 31st December of the admission year.

Minimum Marks: Usually 40-45% (May vary by state and institution.)

Domicile: Some states may require candidates to be residents of that state.

Mode: Online or Offline (varies by state)

Duration: Varies by State

Total Questions: Varies by State

Question Type: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

Subjects: General Knowledge, English, Science and Reasoning (may vary by state)

Marking Scheme: Varies by State
