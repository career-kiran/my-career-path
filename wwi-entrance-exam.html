WWI Entrance Exam

WWI Entrance Exam is the admission test conducted by Whistling Woods International (WWI), one of India's leading institutes.

This exam is conducted for admission to filmmaking, acting, animation, design, media, and creative arts courses.

Students seeking admission to various undergraduate and postgraduate courses at WWI must participate in the admission process.

Conducted by: Whistling Woods International (WWI), Mumbai.

📌 Quick Facts

Eligibility:

Class 12 pass - for UG courses

Bachelor's degree - for PG courses.

Admission Process:

The WWI admission process consists of four stages:

  • MECAT: Media & Entertainment Common Aptitude Test.
  • CAT: Creative Ability Test.
  • SOP: Statement of Purpose.
  • PI: Personal Interview.

Mode: Applications are submitted online. Entrance tests and interviews are generally conducted on campus.

Selection Criteria: Final admission is based on overall performance of all 4 stages of the selection process.

Frequency: Admission rounds are conducted throughout the year.

Qualification:

  • For Undergraduate (UG) courses - candidates must have passed Class 12 (10+2) or an equivalent examination from a recognized board.
  • For Postgraduate (PG) courses - candidates must hold a Bachelor's degree from a recognized university.
  • Students appearing for their final Class 12 or Graduation examinations may also apply provisionally.
  • Some specialized courses may have additional eligibility requirements prescribed by WWI.

Age:

  • Candidates should generally be at least 17 years of age at the time of admission.
  • No specific upper age limit is prescribed for most courses.

Min mark:

  • Class 12 or Graduation must be completed from a recognized institution.
  • Specific minimum percentage requirements may vary depending on the course.

Attempts:

  • There is generally no restriction on the number of attempts for the WWI Entrance Exam.
  • Candidates may apply again in future admission cycles if they meet the eligibility requirements.

Mode:

Applications are submitted online.

Tests and interviews may be conducted online or on campus depending on the admission cycle.

Frequency: Admission rounds are conducted throughout the year.

Duration: Varies depending on the course.

Total Questions: Varies depending on the course.

Total Marks: Varies depending on the course.

Selection Process:

The WWI admission process consists of four stages:

  • Stage 1 – MECAT (Media & Entertainment Common Aptitude Test): Tests your aptitude, general knowledge, and communication skills.
  • Stage 2 – CAT (Creative Ability Test): Tests your creativity and imagination.
  • Stage 3 – SOP (Statement of Purpose): A short write-up explaining why you want to join the course.
  • Stage 4 – PI (Personal Interview): An interview to understand your interests, goals, and suitability for the course.

Evaluation:

  • Performance in all four stages is considered during the admission process.
  • The final selection is based on the overall performance of the candidate.
  • The weightage of each stage may vary depending on the course.

Important: WWI looks for students who are creative, motivated, and passionate about their chosen field.
