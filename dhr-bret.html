DHR-BRET (Department of Health Research - Biomedical Research Eligibility Test)

A student writes the DHR-BRET (Biomedical Research Eligibility Test) after completing a postgraduate degree or an eligible professional degree in a relevant subject.

This examination is for students who wish to pursue research and Ph.D. studies in biomedical and health sciences.

Candidates who qualify become eligible for a Junior Research Fellowship (JRF), where they receive a monthly stipend while doing research.

  • Fellowship Awarded By: Department of Health Research (DHR), Government of India
  • Exam Conducted By: National Testing Agency (NTA)

📌 Quick Facts

Who Can Apply? Students with eligible qualifications in Biotechnology, Life Sciences, and related fields.

Mode: Computer Based Test (CBT)

Questions: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

Frequency: Usually conducted once a year

Educational Qualification: Candidates must have a postgraduate degree or an eligible professional degree in Biomedical Sciences, Health Sciences, Life Sciences, or related subjects.

Eligible Degrees:

  • M.Sc.
  • M.Tech.
  • M.Pharm.
  • M.V.Sc. / MBBS / BDS
  • Integrated M.Sc. / M.Tech.
  • B.E. / B.Tech. (4-year professional degrees)

Minimum Marks (Without Rounding Off):

  • 55% - General / EWS / OBC (NCL) candidates
  • 50% - SC / ST / PwBD candidates

Age Limit (Calculated as of application closing date):

  • General / EWS candidates: 35 years
  • OBC (NCL): 3 years relaxation (Up to 38 years)
  • SC / ST / PwBD / All Female Candidates: 5 years relaxation (Up to 40 years)

Final-Year Students: Candidates appearing for their final semester or final year examinations may apply under the "Result Awaited" (RA) category, subject to fulfilling the requirements upon admission.

Note: Candidates should always check the latest official NTA / DHR-BRET notification, as eligibility requirements may change from year to year.

Mode of Examination: Computer-Based Test (CBT)

Question Type: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

Duration: 3 Hours (180 Minutes)

Total Questions: 150 Questions (All questions are compulsory)

Total Marks: 150 Marks

Question Paper Structure:

  • Part A (50 Questions / 50 Marks): Aptitude, Logical Reasoning, Quantitative Ability, Data Interpretation, Research Methodology, and Bioethics.
  • Part B (50 Questions / 50 Marks): General concepts in Biomedical and Life Sciences.
  • Part C (50 Questions / 50 Marks): Advanced questions from the subject chosen by the candidate.

Marking Scheme:

  • Correct Answer: +1 Mark
  • Wrong Answer: -0.25 Mark
  • Unanswered Question: 0 Marks

Medium of Paper: English Only

Note: Students should always check the latest official DHR-BRET notification, as the exam pattern may change in future years.

Candidates who qualify DHR-BRET may pursue research and doctoral programmes in biomedical and health sciences.

Programmes may include:

  • Ph.D. in Biomedical Sciences
  • Ph.D. in Life Sciences
  • Ph.D. in Biotechnology
  • Ph.D. in Microbiology
  • Ph.D. in Biochemistry
  • Ph.D. in Genetics
  • Ph.D. in Pharmacology
  • Ph.D. in Public Health
  • Ph.D in Nursing
  • Ph.D in Biostatistics
  • Ph.D. in Biomedical Engineering
  • Other Biomedical and Health Science Research Programmes

Note: DHR-BRET is primarily a fellowship examination. Qualifying the exam helps students receive financial support while pursuing research and Ph.D. studies.
