🏛 SSC & Central Government Exams

👤 What is SSC?

The Staff Selection Commission (SSC) is a central government body that conducts exams for various posts in Central Government departments and ministries.

It helps you get government jobs after 12th or graduation.

🛡️ What kind of jobs can you get?

  • Income Tax Inspector – in Income Tax Department
  • Central Excise Inspector – in CBIC
  • Assistant Section Officer – in Central Ministries
  • Sub-Inspector – in CBI(Central Bureau of Investigation)or Delhi Police
  • Junior Engineer(JE) - in Government Engineering departments
  • Auditor – in Government Audit Departments
  • Clerk / Data Entry Operator – in variuos Central Government Offices

Note: To get these jobs, you must clear one of the following SSC exams.

📚 Major SSC Exams

SSC CGL (Combined Graduate Level)

Eligibility: Graduation required

Posts: Inspector, Assistant Section Officer, Auditor etc.

SSC CHSL (Combined Higher Secondary Level)

Eligibility: 12th pass

Posts: Clerk, Postal Assistant, Data Entry Operator

SSC CPO

Eligibility: Graduation + Physical standards

Posts: Sub-Inspector in Delhi Police & CAPF

SSC MTS

Eligibility: 10th pass

Posts: Multi-Tasking Staff in Central Offices

SSC GD Constable

Eligibility: 10th pass

Posts: Constable in CAPFs, SSF, Assam Rifles

SSC JE (Junior Engineer)

Eligibility: Diploma or Engineering Degree

Posts: Junior Engineer in Central Government Departments

SSC Stenographer (Grade C & D)

Eligibility: 12th pass

Posts: Stenographer in Central Government Offices

SSC Selection Post

Eligibility: 10th / 12th / Graduation (varies)

Posts: Various departmental posts in Central Government

SSC JHT (Junior Hindi Translator)

Eligibility: Master’s degree in Hindi/English

Posts: Translator in Central Government Ministries

🏢 Where do they work?

  • Inspector – in Income Tax Department
  • Assistant Section Officer – in Central Government Ministries
  • Auditor – in Comptroller and Auditor General (CAG) offices
  • Sub-Inspector – in CBI or Delhi Police
  • Clerk (LDC/UDC) – in Central Government departments
  • Junior Engineer - in CPWD(Central Public Works Department), MES(Military Engineer Services), BRO(Border Roads Organisation)

🌍 Global Opportunities

Most SSC jobs are within India’s Central Government. However, certain posts like Assistant Section Officer (ASO) in the Ministry of External Affairs (MEA) may offer foreign postings after a few years of service.

⭐ Career Insight

SSC exams are competitive, but with consistent preparation and discipline, many students clear them every year.

📌 How to Apply

Official Website: https://ssc.gov.in/

Application Process: Register at the portal and fill in details.

Eligibility: Generally 18–32 years depending on the post.
