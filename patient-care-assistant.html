Patient Care Assistant

A Patient Care Assistant helps patients with their daily needs in hospitals and care centers. They work closely with nurses to make sure patients are comfortable and safe.

What does a Patient Care Assistant usually do?

  • Help patients with bathing, dressing, and hygiene
  • Assist patients in moving, sitting, or walking
  • Help patients eat meals and drink fluids
  • Check patient needs and report problems to nurses
  • Keep patient beds and surroundings clean

You may enjoy this career if you:

  • Like helping and caring for people
  • Are comfortable working closely with patients
  • Can follow instructions from nurses and doctors
  • Prefer hands-on, practical work
  • Want to enter healthcare without long academic courses
Real-life example:
In a hospital ward, a patient care assistant helps a patient get out of bed, assists them to the bathroom, helps with meals, and informs the nurse if the patient feels unwell.

Courses Available

Certificate Course in Patient Care Assistant

  • Duration: 6 months – 1 year
  • Eligibility: 10th pass
  • Entrance exam: Not required
  • Admission: Direct admission

Diploma in Patient Care Assistant / Nursing Assistant

  • Duration: 1 – 2 years
  • Eligibility: 10th or 12th pass
  • Entrance exam: Usually not required
  • Admission: Direct or merit-based

Where do Patient Care Assistants work?

  • Hospitals
  • Nursing homes
  • Old-age homes
  • Rehabilitation centers
  • Home healthcare services
Important note:
Patient Care Assistants work under the supervision of nurses and doctors. They do not perform medical procedures or give treatment independently.
