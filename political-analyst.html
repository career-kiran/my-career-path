🏛️ Political Analyst

👤 Who is a Political Analyst?

A political analyst is someone who studies politics and government decisions and explains what they mean for people and the country.

🛡️ What does a Political Analyst do?

  • Studies government policies and political events.
  • Analyzes election results and political trends.
  • Shares opinions and insights through articles, reports, or TV debates.
  • Advises organizations or leaders on political matters.

💖 You may enjoy this profession if:

  • You are interested in politics and current affairs.
  • You like discussing and analyzing issues.
  • You enjoy reading news and forming opinions.

💡 Real Life Examples

A political analyst explains election results on TV and helps people understand why a party won or lost.

They may write newspaper articles explaining new government policies in simple language.

📚 How to Become a Political Analyst?

BA in Political Science (3 Years)

Duration: 3 Years

Eligibility: 12th pass in any stream

Minimum Marks: 45–50% aggregate (varies by college)

Admission: Merit-based or entrance exam (like CUET)

MA in Political Science / Public Policy (2 Years)

Duration: 2 Years

Eligibility: Bachelor’s degree

Minimum Marks: Usually 50% in graduation

Admission: University-level entrance exams or merit-based

PhD in Political Science (3–5 Years)

Duration: 3–5 Years

Eligibility: Master’s degree in relevant field

Admission: UGC-NET / University PhD entrance exam

📘 Exam Full Forms

CUET – Common University Entrance Test

UGC-NET – University Grants Commission National Eligibility Test

University-level exams – Some colleges and universities conduct their own entrance test for admission.

🏢 Where do they work?

  • Policy Research Analyst – in think tanks and policy research institutes
  • Political Consultant – in political consulting firms
  • Research Associate – in universities and academic institutions
  • Media Political Analyst – in news channels and digital media platforms
  • Government Advisor – in government departments and ministries
  • Legislative Assistant – working with Members of Parliament (MPs) or MLAs
  • Election Strategy Analyst – in election campaign teams
  • NGO Policy Analyst – in non-governmental organizations
  • Public Affairs Specialist – in corporate public affairs divisions

🌍 Global Opportunities

  • Policy Analyst – in international organizations (UN, World Bank, etc.)
  • Research Fellow – in global policy research institutes
  • Foreign Affairs Analyst – in diplomatic and international affairs research bodies
  • Political Correspondent – in international media networks
  • Political Consultant – in political consulting firms abroad

⭐ Career Insight

This career requires strong knowledge of current affairs, critical thinking skills, and the ability to explain complex political issues in simple language.
