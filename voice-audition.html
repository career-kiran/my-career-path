Voice Audition

Voice Audition is a selection process conducted by some colleges, universities, and media institutes for admission to voice acting, dubbing, radio, and other voice-related courses.

During the audition, the judges check your voice quality, pronunciation, clarity, expression, and confidence.

You may be asked to read a script, dub a short video, tell a story, or speak with different emotions.

In simple words, a voice audition is conducted to find out whether you have the voice skills needed to join a voice-related course.

Example:

A student applies for a Diploma in Voice Acting.

During the audition, the student reads a short script, gives a voice to a cartoon character, and speaks the same dialogue with different emotions.

The judges check the student's voice, pronunciation, expression, and confidence.

Based on the performance, the institute decides whether to offer admission.
