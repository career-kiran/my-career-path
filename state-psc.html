🏛️ State Public Service Commission (PSC)

👤 What is PSC?

Each state in India has its own Public Service Commission (PSC).

PSC conducts exams to recruit candidates for various state government jobs.

💖 You May Enjoy This Career Path If:

  • You want job security and a stable income
  • You are interested in serving the public
  • You are ready to prepare consistently for competitive exams
  • You prefer structured career growth
  • You like office work or government jobs

📚 Jobs You Can Get Through PSC

After 10th:

  • Village Assistant
  • Forest Guard(in some states)
  • Office Attendant
  • Police Constable (State Police)

Exam to Clear: State PSC 10th Level Exams

📝 State PSC 10th Level Exam

This is a common recruitment exam conducted by the State Public Service Commission for 10th pass candidates applying for various entry-level government posts.

  • Eligibility: 10th pass (SSLC) or equivalent qualification.
  • Age Limit: Generally 18–36 years (age relaxation applicable for reserved categories as per state rules).
  • Selection Process: Usually includes a Preliminary exam, followed by a Main exam and sometimes physical or skill tests depending on the post.

After 12th:

  • Junior Assistant
  • Clerk
  • Typist
  • Sub Inspector of Police(in some states))
  • village Administrative Officer(VAO)
  • Revenue Assistant
  • Forest Guard/ Forest Watcher
  • Stenographer

Exam to Clear: State PSC 12th Level Exams

📝 State PSC 12th Level Exam

This exam is conducted by the State Public Service Commission for candidates who have completed 12th standard (Higher Secondary). It recruits for clerical, assistant, and certain uniformed service posts.

  • Eligibility: 12th pass (Higher Secondary) or equivalent qualification.
  • Age Limit: Generally 18–36 years (age relaxation applicable for reserved categories as per state rules).
  • Selection Process: Usually includes a Preliminary exam, followed by a Main exam and depending on the post, may include physical or skill tests.

After Graduation: (Major PSC Level)

This is where most important PSC posts come.

Group 1 Level (Top State Administrative Jobs):

  • Deputy Collector
  • Deputy Superintendent of Police (DSP)
  • Assistant Commissioner
  • District Registrar
  • Block Development Officer (BDO)

Group 2 Level:

  • Tahsildar
  • Commercial Tax Officer
  • Sub-Registrar
  • Municipal Commissioner (in smaller municipalities)
  • Assistant Section Officer

Group 3 Level (in some states):

  • Junior Employment Officer
  • Cooperative Officer
  • Assistant Inspector

Exams to Clear:

  • State PSC Group 1 Exam
  • State PSC Group 2 Exam
  • State PSC Group 3 Exam

Exam to Clear: State PSC Graduation Level Exams (Group 1, 2, 3 & 4)

State Public Service Commission conducts different Group exams for candidates who have completed graduation.(Bachelor's degree)

These exams recruit for administrative, revenue, police, and various government department posts.

  • Eligibility: Bachelor’s degree in any discipline from a recognized university.
  • Age Limit: Generally 21–36 years (may vary by state and post; age relaxation applicable).
  • Selection Process: Usually includes Preliminary exam, Main exam, and Interview (for higher groups). Certain posts(like Police or technical roles)may include physical or skill tests.
  • Group 1 – Higher-level officer posts (Example: Deputy Collector, DSP, Assistant Commissioner)
  • Group 2 – Mid-level administrative posts (Example: Assistant Section Officer, Municipal Officer, Commercial Tax Officer)
  • Group 3 / 4 – Clerical and assistant-level posts (Example: Junior Assistant, Typist, Village Assistant)
  • Departmental Exams – For promotion of existing government employees

📌 Important Clarification

  • Each state in India has its own Public Service Commission (PSC) such as Kerala PSC, TNPSC, KPSC, MPSC, etc.
  • Job names may change slightly from state to state, but the exam pattern and group system (Group 1, 2, 3, 4) are mostly similar.
