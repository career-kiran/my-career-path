📰 Journalist / News Reporter

👤 Who is a Journalist?

A journalist finds information and tells people what is happening in the world around them

They help people understand what is happening in society, politics, business, sports, and everyday life.

🛡️ What does a Journalist do?

  • Collects information by interviewing people and researching topics
  • Verifies facts before publishing news
  • Writes articles or reports for media platforms
  • Covers live events like elections, press meetings, or public issues

💖 You may enjoy this profession if:

  • You are curious about what is happening around you
  • You like asking questions and talking to people
  • You enjoy writing or presenting information clearly

💡 Real Life Examples

  • A reporter covers a local flood situation and explains what happened and how people are affected.
  • A political journalist interviews leaders and explains government decisions in simple language for the public.

📚 How to Become a Journalist?

BA in Journalism / Mass Communication / Mass Media

Duration: 3 Years

Eligibility: 12th pass in any stream

Minimum Marks: 50% aggregate (may vary by college)

Admission: Merit-based or university-level exams like CUET

BJMC – Bachelor of Journalism & Mass Communication

Duration: 3 Years

Eligibility: 12th pass

Minimum Marks: 50% aggregate

Admission: University-level entrance exams or merit

MA in Journalism / Mass Communication / Mass Media

Duration: 2 Years

Eligibility: Bachelor’s degree in any subject

Admission: University-level entrance exams or merit

PG Diploma in Journalism

Duration: 1 Year

Eligibility: Bachelor’s degree

Admission: Merit-based or institute-level entrance

📝 Exam Notes

CUET – Common University Entrance Test

University-level exams – Some colleges and universities conduct their own entrance test for admission.

🏢 Where do they work?

  • News Reporter – in newspapers, television news channels, and online news portals
  • News Anchor – in TV news studios
  • Content Writer – in digital media companies
  • Investigative Journalist – in independent news organizations

🌍 Global Opportunities

  • Foreign Correspondent – in international news agencies
  • Media Researcher – in global media networks
  • Digital Journalist – in international online media platforms

⭐ Career Insight

Journalism requires honesty, responsibility, and strong communication skills. Your credibility and accuracy are very important in this profession.
