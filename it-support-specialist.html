🖥️ IT Support Specialist

🧠 Imagine This…

"In large IT companies and offices, employees use computers every day to do their work.

If a system stops working or a software problem occurs, the entire work can be affected.

When such issues happen, someone checks the problem, finds the cause, and fixes it quickly.

They make sure computers, software, and networks work properly without stopping daily tasks."

This is the work of an IT Support Specialist.

💖 You May Enjoy This Path If…

  • You like working with computers and software
  • You enjoy solving problems
  • You feel happy when things start working again
  • You are patient while fixing issues
  • You like helping others with technical problems

👉 How This Works in Real Life?

A computer in an office stops working during important work.
↓
The IT support team checks the system to find the problem.
↓
They identify whether it is a hardware issue (physical problem) or software issue.
↓
They fix the issue by repairing, updating, or reinstalling the system.
↓
The system is tested to make sure everything works properly.
↓
Finally, the computer is ready to use, and work continues smoothly.
↓
Now imagine you doing the same work. If this excites you, this career may suit you.

🚀 Want to Try This Yourself?

  • Watch a video on how to fix common computer problems
  • Try installing and uninstalling software on a computer
  • Explore basic settings like Wi-Fi, sound, and display
  • Restart and troubleshoot (find and fix) small issues on your device
  • Learn basic computer parts like RAM, storage, and CPU
  • Try simple system checks like cleaning files or updating software
  • Try to fix a slow laptop or computer

🎓 How to Become an IT Support Specialist

After 10th Standard (Foundation & Entry Level)

After 10th, students can start learning basic computer hardware and networking.

These courses focus on practical skills like repairing and maintaining computers.

ITI in Computer Hardware & Network Maintenance (CHNM)

Duration: 1 year

Eligibility: 10th pass

Minimum Marks: Usually 35% – 50%

Age Limit: Minimum 14–15 years

Exam: State ITI entrance exam or merit-based admission

Diploma in Computer Hardware and Networking (DCHN)

Duration: 6 months to 1 year

Eligibility: 10th pass

Minimum Marks: Passing marks (varies by institute)

Age Limit: Minimum 14–16 years

Exam: Institute-level test or merit-based

Polytechnic Diploma in Computer Engineering / IT

Duration: 3 years

Eligibility: 10th pass (with Science & Mathematics)

Minimum Marks: Usually 35% – 50%

Age Limit: Minimum 14–15 years

Exam: State polytechnic entrance exams

After 12th Standard (Main Career Path)

After 12th, students can enter degree courses to learn about computer systems, software, and networks.

This is the most common path to become an IT Support Specialist.

BCA (Bachelor of Computer Applications)

Duration: 3 years

Eligibility: 12th pass (any stream)

Minimum Marks: Usually 45% – 50%

Age Limit: Minimum 17 years

Exam: CUET UG / university entrance exams / merit-based

BSc Information Technology / Computer Science

Duration: 3 years

Eligibility: 12th pass (Science stream preferred)

Minimum Marks: Usually 45% – 60%

Age Limit: Minimum 17 years

Exam: CUET / university entrance exams / merit-based

B.Tech in Computer Science / Information Technology

Duration: 4 years

Eligibility: 12th with Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics (PCM)

Minimum Marks: Usually 50% – 60%

Age Limit: Minimum 17 years

Exam: JEE Main / state entrance exams / university tests

After Graduation (Advanced Skills – Optional)

After completing a degree, students can improve their knowledge and move into higher roles in IT support or system management.

MCA (Master of Computer Applications)

Duration: 2 years

Eligibility: Graduation (with Mathematics at 10+2 or degree level)

Minimum Marks: Usually 50% – 60%

Age Limit: No strict age limit

Exam: NIMCET / MAH MCA CET / CUET PG

PGDCA (Post Graduate Diploma in Computer Applications)

Duration: 1 year

Eligibility: Graduation in any stream

Minimum Marks: Around 50%

Age Limit: No age limit

Exam: Merit-based / institute-level admission

⭐ Note

IT support courses include both theory and practical learning.
Students learn about computer hardware (physical parts), software, and basic networking.

In many courses, students also practice solving real problems.
They learn how to fix system errors, install software, and handle common technical issues.

Regular practice and hands-on experience are very important to become confident in this field.

💼 Where do IT Support Specialists work? (Job + Where)

  • IT Support Specialist – work in offices, schools, and companies where computer systems are maintained and problems are fixed
  • Help Desk Executive – work in IT companies or support centers where users report issues and get help
  • Desktop Support Engineer – work in offices and organizations where computers are installed, updated, and repaired
  • System Support Assistant – work in hospitals, banks, and institutions to keep systems running smoothly
  • Network Support Technician – work in companies where internet and network connections are managed and fixed
  • Technical Support Executive – work in customer support teams helping people solve technical problems
  • IT Technician – work in companies or service centers where computer hardware and software issues are handled

🌍 Global Opportunities

  • IT Support Jobs in International Companies – work in global companies where computer systems and networks are managed
  • Technical Support Jobs Abroad – work in companies in other countries helping users fix software and system issues
  • Remote IT Support Jobs – work from home supporting international clients through calls, chat, or online tools
  • Cloud and System Support Roles – work in companies managing online systems and cloud-based services worldwide
  • Freelance IT Support Services – work independently helping clients fix technical problems
  • Tech Companies – work in global IT companies maintaining systems, networks, and software
  • Startups – work in growing tech startups handling IT support and system setup

💡 Career Insight

Many IT support specialists start by learning how computer systems and networks work, and by gaining experience through small tasks or internships.

With time, they can grow into roles like system administration, network management, or cybersecurity.

Experienced professionals can work in top companies, handle complex systems, or move into higher technical roles.
