🎓 How to Become an Animator

After 10th Grade

Courses:

  • Certificate in Animation
  • Diploma in Animation
  • Diploma in 2D/3D Animation
  • Diploma in Animation & VFX

Duration: 6 months to 2 years

Eligibility: After 10th

Minimum Marks: Usually around 40–50%

Exam to Pass: Usually not required (some institutes may conduct their own test)

After 12th Grade

Courses:

  • B.Sc Animation
  • Bachelor of Animation
  • B.Des Animation
  • BA Animation & Multimedia
  • Bachelor of Visual Arts(BVA)

Duration: 3 to 4 years

Eligibility: After 12th (any stream)

Minimum Marks: Usually around 50%

Exam to Pass: NID DAT, UCEED, CUET or institute entrance exams

After Graduation (Optional)

Courses:

  • M.Sc Animation
  • M.Des Animation
  • MA Animation
  • MA in Visual Effects & Animation
  • PG Diploma in Animation

Duration: 2 years; 1-2 years for PG Diploma

Eligibility: Bachelor’s degree in animation, design, or related field

Minimum Marks: Usually around 50%

Exam to Pass: CEED or institute entrance exams

Note: Students can study Animation after 12th from any stream (Science, Commerce, or Arts).

🏢 Where do Animators Work?

  • 2D/3D Animator – in animation studios that create movies, cartoons, and animated series.
  • Game Animator – in gaming companies creating character movements and actions for video games.
  • VFX Animator – in film and television production houses creating visual effects.
  • Advertising Animator – in advertising agencies creating animated ads and promotional videos.
  • Freelance Animator – work independently on projects for YouTube channels, brands, or online content.

🌍 Global Opportunities

  • Animation Studios – Work on animated movies, cartoons, and web series for international audiences.
  • Gaming Companies – Create character animations and movements for global video games.
  • Film & VFX Studios – Work on visual effects and animation for international movies and TV shows.
  • Advertising Agencies – Create animated ads and promotional videos for global brands.
  • Freelance Animation – Work with clients around the world through online platforms.

⭐ Career Insight

Many animators start their careers by working in animation studios, gaming companies, or film production houses. They create character movements, visual effects, and animated scenes for movies, games, advertisements, and digital content.

With experience, some animators specialize in areas like 3D animation, visual effects (VFX), or game animation. Skilled animators may also work with international studios or take freelance projects from clients around the world.
