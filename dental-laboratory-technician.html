Dental Laboratory Technician

A dental laboratory technician is a healthcare professional who makes dental items like crowns, bridges, dentures, and braces based on the dentist’s instructions. They usually work in a laboratory and do not treat patients directly.

What does a dental laboratory technician usually do?

  • Makes crowns, bridges, dentures, and dental appliances
  • Works with materials like metal, ceramic, and acrylic
  • Follows measurements and instructions given by dentists
  • Repairs and modifies dental appliances
  • Ensures dental devices fit properly and look natural
Real-life example
A patient loses a tooth and visits a dentist. The dentist takes measurements and sends them to a dental laboratory technician. The technician carefully makes a crown or denture so the patient can chew and smile comfortably again.

You may enjoy this career if you:

  • Like making things with your hands
  • Enjoy careful and neat work
  • Are comfortable working in a lab or workshop
  • Are interested in dental or medical work
  • Prefer less interaction with patients

Courses to become a Dental Laboratory Technician (India)

Diploma in Dental Laboratory Technology (DDLT / DLT)

Duration: 2 years (some institutes include internship)

Eligibility:

  • 10+2 (Science preferred, varies by institute)
  • Minimum marks: usually 40–50%

Admission process: Mostly merit-based; some institutes may conduct their own entrance tests.

Note: Course name, duration, and eligibility may vary by institute. Always check the official college website.

Other related courses and short-term training

  • Certificate courses in Dental Laboratory Technology
  • Short-term training in CAD/CAM dentistry
  • Dental ceramics and materials workshops
  • Removable denture and prosthetics training

These courses help improve specific skills but are usually taken along with or after a diploma, not as a full replacement.

Where do dental laboratory technicians work?

  • Dental laboratories
  • Dental colleges
  • Hospitals
  • Private dental clinics (attached labs)
  • Dental equipment and manufacturing companies

Important note

Dental laboratory technicians work behind the scenes and do not treat patients directly. Their role is essential in dental treatment by creating accurate and comfortable dental appliances.
