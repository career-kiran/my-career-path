GMAT (Graduate Management Admission Test)

GMAT is an international entrance examination conducted by the Graduate Management Admission Council (GMAC)for admission to MBA and other postgraduate management courses offered by business schools around the world.

GMAT scores are accepted by 2,400+ business schools across more than 110 countries for admission to MBA, Master's in Management, Master of Finance, Master of Business Analytics, and other postgraduate management programmes.

Conducted by: Graduate Management Admission Council (GMAC).

📌 Quick Facts

Eligibility: Bachelor's degree from a recognized university.

Exam Pattern: Computer-Based Test (CBT) consisting of Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) and Multi-Select Questions (MSQs).

Mode: Online (Computer-Based Test).

Duration: 2 Hours 15 Minutes (135 Minutes).

Frequency: Available throughout the year on multiple test dates.

Important: The examination is accepted by thousands of business schools worldwide for admission to MBA and other postgraduate management programmes.

Qualification:

  • There is no minimum educational qualification required to take the GMAT examination.
  • However, most business schools require a Bachelor's degree from a recognized university for admission.

Age:

  • Candidates must be at least 18 years old to take the GMAT examination.
  • Candidates aged 13 to 17 years can take the exam with written permission from a parent or legal guardian.
  • There is no upper age limit.

Minimum Marks:

  • For the Exam: There is no minimum score or percentage required to appear for the GMAT examination.
  • For Admission: The minimum graduation marks required vary depending on the business school and course.

Attempts:

  • You can take the GMAT up to 5 times in a 12-month period.
  • You can take the GMAT a maximum of 8 times in your lifetime.
  • You must wait at least 16 days before taking the exam again.

Nationality:

  • Candidates of all nationalities can apply for the GMAT examination.

Mode of Examination: Online (Computer-Based Test).

Question Type: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) and Multi-Select Questions (MSQs).

Duration: 2 Hours 15 Minutes (135 Minutes).

Total Questions: 64 Questions.

Sections:

  • Quantitative Reasoning: 21 Questions.
  • Verbal Reasoning: 23 Questions.
  • Data Insights: 20 Questions.

Marking Scheme:

  • GMAT has no fixed marking scheme.
  • Your overall score depends on the number of correct answers, the difficulty level of the questions, and the questions you leave unanswered.
  • It is recommended to answer every question before the exam ends.

Scoring: Your total GMAT score ranges from 205 to 805.

Students who qualify through the GMAT (Graduate Management Admission Test) can apply for postgraduate management programmes offered by business schools around the world.

Postgraduate Courses:

  • MBA (Master of Business Administration)
  • Master in Management (MiM)
  • Master of Finance (MFin)
  • Master of Business Analytics (MBAn)
  • Executive MBA (EMBA)
  • Specialized Master's Programmes
    • Marketing
    • Finance
    • Business Analytics
    • Human Resource Management
    • Operations Management
    • Supply Chain Management
    • International Business
    • And many more...
  • Doctoral Programmes (Ph.D./DBA) offered by select universities.

Important Note:

Qualifying for the GMAT does not guarantee admission. Candidates must meet the eligibility criteria of the respective university or business school.

Note:

In India, GMAT scores are accepted by many leading business schools(Indian School of Buisness, Xavier School of Management) for Executive MBA, one-year MBA, and other postgraduate management programmes.

Always check the admission policy of your preferred institute.
