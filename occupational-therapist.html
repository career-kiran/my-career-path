Occupational Therapist

An occupational therapist helps people regain the ability to perform daily activities such as dressing, eating, writing, working, or playing — especially after illness, injury, or developmental challenges.

Real-life example An adult finds it difficult to dress, cook, or write after a stroke. An occupational therapist helps the person practise these daily activities step by step and suggests simple changes to make tasks easier, so they can live more independently.

You may enjoy this career if you…

  • Like helping people become independent in daily life
  • Are willing to listen, understand, and support people patiently
  • Enjoy practical, activity-based work
  • Like working closely with individuals over time

Courses to become an Occupational Therapist (India)

Bachelor of Occupational Therapy (BOT)

Duration: 4.5 years (4 years study + 6 months internship)

Eligibility:

  • 12th standard with Physics, Chemistry, Biology
  • Minimum marks: 50–60% (varies by college)

Admission process: Entrance exam or merit-based (varies by institution)

Admission note Some colleges admit students based on merit, while others conduct university-level or state-level entrance exams.

Master of Occupational Therapy (MOT)

Duration: 2 years

Eligibility:

  • Completed Bachelor of Occupational Therapy (BOT)
  • Minimum marks: 50–55%

Admission process: University-level entrance exam or merit-based

Focus of this course:
Advanced training in areas such as paediatrics, neurology, mental health, rehabilitation, and ergonomics.

Where do Occupational Therapists work?

  • Hospitals and rehabilitation centres
  • Schools and special education centres
  • Mental health facilities
  • Community health settings
  • Private therapy clinics

Important note

Occupational therapy focuses on improving quality of life through meaningful daily activities. It requires patience, creativity, and long-term involvement with clients.
