🎓 How to Become an Actor

After 10th Standard (Foundations & Workshops)

Courses:

  • Certificate in Acting
  • Diploma in Dramatic Arts
  • Foundation Course in Acting

Duration: 3 – 6 months

Eligibility: 10th pass from any recognized board

Minimum Marks: Usually 45% – 50% aggregate

Age Limit: Minimum 15 – 16 years

Exam to Pass: Usually audition-based or direct admission in private acting academies

After 12th Standard (Undergraduate Degrees)

Courses:

  • B.A. in Acting
  • B.A. in Theatre Arts
  • Bachelor of Dramatic Arts (BDA)
  • B.A. in Performing Arts

Duration: Around 3 – 4 years

Eligibility: 10+2 pass in any stream

Minimum Marks: Minimum 50% aggregate in 12th

Age Limit: Around 17 – 25 years for many universities

Exam to Pass:

  • National Level: CUET-UG
  • Institute Level: FTII Entrance Exam (JET), CUET, or university-specific entrance exams
  • Some institutes may also conduct auditions

After Graduation (Advanced Specialisation & Masters)

Courses:

  • M.A. in Theatre / Acting
  • PG Diploma in Acting
  • Diploma in Acting from National School of Drama (NSD)

Duration: 2 – 3 years

Eligibility: Graduation in any field (theatre experience may be required for some institutes)

Minimum Marks: Around 50% in graduation

Age Limit: Around 18 – 30 years for NSD, 21 – 30 years for FTII

Exam to Pass:

  • National Level: NSD Entrance Exam
  • Institute Level: JET (FTII / SRFTI) and auditions or interviews

(Note: Age limits, marks, and admission processes may vary depending on the institute, audition process, or university regulations.)

📝 Exam Details

FTII JET CUET NSD Entrance Exam SRFTI JET

Audition: An audition is a performance test used in the acting industry. A candidate performs a short scene, dialogue, or monologue in front of directors or casting teams. They evaluate acting ability, expressions, voice, confidence, and stage presence before selecting actors for films, theatre, or acting courses.

⭐ Note

Acting courses are not always mandatory to become an actor. Many actors learn through theatre, workshops, and real acting experience.

However, acting courses can help in developing skills such as voice control, body language, dialogue delivery, and stage confidence.

Admission to many acting institutes is based on auditions where candidates perform short scenes.

🏢 Where do Authors / Writers Work?

  • Book Author – work with publishing houses to write novels, biographies, and non-fiction books.
  • Content Writer – work in content writing agencies, startups, and digital marketing companies.
  • Journalist / Article Writer – work in newspapers, magazines, and online news platforms.
  • Copywriter – work in advertising agencies creating slogans, ads, and campaign content.
  • Script Writer – work in film, television, and web series production companies.
  • Blog Writer – write articles for websites, blogs, and online media platforms.
  • Freelance Writer – work independently for publishers, companies, and online platforms.

🌍 Global Opportunities

  • International Publishing Houses – publish books and novels for readers around the world.
  • Global Media Companies – write articles, reports, and digital content for international audiences.
  • International Advertising Agencies – create marketing content and campaign scripts for global brands.
  • Film & Television Industry – write scripts and stories for international movies and shows.
  • Online Content Platforms – write blogs and articles for global websites and digital platforms.
  • Freelance International Projects – work remotely with clients and publishers from different countries.

⭐ Career Insight

Many successful actors begin their careers with theatre performances, short films, or small roles in television and advertisements.

Regular auditions, acting practice, and networking with directors, casting teams, and production houses are important for finding opportunities.
