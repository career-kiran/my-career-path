🌍 Geological Survey of India (GSI)

🌍 What is Geological Survey of India?

The Geological Survey of India (GSI) is a government organisation that studies the earth, rocks, minerals, and natural resources of India.

It works under the Ministry of Mines.

🛡️ What Do They Do?

  • Study rocks, soil, and minerals across India.
  • Search for important mineral resources.
  • Conduct studies on lands in different regions.
  • Provide scientific data for development projects.

💖 You May Enjoy This Path If You

  • Are interested in earth science and natural resources.
  • Enjoy fieldwork and traveling to different places.
  • Like studying maps and landscapes.
  • Are curious about how the earth is formed.
  • Want to work in a scientific government organisation.

📍 Real Life Examples

  • A geologist travels to a remote area to study different types of rocks.
  • A scientist collects soil and rock samples for testing.
  • An officer prepares maps for a mining project.
  • They conduct studies on land before starting a construction project.

🎯 How to get a job in Geological Survey of India (GSI)

🎓 Jobs After 12th

Ordinary Grade Driver

  • Eligibility: 10th pass with a valid driving license (LMV/HMV as required).
  • Minimum Marks: As mentioned in the official recruitment notification.
  • Age Limit: Usually 18 – 27 years (relaxation as per government rules).
  • Selection: Written Examination conducted by SSC followed by Driving Skill Test.

Multi-Tasking Staff (MTS)

  • Eligibility: 10th pass from a recognised board.
  • Minimum Marks: No fixed percentage; selection is competitive.
  • Age Limit: Usually 18 – 25/27 years (as per notification).
  • Selection: Computer Based Test (MCQ format) conducted by SSC.

🛠️ Roles & Responsibilities (After 12th)

Ordinary Grade Driver

  • Drive official vehicles for field surveys and office work.
  • Transport officers and staff to survey locations.
  • Maintain vehicle condition.
  • Assist during field visits when required.

Multi-Tasking Staff (MTS)

  • Assist in office and field-related basic tasks.
  • Handle files, documents, and basic record keeping.
  • Support research teams when needed.
  • Perform general maintenance and support duties.

📘 Exam Details (After 12th)

Note:

The Geological Survey of India (GSI) is not directly conducting the exam.

Instead, the recruitment exam is organised by the Staff Selection Commission (SSC) on behalf of GSI.

Ordinary Grade Driver

  • Written Examination (SSC): Objective-type (MCQ) test covering General Intelligence, General Awareness, and basic knowledge.
  • Driving Skill Test: Practical test to check driving ability and vehicle handling.
  • SSC – Staff Selection Commission: A central government body that conducts recruitment exams for various ministries and departments of the Government of India.

Multi-Tasking Staff (MTS)

  • Computer Based Test (SSC): MCQ-based online exam covering General Intelligence, Numerical Aptitude, General Awareness, and English.
  • SSC – Staff Selection Commission: Conducts national-level recruitment exams for central government support and clerical posts.

📌 Selection Process (After 12th)

Ordinary Grade Driver

Step 1 Apply online through the Staff Selection Commission (SSC) website when notification is released.
Step 2 Appear for the Written Examination (MCQ format).
Step 3 Qualified candidates attend the Driving Skill Test.
Step 4 Final merit list prepared based on exam and skill test performance.

Multi-Tasking Staff (MTS)

Step 1 Apply online through the SSC website when notification is released.
Step 2 Appear for the Computer Based Test (MCQ format).
Step 3 Final merit list prepared based on exam performance.

🎓 Jobs After Graduation

Geologist (Group A)

  • Eligibility: Master’s Degree in Geology / Applied Geology or related subject from a recognised university.
  • Minimum Marks: As specified in UPSC notification.
  • Age Limit: Usually 21 – 32 years (relaxation as per government rules).
  • Selection: UPSC Combined Geo-Scientist Examination (Written + Interview).

Geophysicist

  • Eligibility: Master’s Degree in Geophysics / Applied Geophysics or related subject.
  • Minimum Marks: As per UPSC notification.
  • Age Limit: Usually 21 – 32 years.
  • Selection: UPSC Combined Geo-Scientist Examination.

Chemist (Group A)

  • Eligibility: Master’s Degree in Chemistry / Applied Chemistry.
  • Minimum Marks: As specified in notification.
  • Age Limit: Usually 21 – 32 years.
  • Selection: UPSC Combined Geo-Scientist Examination.

Technical Assistant / Scientific Assistant

  • Eligibility: Bachelor’s Degree in Geology / Science / Engineering (as specified in notification).
  • Minimum Marks: As mentioned in official recruitment notification.
  • Age Limit: Usually up to 30 years (varies by recruitment).
  • Selection: Written Examination (often through SSC) and/or Interview.

🛠️ Roles & Responsibilities (After Graduation)

Geologist (Group A)

  • Study rock formations and mineral deposits.
  • Conduct field surveys in different regions.
  • Collect and analyze soil and rock samples.
  • Prepare maps and technical reports.

Geophysicist

  • Study the physical properties of the earth.
  • Use scientific instruments to collect underground data.
  • Analyze subsurface structures.
  • Support mineral and resource exploration projects.

Chemist (Group A)

  • Analyze mineral and rock samples in laboratories.
  • Conduct chemical tests on geological materials.
  • Prepare laboratory reports based on findings.
  • Assist in scientific research activities.

Technical Assistant / Scientific Assistant

  • Assist scientists during field surveys and lab work.
  • Collect and record geological data.
  • Maintain instruments and equipment.
  • Support preparation of reports and documentation.

📘 Exam Details (After Graduation)

Note:

Most Group A scientific officer posts in the Geological Survey of India (GSI) are filled through the UPSC Combined Geo-Scientist Examination.

Candidates must apply through the official UPSC website when notification is released.

Geologist (Group A)

  • UPSC Combined Geo-Scientist Examination: National-level examination conducted by UPSC.
  • Written Examination: Objective-type (MCQ) papers based on Geology subjects.
  • Interview: Personality test to assess subject knowledge and suitability.

Geophysicist

  • UPSC Combined Geo-Scientist Examination: Conducted by UPSC for recruitment to GSI and other departments.
  • Written Examination: MCQ-based papers covering Geophysics subjects.
  • Interview: Assessment of technical knowledge and understanding of the field.

Chemist (Group A)

  • UPSC Combined Geo-Scientist Examination: National-level recruitment exam.
  • Written Examination: MCQ-based papers covering Chemistry subjects.
  • Interview: Evaluation of subject knowledge and job suitability.

Technical Assistant / Scientific Assistant

  • Written Examination: Computer Based Test (MCQ format) conducted through SSC or department notification (if applicable).
  • Interview / Skill Test: May be conducted depending on recruitment notification.

📌 Selection Process (After Graduation)

Geologist / Geophysicist / Chemist (Group A)

Step 1 Apply online through the official UPSC website when the Combined Geo-Scientist Examination notification is released.
Step 2 Appear for the Written Examination (MCQ-based papers).
Step 3 Qualified candidates attend the Interview conducted by UPSC.
Step 4 Final merit list prepared based on written exam and interview performance.

Technical Assistant / Scientific Assistant

Step 1 Apply online through SSC or official recruitment notification (if released).
Step 2 Appear for the Computer Based Test (MCQ format).
Step 3 Attend Skill Test / Interview (if applicable).
Step 4 Final selection based on overall performance.

⚠ Important

  • Most scientific officer posts in GSI are filled through the UPSC Combined Geo-Scientist Examination.
  • Support staff posts are usually recruited through the Staff Selection Commission (SSC).
  • Vacancies are not released every year and vacancy for a job is called based on government requirements.
  • Candidates must carefully read the official recruitment notification before applying.
