UCEED (Undergraduate Common Entrance Examination for Design)

UCEED is a national-level entrance examination for admission to Bachelor of Design (B.Des) programmes offered by participating institutes.

A student interested in design writes the UCEED examination and receives a score.

The student can use this score to apply for B.Des programmes in institutes that accept UCEED scores for admission.

Courses may include: Bachelor of Design (B.Des), Product Design, Interaction Design (UI/UX), Communication Design, Animation Design, Industrial Design, and other design-related programmes.

Conducted by: Indian Institute of Technology Bombay (IIT Bombay).

📌 Quick Facts

Eligibility: 12th Pass (Any stream: Science, Commerce, Arts/Humanities)

Mode: Computer-Based Test (Part A) and Pen-and-Paper Drawing Test (Part B)

Questions: Fixed distribution (57 Objective questions in Part A + 2 Subjective questions in Part B)

Frequency: Conducted once a year (Usually in January)

Here is the quick eligibility checklist for candidates planning to appear for the UCEED examination:

  • Qualification:
    • Must have passed or be appearing for Class XII (10+2) or an equivalent examination.
    • Students from any stream (Science, Commerce, Arts, or Humanities) are eligible.
  • Age:
    • General / OBC-NCL / EWS : candidates must be born on or after the prescribed date announced in the official notification.(usually below 25 years)
    • SC / ST / PwD :candidates receive age relaxation as per UCEED rules.(usually 5 years)
  • Minimum Marks:
    • No minimum percentage in Class 12 is required to appear for UCEED.
    • Candidates must successfully pass Class 12 examination.
  • Attempts:
    • A candidate can attempt UCEED a maximum of two times.
    • The attempts must be in consecutive years.
    • Example: If a student appears for UCEED in 2026, they can make their second attempt in 2027. They cannot skip a year and appear again in 2028.

📝 Exam Pattern & Marking Scheme

Mode of Examination: Computer-Based Test (CBT) for Part A and Pen-and-Paper Assessment for Part B

Duration: 3 Hours (Part A: 2 Hours | Part B: 1 Hour)

Medium: English

Question Paper: One paper split into Part A and Part B (Total Marks: 300)

Part A: Computer-Based Test (200 Marks)

  • Numerical Answer Type (NAT):
    • Questions: 14
    • Marking Scheme: +4 Marks for every correct answer.
    • Negative Marking: 0 (No negative marking).
  • Multiple Select Questions (MSQ):
    • Questions: 15
    • Marking Scheme: Full +4 Marks if all correct choices are selected. Gets partial marks (+3, +2, or +1)also depending on how many correct answers.
    • Negative Marking: -1 Mark penalty for selecting any incorrect option or wrong combination.
  • Multiple Choice Questions (MCQ):
    • Questions: 28
    • Marking Scheme: +3 Marks for every correct answer.
    • Negative Marking: -0.71 Marks deducted for every incorrect choice.

Part B: Subjective Assessment (100 Marks)

  • Sketching Question:
    • Questions: 1
    • Marking Scheme: 50 Marks (Evaluates line quality, perspective, proportions, and composition).
    • Negative Marking: 0 (No negative marking).
  • Design Aptitude Question:
    • Questions: 1
    • Marking Scheme: 50 Marks (Evaluates creative problem solving, visualization, and ideation).
    • Negative Marking: 0 (No negative marking).
Crucial Evaluation Rule: Part B answer booklets are strictly evaluated only for those candidates who successfully meet or exceed the minimum qualifying cutoff marks set for Part A first.
