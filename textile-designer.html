🧵 Textile Designer

🧠 Imagine Being a Textile Designer...

A clothing company is planning a new collection of printed cotton shirts. They want attractive patterns for the fabric, such as flowers, stripes, dots, or traditional designs seen in sarees.

They approach you for help.

You start drawing designs for the cloth. You create patterns using colours, shapes, and creative ideas.

The factory prints your designs on the fabric.

🛡️ This is the work of a Textile Designer.

💖 You may enjoy this path if you...

  • You enjoy drawing patterns or creative designs.
  • You enjoy creating designs that people can wear or use at home.
  • You are interested in fashion, fabrics, and textiles.

👉 Try This Now

Look at the clothing material of a shirt, curtain, or bedsheet in your home.

🎓 How to Become a Textile Designer

After 10th Grade

Courses: Diploma in Textile Design

Certificate in Textile Design

Duration: 6 months to 3 years

Eligibility: Passed Class 10

Minimum Marks: Usually 40% to 50%

Exam to Pass: Usually merit-based or institute entrance test

After 12th Grade

Courses: Bachelor of Design (B.Des) in Textile Design

B.Sc in Textile Design

Diploma in Textile Design

Duration: 3 to 4 years (Bachelor’s degree)

Eligibility: 10+2 from any stream

Minimum Marks: Usually around 45% to 50%

Exam to Pass: NIFT Entrance Exam, NID DAT, UCEED, or CUET

After Graduation

Courses: Master of Design (M.Des) in Textile Design

M.Sc in Textile Design

Post Graduate Diploma in Textile Design

Duration: 1 to 2 years

Eligibility: Bachelor’s degree in a relevant field

Minimum Marks: Usually around 50%

Exam to Pass: CEED, NIFT PG, NID DAT (M.Des), or institute entrance test

Note: Students can study Textile Design after 12th from any stream (Science, Commerce, or Arts).

🏢 Where do Textile Designers Work?

  • Textile Designer – in textile manufacturing companies, fashion brands, and design studios
  • Print Designer – create patterns and prints for clothes, fabrics, and home décor products
  • Home Textile Designer – design patterns for bedsheets, curtains, carpets, and upholstery
  • Fabric Developer – work with textile companies to develop new types of cloth materials
  • Freelance Textile Designer – work independently with fashion brands or textile companies

🌍 Global Opportunities

  • Textile Designer – Work with international fashion brands and textile companies.
  • Print Designer – Create fabric patterns for global clothing brands and home décor companies.
  • Home Textile Designer – Design patterns for bedsheets, carpets, and curtains sold worldwide.
  • Freelance Textile Designer – Work with clients from different countries by sharing designs online.

⭐ Career Insight

Many textile designers begin their careers by working with textile manufacturers, fabric printing companies, or fashion brands.

With experience, some designers specialize in areas like fabric printing, home textiles, or sustainable materials.
