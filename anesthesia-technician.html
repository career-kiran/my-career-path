Anesthesia Technician

An Anesthesia Technician is a healthcare professional who helps doctors give anesthesia to patients before and during surgeries.

What does an Anesthesia Technician usually do?

  • Prepare anesthesia machines and equipment before surgery
  • Assist anesthesiologists during operations
  • Check oxygen supply and anesthesia equipment
  • Clean and maintain anesthesia instruments
  • Help monitor patients during surgery

You may enjoy this career if you:

  • Are calm and focused in serious situations
  • Like working inside operation theatres
  • Can follow instructions carefully
  • Are comfortable around medical equipment
  • Want a technical support role in healthcare
Real-life example:
Before a surgery begins, the anesthesia technician prepares the anesthesia machine and checks the oxygen supply. During surgery, they assist the anesthesiologist to ensure the patient stays safely unconscious.

Courses Available

Diploma in Anesthesia Technology

  • Duration: 1 – 2 years
  • Eligibility: 12th pass (Science preferred)
  • Entrance exam: Usually not required
  • Admission: Merit-based or direct

BSc in Anesthesia Technology

  • Duration: 3 years
  • Eligibility: 12th with Physics, Chemistry, Biology
  • Entrance exam: Varies by college (CUET / state-level exams)
  • Admission: Merit-based / entrance-based

Where do Anesthesia Technicians work?

  • Hospitals
  • Operation theatres
  • Surgical centers
  • Multi-specialty hospitals
Important note:
Anesthesia Technicians work under the supervision of anesthesiologists and do not give anesthesia independently.
