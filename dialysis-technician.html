Dialysis Technician

A Dialysis Technician is a healthcare professional who helps patients having kidney problems by operating dialysis machines and supporting them during treatment.

What does a Dialysis Technician usually do?

  • Prepare and operate dialysis machines
  • Monitor patients during dialysis sessions
  • Check blood pressure and other vital signs
  • Maintain cleanliness and safety of equipment
  • Assist nurses and doctors during treatment

You may enjoy this career if you:

  • Are comfortable working closely with patients
  • Can stay calm and focused for long procedures
  • Like healthcare work with hands-on involvement
  • Are careful and follow procedures strictly
  • Are willing to work in hospitals or dialysis centers
Real-life example:
A patient with kidney problems visits a dialysis center several times a week. The dialysis technician prepares the machine, helps connect the patient safely, monitors them during treatment, and ensures everything runs smoothly until the session is completed.

Courses to become a Dialysis Technician (India)

Diploma in Dialysis Technology

Duration: 1–2 years

Eligibility: 10+2 (Science stream preferred)

Admission: Merit-based

Certificate in Dialysis Technology

Duration: 6–12 months

Eligibility: 10+2 (varies by institute)

Admission: Merit-based / institute screening

Entrance exams:
There is no single national-level entrance exam for Dialysis Technology courses. Some institutes may conduct basic tests or interviews. Admission process may vary by institute and state.

Where do Dialysis Technicians work?

  • Hospitals
  • Dialysis centers
  • Kidney care clinics
  • Multi-specialty hospitals
  • Home dialysis services (under supervision)
Important note:
Dialysis Technicians do not diagnose or prescribe treatment. They work under the supervision of doctors and nurses and play a crucial role in supporting patients with long-term kidney conditions.
