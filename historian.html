Historian

Who is a Historian?

A historian is a person who studies the past by reading old records, books, documents, and evidence to understand how people lived and what happened long ago.

What does a Historian do?

  • Studies old documents, letters, and historical records
  • Researches important events and explains their meaning
  • Writes books, articles, or research papers
  • Helps preserve historical monuments and heritage

You may enjoy this career if you:

  • Love reading about history and past events
  • Are curious about how societies changed over time
  • Enjoy research and deep thinking

Real Life Examples

  • A historian studies old freedom movement documents to write about India’s independence struggle.
  • A historian helps a museum prepare information about ancient temples and kings.

Courses & Eligibility

BA in History (3 years)

Eligibility: 12th pass (any stream)

Minimum Marks: Usually 50% (varies by college)

Admission: Merit-based in many colleges

Entrance Exams: CUET (Common University Entrance Test) for central universities

MA in History (2 years)

Eligibility: Bachelor’s degree in History or related subject

Minimum Marks: Usually 50–55%

Entrance Exams: CUET-PG, university-level entrance exams

PhD in History (3–5 years)

Eligibility: Master’s degree in History

Entrance Exams: UGC-NET, JRF, university entrance exams

Where do Historians work?

  • Assistant Professor / Lecturer – In universities and colleges
  • Research Historian – In research institutes and academic projects
  • Museum Curator – In museums like National Museum, Delhi
  • Archivist – In National Archives of India
  • Heritage Officer – In Archaeological Survey of India (ASI)

🌍 Global Opportunities

  • Museum Curator – In international museums like The British Museum (UK) or Smithsonian Institution (USA)
  • Research Historian – In universities and research institutes abroad
  • Archivist – In national archives and heritage departments
  • Cultural Heritage Consultant – For UNESCO heritage conservation projects
  • Historical Documentary Researcher – In international media and film production

Important Note

A career in history mainly grows through higher studies and research. If you are passionate about understanding the past and explaining it clearly, this can be a meaningful career.
