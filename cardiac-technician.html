ECG / Cardiac Technician

An ECG / Cardiac Technician is a healthcare professional who performs heart-related tests such as ECG to help doctors diagnose and monitor heart conditions.

What does an ECG / Cardiac Technician usually do?

  • Perform ECG tests to record heart activity
  • Prepare patients for cardiac tests
  • Operate ECG and related cardiac testing machines
  • Monitor patient condition during tests
  • Assist doctors in diagnosing heart problems

You may enjoy this career if you:

  • Like healthcare work and patient interaction
  • Pay attention to detail
  • Are comfortable working with medical machines
  • Can follow instructions carefully
  • Prefer hospital or clinic environments
Real-life example:
A patient visits a hospital with chest discomfort. The ECG technician prepares the patient, conducts an ECG test using a computer-based machine, and provides the results to the doctor for further diagnosis.

Courses to become an ECG / Cardiac Technician

Diploma in ECG / Cardiac Technology

Duration: 1–2 years

Eligibility: 10+2 (Science preferred)

Admission: Merit-based / Entrance-based

BSc in Cardiac Care Technology

Duration: 3 years

Eligibility: 10+2 with Science

Admission: Merit-based / Entrance-based

Where do ECG / Cardiac Technicians work?

  • Hospitals
  • Cardiology departments
  • Diagnostic centers
  • Heart clinics
  • Health check-up centers
