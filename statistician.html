Statistician

Who is a Statistician?

A statistician is a person who studies numbers and data to help people understand information and make better decisions. They collect data, organize it, and find patterns to solve real-life problems.

You may enjoy this career if:

  • You like working with numbers
  • You enjoy finding patterns and trends
  • You like solving problems using logic

Real Life Examples (India)

  • A statistician studies health data to help control diseases.
  • A statistician analyzes customer data to improve business sales.
  • A statistician uses data to help the government plan for the future.

Courses to Become a Statistician

Bachelor’s Degree (3 years)

  • BSc in Statistics
  • BSc in Mathematics & Statistics
  • BSc in Data Science
  • BStat (Bachelor of Statistics – Indian Statistical Institute)

Eligibility: 12th with Mathematics

Minimum Marks: Usually 50–60% (varies by college)

Admission: Merit-based in many colleges. Some institutes conduct entrance exams (ISI entrance, CUET, state-level exams).

Master’s Degree (2 years)

  • MSc in Statistics
  • MSc in Applied Statistics
  • MSc in Data Science
  • MStat (Master of Statistics – Indian Statistical Institute)

Eligibility: Bachelor’s degree in Statistics / Mathematics / related field

Entrance Exams: CUET-PG (Common University Entrance Test – Postgraduate), ISI entrance exam, IIT JAM (Joint Admission Test for Masters), university-level exams

Postgraduate Diploma (Optional – 1 year)

  • PG Diploma in Data Science
  • PG Diploma in Business Analytics
  • PG Diploma in Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning

Where Do Statisticians Work?

  • Ministry of Statistics and Programme Implementation (MoSPI) – Planning and managing national statistics.
  • Reserve Bank of India (RBI) – Analyzing economic and financial data.
  • National Sample Survey Office (NSSO) – Conducting surveys and studying population data.
  • Indian Statistical Institute (ISI) – Research and advanced data science work.
  • Council of Scientific and Industrial Research (CSIR) – Working on scientific research projects.
  • Indian Statistical Service (ISS) – As government statistical officers through the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) examination.
  • Banks and Financial Institutions – As financial risk and data analysts.
  • IT Companies – As data analysts and data scientists.
  • Insurance Companies – In actuarial and risk analysis roles.
  • Healthcare Sector – As biostatisticians analyzing medical data.
  • Universities and Colleges – Teaching and conducting research.

Important Note

Statistics is one of the most important skills in today’s data-driven world. With strong knowledge of statistics and data analysis, you can work in finance, technology, healthcare, research, and government sectors.
