Pharmacist

A pharmacist prepares, dispenses, and explains medicines prescribed by doctors. They help people use medicines safely and correctly, and guide patients about dosage, side effects, and proper use.

You may enjoy this career if you…

  • Are interested in medicines and how they work
  • Like helping people understand their treatment
  • Are careful, responsible, and detail-oriented
  • Prefer a healthcare role that is less physically demanding

Courses to become a Pharmacist (India)

Bachelor of Pharmacy (B.Pharm)

Duration: 4 years

Eligibility:

  • Completed 12th standard
  • Physics and Chemistry compulsory
  • Biology or Mathematics required
  • Minimum marks: 50–60%

Admission process: Entrance exam or merit-based

Entrance exam Some colleges admit students based on state-level or university-level entrance exams, while others may use merit-based admission.

Doctor of Pharmacy (Pharm.D)

Duration: 6 years (5 years study + 1 year internship)

Eligibility:

  • Completed 12th standard
  • Physics and Chemistry compulsory
  • Biology or Mathematics required
  • Minimum marks: 50–60%

Admission process: Mostly merit-based or university entrance exam

About Pharm.D Pharm.D focuses on clinical pharmacy and patient care. Graduates often work closely with doctors in hospitals.

Diploma in Pharmacy (D.Pharm)

Duration: 2 years + practical training

Eligibility:

  • Completed 12th standard
  • Physics and Chemistry required
  • Biology or Mathematics required (varies by state)
  • Minimum marks: 50%

Admission process: Mostly merit-based

Diploma route D.Pharm holders usually work as pharmacists in medical shops, hospitals, and clinics.

Master of Pharmacy (M.Pharm)

Duration: 2 years

Eligibility:

  • Completed B.Pharm
  • Minimum marks: 50–55%

Admission process: Entrance exam: GPAT(Graduate Pharamacy Aptitude Test) or university-level entrance exam

Focus of this course:
Advanced study in areas such as pharmacology, pharmaceutics, pharmaceutical analysis, or quality assurance.

Where do Pharmacists work?

  • Medical shops and pharmacies
  • Hospitals and clinics
  • Pharmaceutical companies
  • Drug manufacturing units
  • Research and quality control labs

Important note

To legally work as a pharmacist in India, registration with the State Pharmacy Council is required after completing D.Pharm, B.Pharm, or Pharm.D.
