🌿 State Pollution Control Boards (SPCBs)

🌿 What are State Pollution Control Boards?

State Pollution Control Boards (SPCBs) are state-level government bodies responsible for controlling pollution within their respective states.

They work under the guidance of the Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB).

SPCBs make sure that industries and organisations follow environmental laws and pollution control standards.

🛡️ What Do They Do?

  • Check pollution levels in air and water across the state.
  • Give permission to industries to operate under pollution rules.
  • Inspect factories to make sure they follow environmental laws.
  • Take action if any industry causes pollution.
  • Collect and test samples from air, water, and soil.

💖 You May Enjoy This Path If You

  • Care about protecting the environment.
  • Are interested in pollution control and public health.
  • Like science, field visits, and inspection work.
  • Want a government job with social impact.
  • Enjoy working to improve your local community.

📍 Real Life Examples

  • An SPCB officer visits a factory to check whether it is following pollution control rules.
  • A scientist tests river water samples to check for harmful chemicals.
  • An engineer monitors air quality levels in major cities of the state.
  • A board official takes action against industries causing excessive pollution.

🎓 Jobs After 12th

Junior Laboratory Assistant

  • Eligibility: 12th pass with Science subjects (Physics, Chemistry, Biology) or relevant laboratory diploma (if specified).
  • Minimum Marks: As mentioned in the respective state recruitment notification.
  • Age Limit: Usually 18 – 30 years (relaxation as per state government rules).
  • Selection: Computer Based Test (MCQ) followed by Practical / Skill Test (if applicable).

Laboratory Technician

  • Eligibility: 12th pass in Science or Diploma in Laboratory Technology.
  • Minimum Marks: As specified in the official notification.
  • Age Limit: Generally 18 – 30 years (varies by state).
  • Selection: Computer Based Test (MCQ) and Practical Test (if required).

Multi-Tasking Staff (MTS) / Field Attendant

  • Eligibility: 10th or 12th pass from a recognised board.
  • Minimum Marks: No fixed percentage; selection is competitive.
  • Age Limit: Usually 18 – 27/30 years (varies by state rules).
  • Selection: Computer Based Test (MCQ).

🛠️ Roles & Responsibilities (After 12th)

Junior Laboratory Assistant

  • Collect air, water, and soil samples for testing.
  • Assist senior scientists during laboratory experiments.
  • Clean and maintain laboratory equipment.
  • Record test results and maintain basic lab records.

Laboratory Technician

  • Perform basic laboratory tests on samples.
  • Operate testing machines and equipment.
  • Maintain laboratory safety and cleanliness.
  • Prepare simple reports based on test results.

Multi-Tasking Staff (MTS) / Field Attendant

  • Support daily office and field activities.
  • Assist during inspections and sample collection.
  • Carry files, documents, and equipment when required.
  • Help maintain office and laboratory spaces.

📘 Exam Details (After 12th)

Junior Laboratory Assistant

  • Computer Based Test (CBT): Online exam with Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) covering General Knowledge, Science subjects, and basic Mathematics.
  • Practical / Skill Test: Basic laboratory skill assessment (if mentioned in notification).

Laboratory Technician

  • Computer Based Test (CBT): MCQ-based exam covering Science subjects and General Awareness.
  • Practical Test: Hands-on laboratory test to check technical skills (if applicable).

Multi-Tasking Staff (MTS) / Field Attendant

  • Computer Based Test (CBT): MCQ-based exam covering General Awareness, Reasoning, and basic aptitude.

📌 Selection Process (After 12th)

Junior Laboratory Assistant

Step 2 Appear for the Computer Based Test (MCQ format).
Step 3 Attend Practical / Skill Test (if required).
Step 4 Final merit list prepared based on overall performance.

Laboratory Technician

Step 1 Submit online application through the official State Board website.
Step 2 Appear for the Computer Based Test (MCQ format).
Step 3 Attend Practical Test (if applicable).
Step 4 Final selection based on exam performance.

Multi-Tasking Staff (MTS) / Field Attendant

Step 3 Final merit list prepared based on exam performance.

Junior Scientific Officer (JSO) / Assistant Scientist

  • Eligibility: M.Sc. in Chemistry, Environmental Science, Microbiology or related science subject.
  • Minimum Marks: As specified in the official notification.
  • Age Limit: Usually 21 – 30 years (relaxation applicable).
  • Selection: Written Examination (MCQs) and/or Interview.

Junior Assistant / Assistant Section Officer (ASO)

  • Minimum Marks: As mentioned in the respective state notification.

Junior Accountant

Assistant Environmental Engineer (AEE)

  • Prepare technical reports on environmental compliance.

Junior Assistant / Assistant Section Officer (ASO)

Junior Accountant

  • Maintain financial records and accounts.
  • Process bills, payments, and vouchers.
  • Prepare basic financial statements and reports.
  • Assist in budgeting and audit-related work.

Junior Scientific Officer (JSO) / Assistant Scientist
