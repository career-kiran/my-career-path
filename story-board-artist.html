🎬 Story Board artist

🧠 Imagine This...

"You are watching a movie, animation, or advertisement.

Every scene appears in a specific order to tell the story.

Before the movie or animation is created, someone first draws each scene.

These drawings help directors, animators, and camera teams understand how the story will look on screen.

That person is called a Storyboard Artist.

Storyboard artists create drawings that show how scenes will appear in movies, animation, advertisements, and videos."

❤️ You May Enjoy This Path If You...

  • Enjoy drawing characters or scenes.
  • Like telling stories through pictures or sketches.
  • Like visualizing how a story should appear on screen.

👉 Try This Now

Open a short movie scene, animation clip, or advertisement on YouTube.
↓
Watch how the scene changes from one shot to another.
↓
Notice how one scene is connected to other.
↓
Imagine someone first drawing these scenes before the video was created.
↓
Now think you are doing the same, drawing each scene of a story before it becomes a video.
↓
If this excites you, you may enjoy becoming a Storyboard Artist.

🔧 Want to Try This Yourself?

  • Try drawing a short story using paper and pencil or digital tools like Storyboarder, Photoshop, or Procreate.
  • Search on YouTube for tutorials like "storyboarding for beginners" or "how to create a storyboard".
  • Start by drawing 3–4 simple frames that show what happens first, next, and last in a scene.
  • With practice, you can create storyboards for short films, animations, or advertisements.

🎓 How to Become a Storyboard Artist

After 10th Standard (Art & Visual Foundations)

Courses:

  • Diploma in Commercial Art
  • Certificate in Storyboarding & Layout
  • Diploma in Fine Arts (DFA)

Duration: 6 months – 1 year

Eligibility: 10th pass from any recognised board

Minimum Marks: Usually 45% – 50%

Age Limit: Around 14 – 15 years minimum

Exam to Pass: Usually direct admission. Some institutes may review your sketchbook or conduct a basic drawing test.

After 12th Standard (Professional Degrees)

Courses:

  • B.Des in Animation
  • BFA in Applied Arts
  • BA in Film & Television Production

Duration: 3 – 4 years

Eligibility: 12th pass in any stream (Science, Commerce, or Arts)

Minimum Marks: Usually around 50% in 12th

Age Limit: Some government institutes may have an age limit around 17 – 23 years

Exam to Pass:

  • National Design Institutes: UCEED, NID DAT, NIFT
  • Central Universities: CUET
  • Private Design Colleges: SEED, AIEED

After Graduation (Specialisation / Masters)

Courses:

  • M.Des in Animation Film Design
  • M.A in Film Production
  • PG Diploma in Storyboarding & Pre-production

Duration: 1 – 2 years

Eligibility: Graduation in any field (BFA, B.Des, or related fields preferred)

Minimum Marks: Usually around 50% in graduation

Age Limit: Usually no upper age limit

Exam to Pass:

  • Design Institutes: CEED, NID DAT
  • Film Schools: JET (FTII / SRFTI)

⭐ Note

Students learn skills like drawing characters, planning camera angles, creating scene sequences, and understanding storytelling for films and animation.

In this career, drawing skills and a strong portfolio are very important. Many studios look at the quality of your storyboard work rather than only your degree.

🏢 Where Do Storyboard Artists Work?

  • Storyboard Artist – work in animation studios planning scenes for animated movies and series.
  • Film Storyboard Artist – work in film production companies creating scene sketches before shooting begins.
  • Advertising Storyboard Artist – work in advertising agencies planning visuals for commercials and promotional videos.
  • Pre-visualization Artist – work with directors and production teams to plan camera angles and scene sequences.
  • Freelance Storyboard Artist – work independently creating storyboards for films, animation projects, or advertisements.

🌍 Global Opportunities

  • International Animation Studios – create storyboards for animated movies, series, and streaming content.
  • Global Film Production Companies – plan scenes and visual sequences before movie shooting begins.
  • Advertising Agencies – design storyboards for international advertisements and promotional videos.
  • Game Development Studios – help plan story sequences and cinematic scenes used in video games.
  • Freelance Projects – many storyboard artists work remotely with international clients on films, animation, or advertising projects.

⭐ Career Insight

Storyboard artists create drawings that show how scenes will appear before a movie, animation, or advertisement is produced.

They help directors and animators plan the sequence of scenes and camera angles in a story.

With practice and a strong collection of storyboard sketches, artists can work in animation studios, film production companies, or advertising agencies.
