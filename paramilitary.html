🪖 What are Paramilitary Forces?

Paramilitary forces are security forces that protect the country internally.

They are different from the Army. The Army protects the country from external threats, while paramilitary forces mainly handle internal security.

Also called Central Armed Police Forces (CAPF)

🇮🇳 Major Paramilitary Forces in India

  • CRPF – Central Reserve Police Force
  • BSF – Border Security Force
  • CISF – Central Industrial Security Force
  • ITBP – Indo-Tibetan Border Police
  • SSB – Sashastra Seema Bal

Each of these forces has a specific role, but all of them focus on internal security, border protection, and maintaining law and order.

🛡 What Do They Do?

  • Guard international borders
  • Maintain internal security
  • Control riots and law & order situations
  • Assist during disasters and emergencies
  • Participate in anti-terror operations

💖 You May Enjoy This Path If You:

  • are ready to work in challenging environments
  • want to contribute to national security
  • are comfortable working in different parts of the country

🎓 Jobs After 10th

Constable (General Duty)

Eligibility: 10th pass

Age Limit: 18 – 23 years

Exam to Pass: SSC GD

Forces Include: CRPF, BSF, CISF, ITBP, SSB

Constable: Maintain law and order, guard borders, and assist in security operations.

🎓 Jobs After 12th

Head Constable

Eligibility: 12th pass

Age Limit: 18 – 25 years

Exam to Pass: Independent exams conducted by the respective force

Assistant Sub-Inspector (ASI)

Eligibility: 12th pass (some posts may require specific skills)

Age Limit: 18 – 25 years

Exam to Pass: Independent exams conducted by the respective force

Head Constable: Assist in administrative work, communication duties, and support field operations.

Assistant Sub-Inspector: Supervise constables and assist in operational and security duties.

ℹ Note:Independent exams conducted by respective force - means an official job announcement released by the respective force(eg.,BSF,CRPF,CISF) on their website.

🎓 Jobs After Graduation

Assistant Commandant (Officer Level)

Eligibility: Bachelor’s degree (any stream)

Age Limit: 20 – 25 years

Exam to Pass: UPSC CAPF

Sub-Inspector (SI)

Eligibility: Bachelor’s degree (any stream)

Age Limit: 20 – 25 years

Exam to Pass: SSC CPO

Technical / Medical Officer

Eligibility: Engineering / Medical degree (as required)

Age Limit: Varies by post

Exam to Pass: Recruitment notification released by the respective force

Assistant Commandant: Lead and manage security operations and supervise officers and constables.

Sub-Inspector: Supervise constables and assist in maintaining law and order.

Technical/Medical Officer: Handle technical, engineering, or medical responsibilities within the force.

📝 Exam Full Forms

  • SSC – Staff Selection Commission
  • SSC GD – Staff Selection Commission General Duty
  • SSC CPO – Central Police Organization Exam
  • UPSC – Union Public Service Commission
  • CAPF – Central Armed Police Forces
  • PET – Physical Efficiency Test
  • PST – Physical Standard Test

📌 Selection Process

Selection for paramilitary forces generally includes the following stages:

  • 1) Written Examination: Objective-type test covering General Knowledge, Reasoning, Math, and English.
  • 2) Physical Efficiency Test (PET): Running and physical endurance tests.
  • 3) Physical Standard Test (PST): Height, chest measurement, and other physical standards.
  • 4) Medical Examination: Complete medical check to ensure fitness.
  • 5) Document Verification: Checking educational and identity documents.
