AIMA UGAT (Under Graduate Aptitude Test)

AIMA UGAT is a national-level entrance examination conducted by the All India Management Association (AIMA)for admission to various undergraduate courses offered by participating colleges across India.

UGAT scores are accepted by many colleges for admission to programmes such as BBA, BCA, BHM, B.Com, and Integrated MBA (IMBA).

Conducted by: All India Management Association (AIMA).

📌 Quick Facts

Eligibility: Class 12 (10+2) or equivalent examination from a recognized board.

Exam Pattern: Mostly an Offline (Paper-Based Test) consisting of Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs).

Mode: Offline (Paper-Based Test).

Duration: 120 Minutes (2 Hours).

Frequency: Conducted once a year (usually in June).

Qualification:

  • Must have passed or be appearing for the Class 12 (10+2) or equivalent examination from a recognized board.
  • Candidates appearing for their Class 12 board examinations are also eligible to apply, provided they meet the admission requirements after the results are declared.

Age:

  • There is no upper or lower age limit for appearing in the AIMA UGAT examination.

Minimum Marks:

  • For the Exam: There is no minimum percentage required to appear for the AIMA UGAT examination.
  • For Admission: The minimum Class 12 marks required vary depending on the college and the programme. Always check the eligibility criteria of the institute you wish to apply to.

Attempts:

  • There is no limit on the number of times you can appear for the AIMA UGAT examination.

Nationality:

  • Indian and foreign candidates can apply, subject to the admission rules of the participating institutes.

Mode of Examination: Mostly Offline (Paper-Based Test).

Question Type: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs).

Duration: 120 Minutes (2 Hours).

Frequency: Conducted once a year (usually in June).

Total Questions: 130 Questions (for BBA, BCA, B.Com, and Integrated MBA).

Sections:

  • English Language: 40 Questions.
  • Numerical & Data Analysis: 30 Questions.
  • Reasoning & Intelligence: 30 Questions.
  • General Knowledge: 30 Questions.

Marking Scheme:

  • Correct Answer: +1 Mark.
  • Incorrect Answer: No negative marking.
  • Unattempted Question: No marks are awarded or deducted.

Important: The exam pattern may vary slightly depending on the course you are applying for. For example, candidates applying for BHM (Bachelor of Hotel Management) may have an additional section related to the hospitality field.

Students who qualify through the AIMA UGAT (Under Graduate Aptitude Test) can apply for various undergraduate programmes offered by participating colleges across India.

Undergraduate Courses:

  • BBA (Bachelor of Business Administration)
  • BCA (Bachelor of Computer Applications)
  • B.Com (Bachelor of Commerce)
  • BHM (Bachelor of Hotel Management)
  • Integrated MBA (IMBA)

Important Note:

Qualifying for the AIMA UGAT does not guarantee admission. Candidates must meet the eligibility criteria of the respective college and course.

Admission is based on the AIMA UGAT score and the admission process prescribed by the participating institute.

Some colleges may also conduct Personal Interviews (PI), Group Discussions (GD), or other selection rounds.
