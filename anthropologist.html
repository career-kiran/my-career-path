Anthropologist

Who is an Anthropologist?

An anthropologist studies humans, their cultures, traditions, and how societies develop over time. They try to understand how people live, think, and interact in different parts of the world.

What Do Anthropologists Do?

  • Study different cultures and communities.
  • Research human behavior and social systems.
  • Collect and analyze data through fieldwork.
  • Help organizations understand social impact.

You May Enjoy This Career If You:

  • Are curious about different cultures and lifestyles.
  • Enjoy research and fieldwork.
  • Like understanding how society works.

Real Life Examples

  • An anthropologist studies tribal communities to understand their lifestyle and traditions.
  • A researcher studies how a new government scheme affects rural communities.

Courses & Eligibility

BA / BSc in Anthropology

Duration: 3 years

Eligibility: 10+2 (Minimum 50% marks – may vary by college)

Admission: Merit-based or CUET (Common University Entrance Test) in central universities

MA / MSc in Anthropology

Duration: 2 years

Eligibility: Bachelor’s degree in Anthropology or related subject

Admission: University entrance exams or merit-based

PhD in Anthropology

Duration: 3–5 years

Eligibility: Master’s degree

Entrance Exam: UGC-NET (University Grants Commission – National Eligibility Test)

Where do Anthropologists work?

  • Research Assistant – In universities and research institutes
  • Cultural Researcher – In NGOs and social development organizations
  • Museum Curator – In museums and heritage departments
  • Field Researcher – In tribal and rural development projects
  • Policy Researcher – In government social research departments

🌍 Global Opportunities

  • Corporate Ethnographer – In tech companies studying user behavior
  • Museum Curator – In international museums
  • Forensic Anthropologist – Assisting legal agencies abroad
  • NGO/Development Specialist – In international development projects

Important Note

Anthropology careers often grow through higher studies and research. Fieldwork and specialization improve career opportunities.
