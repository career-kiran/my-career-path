📝 How to Get Public Sector Bank Jobs

In India, appointment for most public sector banks are conducted by three main bodies:

1️⃣ Institute of Banking Personnel Selection (IBPS)

IBPS conducts exams for many public sector banks like Punjab National Bank (PNB), Bank of Baroda, Canara Bank, Union Bank of India and others.

Major IBPS Exams:

  • IBPS PO – For Probationary Officers (Bank Managers in training)
  • IBPS Clerk – For Clerical/Assistant posts
  • IBPS SO – For Specialist Officers (IT Officer, HR Officer, Law Officer, etc.)
  • IBPS RRB – For Regional Rural Banks

2️⃣ State Bank of India (SBI)

Major SBI Exams:

  • SBI PO - For entry level management post
  • SBI Clerk - For customer support and sales posts
  • SBI SO - For specialist officers (IT officers, Law officers)

3️⃣ Reserve Bank of India (RBI)

Major RBI Exams:

  • RBI Grade B Officer - For mangager post
  • RBI Assistant - For clerical and administrative support

🎓 Eligibility

  • Minimum Qualification: Bachelor’s degree in any stream.
  • Age Limit: Usually 20–30 years (varies by exam).
  • Age relaxation is available for reserved categories as per government rules.

🧠 General Exam Process

Most public sector bank exams happen in 3 stages:

  • Preliminary Exam (Prelims): Basic online test to shortlist candidates.
  • Main Exam (Mains): More detailed exam with additional subjects.
  • Interview: Only for Officer posts (like PO, Grade B). Clerikal posts usually do not have an interview.

💼 Jobs You can Get

If you clear public sector bank exams, you can get posts such as:

🏦 Through IBPS (For Most Public Sector Banks)

  • Probationary Officer (PO)
  • Clerk / Customer Service Associate
  • Specialist Officer (IT, HR, Marketing, Law, etc.)
  • Regional Rural Bank (RRB) Officer Scale I, II, III
  • RRB Office Assistant

RRB (Regional Rural Bank):

Public sector banks that mainly work in villages and small towns.

They provide loans and banking services to farmers and local businesses.

🏛 Through SBI (State Bank of India – Conducts Its Own Exams)

  • SBI Probationary Officer (SBI PO)
  • SBI Clerk (Junior Associate)
  • SBI Specialist Officer

🏦 Through RBI (Reserve Bank of India)

  • RBI Grade B Officer
  • RBI Assistant
  • RBI Office Attendant

📌 Important Note

IBPS, SBI, and RBI release official notifications every year. Always check the official website for exact eligibility, syllabus, and exam dates.
