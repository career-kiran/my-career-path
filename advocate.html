⚖️ Lawyer / Advocate

👤 Who is a Lawyer / Advocate?

A lawyer (also called an advocate) is a legal professional who helps people solve problems legally. They guide clients about laws, represent them in court, and protect their legal rights.

🛡️ What does a Lawyer do?

  • Gives legal advice to clients
  • Represents people in court
  • Prepares legal documents and contracts
  • Helps solve problems legally
  • Supports clients in criminal, civil, or business cases

💖 You may enjoy this profession if:

  • You like solving people’s problems
  • You are interested in learning about different laws
  • You enjoy debate and discussion
  • You can think logically
  • You can speak confidently

💡 Real Life Examples

  • A lawyer helps a family solve a property issue legally.
  • A criminal lawyer defends a person accused in court.
  • A corporate lawyer prepares legal contracts for a company.

📚 How to Become a Lawyer?

1️⃣ Integrated Law Degree (After 12th)

Course: BA LLB / BBA LLB / BCom LLB (5 Years)

Duration: 5 Years

Eligibility: 12th pass in any stream

Minimum Marks: Usually 45–50% (may vary by category & college)

Admission: Entrance exams like CLAT, AILET, LSAT–India, or university-level exams

2️⃣ 3-Year LLB (After Graduation)

Course: LLB

Duration: 3 Years

Eligibility: Bachelor’s degree in any subject

Minimum Marks: Usually 45–50% (may vary by college)

Admission: Entrance exams like CLAT PG, LSAT–India, or university-level exams

📜 Advanced Legal Exams

These exams are for lawyers who want higher legal positions:

  • Advocate-on-Record (AoR) Exam: For lawyers who want to practice in the Supreme Court of India.
  • Judicial Services Exam: To become a Civil Judge or Magistrate.

Who is a Civil Judge or Magistrate?
A Civil Judge or Magistrate is a judicial officer who hears cases, examines evidence, and gives legal judgments in court.

📝 Exam Full Forms

Exam Full Forms:

CLAT – Common Law Admission Test

LSAT – Law School Admission Test

AIBE – All India Bar Examination

University-level exams - Some colleges and universities conduct their own entrance tests for admission.

🎓 After Completing LLB

Step 1: Register with your State Bar Council.

Step 2: Appear for the All India Bar Examination (AIBE).

Step 3: After clearing AIBE, you receive a Certificate of Practice.

Step 4: You can officially start practicing as a lawyer in court.

🏢 Where do they work?

  • Criminal Lawyer – in Criminal Courts
  • Civil Lawyer – in Civil Courts
  • Corporate Lawyer – in Companies
  • Legal Advisor – in Government Departments
  • Public Prosecutor – in Government Courts

🌍 Global Opportunities

  • International Lawyer – in Global Law Firms
  • Legal Consultant – in International Organizations
  • Human Rights Lawyer – in NGOs worldwide
⭐ Career Insight: Law requires patience, strong thinking skills, and continuous learning.
