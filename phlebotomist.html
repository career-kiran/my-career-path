Phlebotomist

A Phlebotomist is a healthcare professional who collects blood samples from patients for medical tests and diagnosis.

What does a Phlebotomist usually do?

  • Collect blood samples safely from patients
  • Label and handle samples carefully
  • Ensure patient comfort during blood collection
  • Follow hygiene and safety procedures
  • Send samples to the laboratory for testing

You may enjoy this career if you:

  • Are comfortable working with patients
  • Can stay calm while doing medical procedures
  • Are careful and follow instructions well
  • Like healthcare work but prefer short, focused tasks
  • Can maintain cleanliness and hygiene
Real-life example:
A patient comes to a lab for a blood test. The phlebotomist explains the procedure, collects the blood sample safely, and prepares it for testing, helping doctors find out what is wrong.

Courses to become a Phlebotomist (India)

Certificate in Phlebotomy

Duration: 3–6 months

Eligibility: 10th or 12th pass (varies by institute)

Admission: Merit-based

Diploma in Phlebotomy / Medical Laboratory Technology (Basic)

Duration: 6–12 months

Eligibility: 10+2 (Science preferred)

Admission: Merit-based / institute screening

Entrance exams:
There is no national-level entrance exam for Phlebotomy courses. Some institutes may conduct basic aptitude tests or interviews. Admission process may vary from institute to institute.

Where do Phlebotomists work?

  • Hospitals
  • Diagnostic laboratories
  • Blood banks
  • Clinics
  • Health check-up centers
Important note:
Phlebotomists do not diagnose diseases. Their role is limited to collecting blood samples accurately and safely, which helps laboratories and doctors make correct medical decisions.
