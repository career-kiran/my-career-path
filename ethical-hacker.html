🛡 Ethical Hacker

🧑‍💻 What Do They Do?

"When you use apps like Instagram, banking apps, or websites, your personal informations are stored there.

But have you ever thought… what if someone tries to steal that informations?

That’s where security checking becomes important.

So to find any weak points in these websites, there are people who test these systems..

🛡️ The person who does this job is called an Ethical Hacker.

👉 In simple words: Ethical Hackers find problems in systems and help fix them before real hackers."

💖 You may enjoy this path if you...

  • Are curious about how apps and websites work
  • Like finding mistakes or problems
  • Are interested in keeping information safe
  • Like thinking and solving puzzles

Ethical hackers work in companies to keep systems safe. You usually start with small roles and grow with experience.

Beginner Roles

  • Security Intern – works in IT companies or startups → learns how systems are tested for safety
  • Junior Security Analyst – works in tech companies and security teams → checks systems and finds basic problems
  • IT Support (Security) – works in offices and IT departments → helps fix simple security issues

Mid-Level Roles

  • Ethical Hacker – works in cybersecurity firms and tech companies → tests websites and apps for weaknesses
  • Penetration Tester – works in security companies and consulting firms → tries to break into systems (legally)
  • Security Analyst – works in banks, IT companies, and organizations → monitors and protects systems

Advanced Roles

  • Senior Ethical Hacker – works in large companies and leads teams → handles complex security testing
  • Cyber Security Engineer – works in product companies and tech firms → designs secure systems
  • Security Consultant – works independently or in consulting firms → advises companies on security
  • IT Companies – protect websites, apps, and systems from attacks
  • Cyber Security Companies – test systems and find security problems
  • Banks & Financial Companies – protect money and user data
  • Government Organizations – keep national data and systems safe
  • Startup Companies – build secure apps and protect user information
  • Freelancing Platforms – work on security testing projects
  • Remote Jobs (Work From Home) – work for companies from anywhere
  • E-commerce & Social Media Companies – protect user accounts and data
  • Healthcare Companies – keep patient data safe

Ethical hackers help keep apps, websites, and systems safe from attacks.

They do not harm systems. Instead, they check for problems and help fix them.

Today, almost every company stores important data online. This makes security very important.

Ethical hackers are needed in many areas like banking, apps, and online services.

As technology grows, the need for people who can protect systems is also increasing.
