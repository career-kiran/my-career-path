NIFT PG Entrance Exam

NIFT PG Entrance Exam is the entrance examination conducted for admission to postgraduate programmes offered by the National Institute of Fashion Technology (NIFT).

A student writes the NIFT PG Entrance Examination and receives a score.

Based on the score and subsequent selection stages, the student can apply for postgraduate programmes offered by NIFT campuses across India.

Courses may include: Master of Design (M.Des), Master of Fashion Management (MFM), and Master of Fashion Technology (M.F.Tech).

Conducted by: National Institute of Fashion Technology (NIFT).

📌 Quick Facts

Eligibility: Undergraduate Degree in any discipline from a recognized university OR a 3-year Undergraduate Diploma from NIFT/NID.

Mode: GAT: Computer-Based Test (CBT) | CAT: Offline Pen-and-Paper.

Total Questions: GAT: 120 Questions | CAT: 3–4 subjective design prompts.

Total Marks: GAT: 100 Marks | CAT: 100 Marks.

Frequency: Conducted once a year (Usually in February).

Qualification:

  • Must have completed a bachelor's degree from a recognized university.
  • OR a minimum 3-year undergraduate diploma from NIFT or NID.
  • Students appearing for their final year examinations can also apply.

Age:

  • There is no upper age limit for the NIFT PG Entrance Exam.

Min mark:

  • No minimum percentage is required.
  • Candidates must have successfully completed the qualifying degree or diploma.

Attempts:

  • There is no restriction on the number of attempts.
  • You can appear multiple times if you meet the eligibility requirements.

Question Paper Structure:

The NIFT PG (M.Des) Entrance Exam has two sections:

  • GAT (General Ability Test): Tests communication ability, English comprehension, quantitative ability, analytical ability, general knowledge, and current affairs.
  • CAT (Creative Ability Test): Tests creativity, observation skills, visualization, design thinking, and problem-solving ability.

Mode: GAT is conducted as a Computer-Based Test (CBT) and CAT is conducted as a Pen-and-Paper Test.

Frequency: Conducted once a year (usually in February).

Duration: 5 Hours Total (GAT: 2 Hours | CAT: 3 Hours).

Total Questions: GAT – 120 Questions | CAT – 3 to 4 Creative Design Questions.

Total Marks: GAT – 100 Marks | CAT – 100 Marks.

Question Type: Multiple Choice Questions (GAT) and Creative Design Questions (CAT).

Question Paper Structure:

  • GAT (General Ability Test): Tests Communication Ability, English Comprehension, Quantitative Ability, Analytical Ability, General Knowledge, and Current Affairs.
  • CAT (Creative Ability Test): Tests creativity, observation skills, visualization, design thinking, and problem-solving ability.

Marking Scheme (GAT):

  • +1 Mark for every correct answer.
  • -0.25 Mark for every incorrect answer.
  • 0 Marks for unanswered questions.

Important: Candidates must qualify in both GAT and CAT to be shortlisted for the Personal Interview stage.
