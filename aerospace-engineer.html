🚀 Aerospace Engineer

🧑‍🏭 What Do They Do?

"Think about airplanes, rockets, and even satellites.

Have you ever wondered how airplanes fly in the sky and how rockets travel into space?

These are not the same — one works in air, the other works beyond Earth.

Behind these machines, someone carefully designs and tests everything.

That person is an Aerospace Engineer.

Aeronautical Engineers focus only on aircraft, but Aerospace Engineers work on both aircraft and space systems like rockets and satellites.

👉 In simple words: Aerospace engineers design and improve machines that work in air and space."

  • Are interested in airplanes, rockets, or space technology
  • Feel curious about how things fly in air and space
  • Enjoy understanding how machines work

🎓 Courses to Become an Aerospace Engineer

After completing 10th, you can start your journey in aerospace engineering through diploma-level courses.

Diploma in Aerospace Engineering

Duration: 3 Years

Eligibility: Class 10 (SSC) pass

Minimum Marks: 45%–50% aggregate (Science & Maths required)

Age Limit: 15 to 21 years

Exam: State Polytechnic Exams or AME CET

After completing 12th with PCM, you can pursue a degree in aerospace engineering to build strong knowledge in aircraft, rockets, and space systems.

B.Tech / B.E. in Aerospace Engineering

Duration: 4 Years

Eligibility: 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM)

Minimum Marks: 60% aggregate in PCM (higher for top institutes)

Age Limit: Minimum 17 years

Exam: JEE Main, JEE Advanced, BITSAT, COMEDK

B.Tech in Avionics (Space Systems Focus)

Duration: 4 Years

Eligibility: 10+2 with PCM

Minimum Marks: 60%–75% aggregate in PCM

Age Limit: Minimum 17 years

Exam: JEE Advanced (for IIST)

(Click on the exam name to see full details)

M.Tech / M.E. in Aerospace Engineering

Duration: 2 to 3 Years

Eligibility: B.Tech in relevant engineering branch

These are some roles you can explore in Aerospace Engineering.
You usually start with basic roles and grow with experience.

Beginner Roles

  • Junior Aerospace Engineer – works in aviation or space companies
    ► assists in design, testing, and maintenance work
  • Maintenance Engineer – works in airlines or aerospace companies
    ► checks and maintains aircraft systems

Mid-Level Roles

  • Design Engineer – works in aerospace design teams
    ► designs aircraft, rockets, and systems
  • Flight Test Engineer – works in testing teams
    ► tests performance and safety of aircraft and space systems

Advanced Roles

  • Senior Aerospace Engineer – leads engineering work
    ► handles complex systems and improvements
  • Space Systems Engineer – works in space projects
    ► designs and manages satellite and rocket systems
  • Aerospace Companies – design and build aircraft, rockets, and space systems
  • Airlines – maintain and manage aircraft systems and safety
  • Space Organizations – work on satellites, rockets, and space missions
  • Defense Organizations – develop military aircraft and advanced systems
  • Research and Development (R&D) – create new aerospace technologies
  • MNCs (Aerospace Sector) – work on large international aerospace projects
  • Maintenance, Repair & Overhaul (MRO) – inspect and maintain aircraft
  • Abroad Opportunities – countries like USA, France, Germany, and UK have strong aerospace industries

Aerospace Engineers play an important role in designing and improving machines that fly in air and space.

They work on airplanes, rockets, satellites, and other advanced systems used for travel and space missions.

Instead of just building machines, they carefully plan how each part should work and ensure everything is safe.

They focus on design, performance, and safety to make systems more efficient and reliable.

Their work is important in aviation, defense, and space exploration.

Today, this field is growing with advancements in space missions, satellites, and modern aerospace technology.
