🕵️ Criminologist

👤 Who is a Criminologist?

A criminologist is a person who studies crime, criminals, and why crimes happen in society. They try to understand the reasons behind criminal behavior and help improve laws and safety systems.

🛡️ What does a Criminologist do?

  • Studies different types of crimes and their causes
  • Analyzes crime data and patterns
  • Researches how to prevent crimes
  • Advises police, courts, or government on crime-related issues

💖 You may enjoy this profession if:

  • You are curious about why crimes happen
  • You are interested in law and human behavior
  • You like analyzing situations deeply
  • You want to improve society and reduce crime

💡 Real Life Examples

  • A criminologist studies why cybercrime is increasing among teenagers and suggests ways to prevent it.
  • A criminologist studies crime patterns in a city to understand trends and causes.

📚 How to Become a Criminologist?

BA / BSc in Criminology

Duration: 3 Years

Eligibility: 12th pass (Any stream – some colleges prefer Arts).

Minimum Marks: Usually 50% aggregate (varies by college).

Admission: Merit-based or university-level entrance exams (such as CUET).

MA / MSc in Criminology

Duration: 2 Years

Eligibility: Bachelor’s degree in Criminology / Sociology / Psychology / Law or related field.

Minimum Marks: Usually 50–55% in graduation.

Admission: University-level entrance exams or merit-based.

PhD in Criminology

Duration: 3–5 Years

Eligibility: Master’s degree in related field.

Admission: UGC-NET or university-level PhD entrance exam.

Integrated BA + MA in Criminology

Duration: 5 Years

Eligibility: 12th pass (any stream – Arts preferred in some colleges)

Minimum Marks: 50% aggregate (may vary by college)

Admission: Merit-based or university-level entrance exams

📝 Exam Full Forms

CUET – Common University Entrance Test

UGC-NET – University Grants Commission – National Eligibility Test

University-level exams – Some colleges and universities conduct their own entrance tests for admission.

🏢 Where do they work?

  • Criminologist / Research Officer – in Police Departments
  • Crime Analyst – in Government Investigation Agencies
  • Research Associate – in Universities and Research Institutions
  • Policy Advisor – in Government Crime Prevention Departments
  • Consultant – in NGOs working on crime and rehabilitation

🌍 Global Opportunities

  • Crime Analyst – in International Law Enforcement Agencies
  • Researcher – in Global Crime Research Institutes
  • Policy Consultant – in International Human Rights Organizations

⭐ Career Insight

Criminology is mainly research and analysis based. If you are looking for field investigation work like police officers or forensic experts, that is a different career path. Criminologists mainly study, analyze, and advise on crime-related issues.
