🧠 Imagine This...

"You are watching a movie with an interesting story.

The characters speak powerful dialogues and every scene connects perfectly to the next.

Before the movie was filmed, someone had to write the story, scenes, and dialogues.

That person is called a Screenwriter/ScriptWriter.

Screenwriters create the script.This script is used by directors, actors, and the film crew to bring a story to life."

🎓 How to Become a Screenwriter

After 10th Standard (Creative Foundations)

Courses:

  • Certificate in Creative Writing
  • Diploma in Scriptwriting
  • Diploma in Narrative Storytelling

Duration: 3 – 6 months

Eligibility: 10th pass from any recognized board

Minimum Marks: Usually 45% – 50%

Age Limit: Around 15 years minimum

Exam to Pass: Usually direct admission based on a short writing sample or creative essay.

After 12th Standard (Professional Degree Programs)

Courses:

  • BA in Film & Television Production
  • BA in English Literature (with Film electives)
  • B.Des in Communication Design

Duration: 3 years

Eligibility: 12th pass in any stream (Arts / Humanities recommended)

Minimum Marks: Usually around 50% in 12th

Age Limit: Some government institutes may have an age limit around 17 – 25 years

Exam to Pass:

  • National Level: CUET, NID DAT
  • Film Institutes: FTII JET, CUCET

After Graduation (Advanced Specialisation & Masters)

Courses:

  • PG Diploma in Screenplay Writing
  • MA in Film Production
  • MA in Media & Communication

Duration: 1 – 2 years

Eligibility: Graduation in any field (Literature or Journalism helpful)

Minimum Marks: Around 50% in graduation

Age Limit: Around 21 – 30 years for major government institutes like FTII

Exam to Pass:

  • National Level: FTII JET (Direction & Screenplay Writing track)
  • Writing Sample / Interview: Required in some institutes

(Note: Age limits and minimum marks may vary depending on the institute, exam authority, or state regulations.)

⭐ Note

Screenwriting is a creative field where ideas, storytelling, and imagination play a very important role.

There is no single degree required to become a screenwriter. Many successful screenwriters learn by reading, writing stories, and practicing script writing regularly.

However, studying film, literature, or media courses can help you understand story structure, character development, and screenplay techniques.

🏢 Where Do Screenwriters Work?

  • Movie Screenwriter – write scripts and dialogues for films and movies.
  • Television Screenwriter – write stories and dialogues for TV serials and shows.
  • Web Series Screenwriter – create scripts for web series and digital shows on OTT platforms.
  • Advertising Scriptwriter – write scripts for commercials and promotional videos.
  • Documentary Scriptwriter – write scripts and narration for documentary films.
  • YouTube / Digital Content Writer – write scripts for YouTube videos and online media content.
  • Freelance Screenwriter – work independently writing scripts for films, shows, ads, or online content.

🌍 Global Opportunities

  • International Film Production Companies – write scripts for movies and film projects produced for global audiences.
  • OTT & Streaming Platforms – create stories and scripts for web series and digital shows on platforms like Netflix, Amazon Prime, and others.
  • International Advertising Agencies – write scripts for global commercials and marketing campaigns.
  • Animation & Entertainment Studios – write stories and scripts for animated films, cartoon series, and entertainment productions.
  • Digital Media Companies – create scripts for online video content, documentaries, and digital storytelling projects.
  • Freelance International Projects – collaborate with filmmakers and production companies from different countries on scripts and story development.

⭐ Career Insight

Screenwriters are responsible for creating the story and script that films, web series, and shows are based on.

They develop characters, scenes, and dialogues that guide the entire filmmaking process.

Many screenwriters start by writing short scripts, stories, or online content, gradually building experience before working on professional film or television projects.
