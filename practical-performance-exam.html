Practical Performance Exam

Practical Performance Exam is a selection process conducted by some colleges, universities, and performing arts institutes for admission to performance-based courses.

This exam is mainly conducted to check a student's practical skills, confidence, and ability to perform.

The exam may include:

• Singing a song, playing an instrument, dancing, or acting
• Performing in front of the judges
• Performing a new activity given during the exam
• Showing confidence and expression
• Answering a few simple questions about your performance

In simple words, a practical performance exam is conducted to find out whether you have the practical skills needed to join a performing arts course.

Example:

A student applies for a Bachelor of Performing Arts.

During the exam, the student performs before the judges.

The judges evaluate the student's practical skills, confidence, and overall performance.

Based on the performance, the institute decides whether to offer admission for the course.
