Counsellor/Psychologist

A counsellor helps people talk about their problems, understand their feelings, and find ways to deal with stress, confusion, or difficult situations in life.

Real-life example
A college student feels anxious, confused about career choices, and struggles with self-confidence. A counsellor listens carefully, helps the student understand their feelings, and guides them to think clearly and make decisions step by step.

You may enjoy this career if you:

  • Like listening to people and understanding their problems
  • Are willing to support others without judging them
  • Enjoy meaningful conversations
  • Want to help people improve their emotional well-being

Courses to become a Counsellor (India)

Bachelor’s Degree (Psychology or related fields)

Common options: BA Psychology, BSc Psychology

Duration: 3 years

Eligibility:

  • 12th standard (any stream for most colleges)
  • Minimum marks: usually 45–50%

Admission process: Mostly merit-based or university-level entrance exam

Master’s Degree (Counselling Psychology / Applied Psychology)

Duration: 2 years

Eligibility:

  • Bachelor’s degree in Psychology or a related field
  • Minimum marks: usually 50–55%

Admission process: University-level entrance exam or merit-based

Important
To work as a professional counsellor, a master’s degree and supervised practical training are usually required.

Where do counsellors work?

  • Schools and colleges
  • Hospitals and mental health centres
  • Career guidance centres
  • Rehabilitation centres
  • NGOs and community organisations
  • Private counselling clinics

Important note

Counselling focuses on listening, understanding, and guiding people through challenges. It requires patience, empathy, and continuous learning.
