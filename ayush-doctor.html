AYUSH Doctor

An AYUSH doctor treats patients using traditional systems of medicine such as Ayurveda, Homeopathy, Unani, Siddha, or Yoga and Naturopathy. AYUSH focuses on natural healing, lifestyle balance, prevention, and overall well-being.

What does an AYUSH doctor usually do?

  • Treats common illnesses using traditional medicine systems
  • Focuses on long-term health and disease prevention
  • Advises on diet, lifestyle, and daily habits
  • Uses natural medicines, therapies, and exercises
  • Works to improve overall health, not just symptoms
Real-life example
A person has long-term digestion problems or joint pain and prefers natural treatment. An AYUSH doctor examines the person and suggests natural medicines, diet changes, and lifestyle habits to improve health over time.

You may enjoy this career if you:

  • Believe in natural or traditional healing methods
  • Are interested in holistic health and lifestyle care
  • Like spending time understanding patients’ daily habits
  • Prefer prevention and long-term wellness approaches
  • Want to work in healthcare with a traditional system

Courses to become an AYUSH Doctor (India)

BAMS – Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery

System: Ayurveda

Duration: 5½ years (4½ years study + 1 year internship)

Eligibility:

  • 12th standard with Physics, Chemistry, Biology
  • Minimum marks: usually 50% (varies by category)

Entrance exam: NEET-UG

BHMS – Bachelor of Homeopathic Medicine and Surgery

System: Homeopathy

Duration: 5½ years (including internship)

Eligibility:

  • 12th standard with Physics, Chemistry, Biology
  • Minimum marks: usually 50%

Entrance exam: NEET-UG

BUMS – Bachelor of Unani Medicine and Surgery

System: Unani

Duration: 5½ years (including internship)

Eligibility:

  • 12th standard with Physics, Chemistry, Biology
  • Minimum marks: usually 50%

Entrance exam: NEET-UG

BSMS – Bachelor of Siddha Medicine and Surgery

System: Siddha

Duration: 5½ years (including internship)

Eligibility:

  • 12th standard with Physics, Chemistry, Biology
  • Minimum marks: usually 50%

Entrance exam: NEET-UG

BNYS – Bachelor of Naturopathy & Yogic Sciences

System: Naturopathy & Yoga

Duration: 5½ years (including internship)

Eligibility:

  • 12th standard with Physics, Chemistry, Biology
  • Minimum marks: usually 50%

Entrance exam: NEET-UG

Note: Admission process, counselling, and seat allocation may vary by state and institute. Always check official AYUSH counselling or college websites for exact details.

Where do AYUSH doctors work?

  • AYUSH hospitals and clinics
  • Wellness and therapy centres
  • Government AYUSH hospitals
  • Private practice
  • Yoga and naturopathy centres

Important note

Some AYUSH doctors, after completing postgraduate training, are permitted to perform certain limited procedures as per government regulations. The scope of practice depends on the system of medicine, qualification, and state rules.
