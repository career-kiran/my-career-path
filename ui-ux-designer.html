UI / UX Designer

⭐ Save 📤 Share

🧠 Imagine this...

You open a food delivery app like Zomato or Swiggy on your phone.

You easily find restaurants, choose your food, and place an order.

Everything feels simple and easy to use.

Do you ever wonder how everything works so smoothly?

Someone carefully designed how the app should look and how people should use it.

That is the work of a UI / UX Designer.

NOTE:

Website Designer → Designs how a website looks. (Focus on looks)

UI / UX Designer → Makes apps and websites easy to use. (Focus on usage)

👉 Try This Now

Want to see how UI / UX designers think? Try this small activity

1

Open any app on your phone

2

Look at the buttons, menus, and search bar

3

Ask yourself: Is this easy to use?

4

Think about one thing you can do to make the app better

If this feels interesting to you, you may enjoy being a UI / UX Designer.

🛡️ What does a UI / UX Designer do?

  • Designs how apps and websites should look.
  • Makes apps and websites easy for people to use.
  • Arranges buttons, menus, and content on the screen.
  • Improves the user experience while using an app or website.

💖 You may enjoy this path if you...

  • Like designing apps and websites.
  • Like making apps and websites easier for people to use.
  • Like working with technology.

🎓 Courses to Enter This Field

After 10th – Diploma Courses

Courses

  • Diploma in Web Design
  • Diploma in Graphic Design
  • Diploma in UI / UX Design

Duration: 1 – 2 years

Eligibility: 10th pass

Min Marks: Usually not strict

Exam to pass: Not required

After 12th – Bachelor's Degree

Courses

  • B.Des (Bachelor of Design)
  • B.Sc in UI / UX Design
  • BCA (Bachelor of Computer Applications)

Duration: 3 – 4 years

Eligibility: 12th pass (any stream)

Min Marks: Around 50% (varies by college)

Exam to pass: NID / UCEED / CUET (varies by college)

After Graduation – Master’s Degree

Courses

  • M.Des (Master of Design)
  • M.Sc in UI / UX Design

Duration: 2 years

Eligibility: Bachelor’s degree

Exam to pass: Entrance exam or interview (varies by institute)

Short-Term Certification Courses

Courses

  • UI / UX Design Certification
  • UX Design Fundamentals
  • Web Design Certification
  • UI Design using Figma

Duration: Few weeks – 6 months

Eligibility: Open to anyone

Exam to pass: Not required

📝 Exam Details

  • NID – National Institute of Design Entrance Exam


    Conducted by the National Institute of Design for admission to design courses like B.Des (Bachelor of Design).

  • UCEED – Undergraduate Common Entrance Exam for Design


    Conducted by IIT Bombay for admission to B.Des programs in IITs and some other design institutes.

  • CUET – Common University Entrance Test


    Conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) for admission to undergraduate programs in many central universities.

🏢 Where do they work?

  • UI / UX Designer – In companies that create apps and websites.
  • Product Designer – In technology companies that build digital products.
  • Interaction Designer – In design teams that decide how users interact with apps.
  • UX Researcher – In companies that study how people use apps and websites.
  • Freelance UI / UX Designer – Working independently on design projects for different companies.

🌍 Global Opportunities

  • International Companies – Work for companies in other countries that build apps and digital products.
  • Remote Design Jobs – Work online from home for international companies.
  • Freelance Platforms (International Projects) – Take design projects from people and companies around the world.
  • Overseas Employment – Get full-time design jobs in countries like USA, UK, Canada, or Australia.

⭐ Career Insight

Good UI / UX Designers focus on making apps and websites simple and easy for people to use.

If you enjoy technology, creativity, and solving problems, this field can offer many exciting opportunities.

You can also try creating simple designs using tools like Figma or Canva.
