State-Level Lateral Entry Entrance Exams

State-Level Lateral Entry Entrance Exams are entrance examinations conducted by various state governments or universities for direct admission into the second year (third semester) of B.E./B.Tech programmes.

These examinations are mainly meant for students who have completed a Diploma in Engineering or, in some states, a B.Sc. degree.

These students can skip the first year and join in second year and can complete their engineering degree in three years instead of four.

Conducted by: The respective State Government, State Entrance Examination Authority, or University, depending on the state.

📌 Quick Facts

Eligibility: Diploma in Engineering or equivalent qualification (B.Sc. graduates are also eligible in some states).

Exam Pattern: Varies from state to state. Most examinations consist of Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs).

Mode: Online (CBT) or Offline (Pen-and-Paper), depending on the state.

Admission: Successful candidates are usually admitted directly into the second year (third semester) of B.E./B.Tech programmes.

Frequency: Conducted once a year.

Important: Every state has its own lateral entry admission process. Eligibility criteria, exam pattern, syllabus, and participating colleges may differ from one state to another.

Qualification:

  • Must have completed a Diploma in Engineering/Technology from a recognized institution.
  • In some states, candidates with a B.Sc. degree (with Mathematics as a subject) are also eligible.
  • Qualification may vary depending on the rules of the respective state.

Age:

  • Most states do not have an upper age limit for lateral entry admission.
  • However, age requirements may vary from state to state.

Minimum Marks:

  • For the Exam: The minimum percentage required varies depending on the state.
  • For Admission: Candidates must satisfy the eligibility criteria prescribed by the respective state or institution.

Domicile/Residential Criteria:

  • Most state-level lateral entry entrance examinations are primarily meant for candidates who are residents (domiciles) of that state.
  • Some states may also allow candidates from other states, depending on their admission rules.
  • Always check the official notification of your state's examination authority for the latest domicile requirements.

Attempts:

  • There is generally no limit on the number of attempts.
  • However, this may vary depending on the state's admission rules.

Nationality:

  • Indian citizens are eligible to apply.
  • Some states may also allow NRI or other categories as per their admission policies.

Mode of Examination: Online (Computer-Based Test) or Offline (Pen-and-Paper), depending on the state.

Question Type: Mostly Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs).

Subjects: Mathematics, Engineering Fundamentals, and branch-specific technical subjects (varies by state).

Duration: Usually 2 to 3 hours (varies by state).

Frequency: Conducted once a year.

Total Questions: Varies from one state to another.

Total Marks: Varies from one state to another.

Marking Scheme:

  • Correct Answer: The marks awarded for each correct answer vary depending on the state.
  • Incorrect Answer: Some states have negative marking, while others do not.
  • Unattempted Question: No marks are awarded or deducted.

Important:

The syllabus, exam pattern, marking scheme, and duration are decided by the respective state examination authority.

Candidates should always check the official notification or website of their state for the latest information.

Students who qualify through State-Level Lateral Entry Entrance Exams can secure direct admission into the second year (third semester) of various undergraduate programmes. Engineering is the most common course offered through lateral entry.

Available Programmes:

  • Engineering: B.E. (Bachelor of Engineering) and B.Tech (Bachelor of Technology).
  • Pharmacy: B.Pharm (Bachelor of Pharmacy) (in states where applicable).

Popular Engineering Branches:

  • Computer Science & Engineering (CSE)
  • Artificial Intelligence & Data Science
  • Information Technology (IT)
  • Electronics & Communication Engineering (ECE)
  • Electrical Engineering (EE)
  • Mechanical Engineering
  • Civil Engineering
  • Chemical Engineering
  • Automobile Engineering
  • Aerospace Engineering
  • Biotechnology Engineering
  • And many more...

Important Note:

Qualifying for a State-Level Lateral Entry Entrance Exam does not guarantee admission.

Final admission depends on your entrance exam rank, eligibility criteria, counselling process, seat availability, reservation rules, and the college or branch you choose.

Always check your state's official admission notification.
