Nurse

Nursing is a healthcare profession where you help people when they are sick, injured, or recovering.

What does a nurse usually do?

  • Care for patients in hospitals, clinics, or communities
  • Monitor health and report changes to doctors
  • Give medicines prescribed by the doctor
  • Support patients emotionally and physically
  • Assist doctors during treatment
  • Educate patients and families about care and recovery

You may enjoy this career if you:

  • Feel comfortable caring for others
  • Can stay calm in difficult situations
  • Are patient, observant, and responsible
  • Don’t mind working in shifts or hospital environments

Courses to become a Nurse (India)

ANM (Auxiliary Nurse Midwife)

Duration: Around 2 years

Eligibility: 12th pass

Minimum marks: Usually 40–45% in 12th (varies by institution)

Entrance exam: State-level nursing entrance exam (varies by state)

GNM (General Nursing and Midwifery)

Duration: Around 3 years

Eligibility: 12th pass (Science preferred)

Minimum marks: Usually 40–45% in 12th (varies by state and category)

Entrance exam: State-level nursing entrance exam

B.Sc Nursing

Duration: 4 years

Eligibility: 12th with Biology

Minimum marks: Usually 45–50% in 12th (varies by college and category)

Entrance exam: NEET-UG / State nursing entrance exam

Where do nurses work?

  • Hospitals (government and private)
  • Clinics and nursing homes
  • Community health centres
  • Schools and colleges
Eligibility rules and duration may vary slightly between states and institutions. Always check official notifications before applying.
