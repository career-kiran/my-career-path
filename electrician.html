💡 Electrician

🧠 Imagine This...

"Look around your home and notice the electrical devices you use every day — lights, fans, switches, and sockets.

You press a switch and the light turns on instantly. The fan starts spinning.

Have you ever wondered how this happens?

When you press a switch, electricity flows through wires hidden inside the walls.

But this works only because someone installed the wiring and connections properly when the house was built."

🛡️ This is the work of an Electrician.

💖 You may enjoy this job if You...

  • Enjoy fixing things when they stop working.
  • Like working with tools and equipment.
  • Feel curious about how electricity and electrical devices work.
  • Enjoy solving practical problems.

🎓 How to Become an Electrician

After 10th (Most Common Path)

Courses:

  • ITI Electrician (NCVT / SCVT)

Duration: 2 years

Eligibility: 10th pass with Science and Mathematics

Minimum Marks: Usually around 35% – 50%

Age Limit: Minimum 14 years

Exam to Pass: State ITI entrance exam or direct admission based on marks

After 10th (Diploma Path)

Courses:

  • Diploma in Electrical Engineering (Polytechnic)

Duration: Around 3 years

Eligibility: 10th or 12th pass with Maths and Science

Minimum Marks: Usually around 35% – 50%

Exam to Pass: State Polytechnic entrance exams

After 12th (Engineering Path)

Courses:

  • B.Tech in Electrical Engineering
  • B.E. in Electrical Engineering

Duration: Around 4 years

Eligibility: 12th pass with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM)

Minimum Marks: Usually 45% – 60% in PCM

Exam to Pass:

  • National Level: JEE Main, JEE Advanced
  • State Level: State engineering entrance exams

After 12th (Diploma Path – Lateral Entry)

Courses:

  • Lateral Entry to Diploma in Electrical Engineering

Duration: Around 2 years (Direct entry to 2nd year)

Eligibility: 12th pass (Science/Vocational) or 10th + 2-year ITI

Minimum Marks: Usually 40% – 50%

Age Limit: Same as regular Diploma (Minimum 14 – 16 years)

Exam to Pass:

  • State Lateral Entry Entrance Exams (example: JELET,LEET)

After Diploma or Graduation (Advanced Specialisation)

Courses:

  • B.Tech Lateral Entry (for Diploma holders)
  • PG Diploma in Power Plant Engineering
  • PG Diploma in Electrical Safety

Duration: 1 – 3 years depending on course

Eligibility: Diploma or Engineering degree in electrical field

Minimum Marks: Usually around 45% – 60%

Exam to Pass: State entrance exam or institute interview

(Note: Age limits, marks, and admission processes may vary depending on the institute or state.)

⭐ Note

Becoming an electrician usually requires practical training and hands-on experience, not just classroom learning.

Many electricians start their careers through ITI courses, apprenticeships, or diploma programs, where they learn electrical wiring, safety procedures, and equipment installation.

🏢 Where do Electricians Work?

  • Residential Electrician – work in homes installing and repairing wiring, switches, lights, and electrical appliances.
  • Commercial Electrician – work in offices, malls, schools, and hospitals installing and maintaining electrical systems.
  • Industrial Electrician – work in factories and industries maintaining machines, motors, and heavy electrical equipment.
  • Maintenance Electrician – work in buildings, hotels, and companies fixing electrical problems and maintaining power systems.
  • Construction Electrician – work on construction sites installing electrical wiring and power systems in new buildings.
  • Independent Electrician – work independently or run their own electrical service business repairing and installing electrical systems.

🌍 Global Opportunities

  • International Construction Projects – electricians work on large construction projects such as buildings, airports, and infrastructure in different countries.
  • Industrial Companies – work in factories and manufacturing plants maintaining electrical systems and machinery.
  • Oil and Gas Industry – electricians work on offshore platforms and energy facilities maintaining power systems.
  • Renewable Energy Projects – work on solar, wind, and other renewable energy installations around the world.
  • Maintenance Companies – work in companies that manage electrical maintenance for buildings, hotels, hospitals, and offices.
  • International Contract Work – electricians can work abroad on contract jobs in countries that require skilled electrical workers.
  • Electrical Installation Companies – work with global companies installing electrical systems in commercial and residential buildings.
  • Self-Employment Abroad – experienced electricians can start their own electrical services or work independently in different countries.

💡 Career Insight

Many electricians start their careers by working with experienced electricians or electrical contractors to gain practical experience.

With time and experience, they can specialize in areas like industrial electrical systems, solar installations, electrical maintenance, or automation systems.

Experienced electricians can also start their own electrical service business or work independently taking repair and installation projects.
