Economist

An economist studies how money, resources, businesses, and governments make decisions. They analyze data and trends to understand how the economy works and suggest solutions.

You may enjoy this career if:

  • You enjoy analyzing numbers and trends.
  • You are curious about how the economy and markets work.
  • You like solving real-world financial and policy problems.

Real Life Examples:

  • An economist studies price rise (inflation) and suggests ways to control it.
  • An economist analyzes unemployment data to help the government create job policies.

Courses to Become an Economist

B.A / B.Sc in Economics (3 years)

Eligibility: 10+2 (preferably with Mathematics)

Minimum Marks: Usually 50–60% (varies by college)

Admission: Merit-based or entrance exams like CUET

M.A / M.Sc in Economics (2 years)

Eligibility: Bachelor’s degree in Economics or related field

Minimum Marks: Usually 50–60%

Admission: University entrance exams, CUET-PG or state-level exams

Ph.D in Economics (3–5 years)

Eligibility: Master’s degree in Economics

Admission: Exams like UGC-NET (University Grants Commission – National Eligibility Test), JRF or university-level Ph.D entrance exams

Where Do Economists Work?

  • Economic Researcher / Policy Analyst – in the Reserve Bank of India (RBI)
  • Budget Analyst / Economic Advisor – in the Ministry of Finance
  • Policy Consultant / Development Analyst – in NITI Aayog
  • Financial Analyst / Risk Analyst – in banks and financial institutions
  • Market Research Analyst / Strategy Analyst – in corporate companies
  • Research Fellow / Research Associate – in economic research institutions
  • Professor / Lecturer – in universities and colleges

Important Note:

For senior research, policy-making, or professor positions, a Master’s degree or Ph.D is usually required.
