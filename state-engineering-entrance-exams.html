State Engineering Entrance Exams

State Engineering Entrance Exams are entrance examinations conducted by various state governments or state authorities for admission to B.E. (Bachelor of Engineering), B.Tech (Bachelor of Technology), and other undergraduate engineering programmes offered by colleges within their respective states.

Different states conduct different engineering entrance exams. Some of the popular state-level engineering entrance exams include MHT CET (Maharashtra), KCET (Karnataka), KEAM (Kerala), WBJEE (West Bengal), AP EAPCET (Andhra Pradesh), and TS EAPCET (Telangana).

Conducted by: The respective State Government, State Entrance Examination Authority, or University, depending on the state.

📌 Quick Facts

Eligibility: Class 12 (or equivalent) with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM).

Exam Pattern: Most exams are conducted as Computer-Based Tests (CBT) with Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs). However, the exam pattern may vary from one state to another.

Mode: Online (CBT) or Offline (Pen-and-Paper), depending on the state.

Question Type: Mostly Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs).

Frequency: Conducted once a year by the respective state authority.

Important: Different states conduct their own engineering entrance examinations for admission to B.E., B.Tech, and other undergraduate engineering programmes. Eligibility criteria, syllabus, exam pattern, and admission process may vary from one state to another.

Qualification:

  • Must have passed or be appearing for the Class 12 (10+2) or equivalent examination from a recognized board.
  • Most states require students to have studied Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM) in Class 12.

Age:

  • The age requirement, if any, depends on the rules of the respective state.

Minimum Marks:

  • For the Exam: The minimum marks required to apply vary from one state to another.
  • For Admission: The minimum marks required for admission also vary depending on the state and the college.

Domicile:

  • Many states reserve seats for students who are permanent residents of that state.
  • Domicile means you are a permanent resident of that state or meet the domicile rules specified by the state government.

Nationality:

  • Indian citizens can apply. Eligibility for NRI, OCI, PIO, and foreign candidates depends on the rules of the respective state.

Mode of Examination: Online (Computer-Based Test) or Offline (Pen-and-Paper), depending on the state.

Question Type: Mostly Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs).

Subjects: Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.

Duration: Usually 2 to 3 hours (varies by state).

Total Questions: Varies from one state to another.

Total Marks: Varies from one state to another.

Marking Scheme:

  • Correct Answer: The marks awarded for each correct answer vary depending on the state.
  • Incorrect Answer: Some states have negative marking, while others do not.
  • Unattempted Question: No marks are awarded or deducted.

Important: The syllabus, exam pattern, marking scheme, and duration are decided by the respective state examination authority. Candidates should always check their state's official website for the latest information.

Students who qualify through State Engineering Entrance Exams can apply for undergraduate engineering and technology programmes offered by government, government-aided, and private colleges within their respective states.

Engineering Courses:

  • B.Tech (Bachelor of Technology)
  • B.E. (Bachelor of Engineering)
  • B.Arch (Bachelor of Architecture) (in states where applicable)
  • B.Plan (Bachelor of Planning) (in states where applicable)
  • Integrated Engineering Programmes (offered by some institutes)

Popular Engineering Branches:

  • Computer Science & Engineering (CSE)
  • Artificial Intelligence & Data Science
  • Information Technology (IT)
  • Electronics & Communication Engineering (ECE)
  • Electrical Engineering (EE)
  • Mechanical Engineering
  • Civil Engineering
  • Chemical Engineering
  • Aerospace Engineering
  • Biotechnology Engineering
  • And many more...

Important Note:

Qualifying a state engineering entrance exam does not guarantee admission.

Admission depends on your entrance exam rank, eligibility criteria, counselling process, seat availability, reservation rules, and the college or branch you choose.
