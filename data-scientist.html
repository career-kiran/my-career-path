🧠 Imagine This...

"Think about a shopping app or food delivery app...

You open it... and it shows you exactly what you might like.
You see products, offers, and suggestions that match your interest.

  • Are curious to find patterns and trends.
  • Like solving problems step by step.

🎓 How to Become a Software Developer

After 12th Standard (Main Career Path)

This is the most common path to become a Data Scientist.
Here, you learn programming, mathematics, and how to work with data.

B.Tech / B.E in Data Science / Computer Science / IT

Duration: 4 years

Eligibility: 12th with Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics (PCM)

Minimum Marks: Usually 50% – 70%

Age Limit: Minimum 17 years

Exam: JEE Main, JEE Advanced, or State Entrance Exams

B.Sc in Data Science / Statistics / Computer Science

Duration: 3 years

Eligibility: 12th with Science stream (Mathematics preferred)

Minimum Marks: Usually 50% – 60%

Age Limit: Minimum 17 years

Exam: CUET UG or university entrance exams

BCA (Bachelor of Computer Applications)

Duration: 3 years

Eligibility: 12th pass (any stream, Maths/Computer Science helpful)

Minimum Marks: Usually 45% – 60%

Age Limit: Minimum 17 years

Exam: CUET, IPU-CET, or institute-level exams

💼 Where do Data Scientists work? (Job Role + Where)

  • Data Scientist – work in companies where large data is studied to find patterns and make decisions
  • Data Analyst – work in companies to analyze data and create reports and charts
  • Machine Learning Engineer – work in tech companies to build systems that can learn from data
  • Business Analyst – work in companies to understand business data and improve decisions
  • AI Engineer – work in companies to build smart systems like chatbots and recommendation systems
  • Research Analyst – work in research firms or labs to study data and trends
  • IT Company Employee – work in startups, MNCs, or tech companies handling data-related projects
  • Freelancer – work independently by analyzing data or building data models for clients

🌍 Global Opportunities

Data Scientists can access global opportunities, but most roles require experience and strong practical skills.

  • Jobs in International Companies – work in global companies handling data, business analysis, and AI projects
  • Jobs Abroad (USA, UK, Canada, etc.) – experienced professionals can work in countries where data science skills are in high demand
  • Tech, Banking & Healthcare Industries – work in different industries using data to solve real-world problems
  • Global Data Projects – work on projects like customer analysis, prediction, and automation
  • Remote Jobs – some companies offer work-from-home roles, but require strong skills and experience
  • Freelancing – experienced professionals can work independently for global clients

💡 Career Insight

Data Scientists usually start by learning basic concepts like data analysis, statistics, and programming.

They gain experience by working on small projects, analyzing data, and practicing with tools like Excel and Python.

With time, they can grow into roles like Machine Learning Engineer, AI Engineer, Data Analyst, or Business Analyst.

Experienced professionals can work in top companies, handle large data projects, or even work with international teams.
