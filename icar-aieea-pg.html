ICAR AIEEA PG (Indian Council of Agricultural Research All India Entrance Examination for Admission - Postgraduate)

ICAR AIEEA PG is a national-level entrance examination conducted for admission to postgraduate agriculture and allied science courses in India.

Conducted by: National Testing Agency (NTA) on behalf of the Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR).

📌 Quick Facts

Eligibility: Bachelor's Degree in a relevant field

Mode: Computer Based Test (CBT)

Duration: 2 Hours

Questions: 120 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

Frequency: Usually conducted once a year(June or July)

Qualification: Bachelor's degree in Agriculture, Veterinary Science, Fisheries Science, Horticulture, Forestry, Agricultural Engineering or another relevant field from a recognised institution.

Age: Age requirements may vary according to the admission guidelines.

Minimum Marks: Candidates must satisfy the eligibility requirements prescribed for their chosen postgraduate programme.

Nationality: Indian Citizen.

Mode: Computer Based Test (CBT)

Question Type: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

Exam Frequency: Conducted once a year (usually June or July)

Duration: 2 Hours

Total Questions: 120

Total Marks: 480 Marks

Marking Scheme:

  • +4 - for every correct answer
  • -1 - for every wrong answer
  • 0 - for unattempted questions

After clearing ICAR AIEEA PG exam, students can get admission to various postgraduate programmes in agriculture and allied sciences.

Courses may include:
  • M.Sc Agriculture
  • M.Sc Horticulture
  • M.Sc Forestry
  • M.V.Sc (Master of Veterinary Science)
  • M.F.Sc (Master of Fisheries Science)
  • M.Tech Agricultural Engineering
  • M.Sc Dairy Science
  • M.Sc Food Science & Technology
  • M.Sc Agricultural Economics
  • M.Sc Community Science
