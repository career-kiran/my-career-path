Medical Scribe

A Medical Scribe is a healthcare professional who helps doctors by documenting patient information during medical visits. This allows doctors to focus more on patients instead of paperwork.

What does a Medical Scribe usually do?

  • Listen to doctor–patient conversations
  • Type medical notes on the computer
  • Record symptoms, diagnoses, and treatment plans
  • Update patient histories and test results
  • Support doctors with accurate documentation

You may enjoy this career if you:

  • Are comfortable with computers and typing
  • Like healthcare work but prefer computer-based tasks
  • Pay attention to detail
  • Can listen carefully and write quickly
  • Prefer working in clinics or hospitals
Real-life example:
A patient visits a doctor for fever and cough. While the doctor talks to the patient, the medical scribe listens carefully and types the symptoms, examination details, and medicines on the computer so the doctor can focus fully on the patient.

Courses to become a Medical Scribe

Certificate in Medical Scribing

Duration: 3–6 months

Eligibility: 10+2 (any stream; science preferred)

Admission: Merit-based / institute screening

Diploma in Medical Transcription / Scribing

Duration: 6–12 months

Eligibility: 10+2

Admission: Merit-based

Entrance exams:
There is no national entrance exam for Medical Scribe courses. Some institutes may conduct a basic English, typing, or aptitude test. Admission process may vary from institute to institute.
Important note:
Medical Scribes do not diagnose or treat patients. Their role is to support doctors by handling medical documentation accurately. Course availability, admission process, and job roles may vary depending on the institute and healthcare setting.
