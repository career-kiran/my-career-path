AAFT GET (AAFT Global Entrance Test)

AAFT GET is the entrance examination conducted by AAFT (Asian Academy of Film & Television) for admission to various creative, media, design, fashion, journalism, animation, and arts programmes.

Students seeking admission to undergraduate, postgraduate, diploma, and certificate programmes at AAFT may need to appear for AAFT GET.

Selection includes an aptitude test followed by a personal interview.

Conducted by: Asian Academy of Film & Television (AAFT).

📌 Quick Facts

Eligibility:

Class 12 pass - for UG programmes

Bachelor's degree - for PG programmes.

Exam Pattern: Aptitude Test followed by Personal Interview.

Mode: Online Aptitude Test.

Total Questions: Varies by course.

Total Marks: Varies by course.

Frequency: Conducted multiple times during the admission cycle.

Qualification:

  • For Undergraduate (UG) programmes - candidates must have passed Class 12 (10+2) or an equivalent examination from a recognized board.
  • For Postgraduate (PG) programmes - candidates must hold a Bachelor's degree or an equivalent qualification from a recognized university.
  • Candidates appearing for their final Class 12 or Graduation examinations may also apply provisionally.

Age:

  • There is generally no upper age limit for appearing in AAFT GET.

Min mark:

  • Minimum marks requirements may vary depending on the course.

Attempt:

  • There is no restriction on the number of attempts.
  • Candidates may apply as long as they meet the eligibility requirements.

Mode: Online Aptitude Test followed by a Personal Interview.

Frequency: Conducted multiple times during the admission cycle.

Duration: Varies depending on the course.

Total Questions: Varies depending on the course.

Total Marks: Varies depending on the course.

Question Type: Objective-type aptitude questions.

Selection Process:

The AAFT GET admission process has two stages:

  • Stage 1 – Aptitude Test: Tests general awareness, logical reasoning, communication skills, creativity, and aptitude related to the chosen course.
  • Stage 2 – Personal Interview: Evaluates the candidate's interest, communication skills, career goals, and suitability for the chosen course.

Marking Scheme:

  • Evaluation criteria may vary depending on the course.
  • Final selection is based on performance in both the Aptitude Test and Personal Interview.
  • No standard negative marking policy is announced for all courses.

Important: Different AAFT courses may have slightly different admission requirements and evaluation criteria.

Students who qualify AAFT GET may apply for various undergraduate and postgraduate programmes offered by the Asian Academy of Film & Television (AAFT).

Courses may include:
  • B.Des / M.Des (Fashion Design)
  • B.Des / M.Des (Interior Design)
  • B.Des (Graphic Design & Visual Communication)
  • B.Des (Product Design)
  • B.Sc / M.Sc Animation & Multimedia (including 3D Animation & VFX)
  • B.Sc Game Design & Development
  • B.Sc / M.Sc Cinema (Specializations in Direction, Cinematography, and Editing)
  • B.A / M.A Journalism & Mass Communication
  • B.A / M.A Advertising & Brand Communication
  • B.A / M.A Event Management
  • B.Sc / M.Sc Digital Marketing
  • B.Sc in Sound Design
  • B.A / M.A in Music Production & Performing Arts
  • B.A in Acting & Film Presentation
  • Bachelor of Fine Arts (BFA) / Master of Fine Arts (MFA)
  • B.Sc / M.Sc Data Science & Artificial Intelligence
  • MBA Media & Entertainment Management
  • Other PG Diploma, Diploma, and Certificate Programmes offered by AAFT

These courses prepare students for careers in design, fashion, animation, filmmaking, sound engineering, journalism, music, digital marketing, event management, media, advertising, photography, performing arts, and related creative industries.
