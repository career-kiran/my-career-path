🎓 How to Become an Illustrator

After 10th Grade (Art Foundations)

Courses:

  • Diploma in Commercial Art
  • Certificate in Digital Illustration
  • Diploma in Fine Arts (DFA)

Duration: 6 months to 1 year

Eligibility: 10th pass from any recognized board

Minimum Marks: Usually around 45–50%

Age Limit: Minimum around 14 years

Exam to Pass: Usually direct admission. Some institutes may ask for a small drawing test or portfolio of your sketches.

After 12th Grade (Professional Degrees)

Courses:

  • BFA (Bachelor of Fine Arts)
  • B.Des in Communication Design
  • B.Des in Illustration / Animation

Duration: Around 4 years

Eligibility: 10+2 in any stream (Arts preferred, but Science or Commerce is also accepted)

Minimum Marks: Usually around 50%

Age Limit: Around 17–23 years for some government institutes; most private colleges have no strict age limit

Exam to Pass: NID DAT, UCEED, SEED, NIFT, or institute-specific drawing tests.

After Graduation (Specialisation)

Courses:

  • M.Des in Visual Communication
  • MFA (Master of Fine Arts)
  • PG Diploma in Illustration

Duration: Around 2 years

Eligibility: Graduation in any field (BFA or B.Des preferred)

Minimum Marks: Usually around 50% in graduation

Age Limit: Usually no upper age limit

Exam to Pass: CEED, NID DAT (M.Des), or portfolio review and interview.

📝 Exam Details

NID DAT UCEED NIFT SEED CEED

Portfolio Review: You may be asked to show your drawings, sketches, or digital artwork to demonstrate your creativity and illustration skills.

Basic Creative Test: Some institutes may ask you to draw a simple object, character, or scene to evaluate your drawing ability and imagination.

Interview: The institute may ask about your interest in art, your creative ideas, and the work you included in your portfolio.

Note: Different institutes may offer illustration courses under different names such as Fine Arts, Communication Design, Animation, or Digital Illustration.

Even if the course name is different, many of them teach similar skills like drawing, visual storytelling, character design, and digital illustration.

Before choosing a course, it is important to check the subjects taught, faculty, facilities, and portfolio opportunities offered by the institute.

🏢 Where do Illustrators Work?

  • Book Illustrator – work with publishing houses to create illustrations for storybooks, novels, and educational books.
  • Concept Artist – work in animation studios, film production companies, and game studios to design characters, environments, and scenes.
  • Advertising Illustrator – create illustrations for advertisements, posters, and marketing campaigns in advertising agencies.
  • Editorial Illustrator – create illustrations for magazines, newspapers, and online articles.
  • Freelance Illustrator – work independently and create illustrations for different clients, brands, or publishers.

🌍 Global Opportunities

  • International Publishing Houses – create illustrations for children's books, comics, and novels published around the world.
  • Animation and Game Studios – design characters, environments, and concept artwork for international animation films and video games.
  • Advertising and Media Companies – create illustrations used in global marketing campaigns, posters, and digital media content.
  • Online Freelance Platforms – work with international clients through freelance websites and remote projects.
  • Digital Content Creation – create illustrations for global websites, apps, and online publications.

⭐ Career Insight

Illustrators create drawings that help explain ideas and tell stories. Their artwork is used in books, magazines, advertisements, games, and digital media.

Many illustrators begin by creating artwork for books, websites, or online content. With experience, they may work in areas such as character design, concept art, or digital illustration.

In this career, creativity and drawing skills are very important. Many illustrators also build a portfolio(artwork collection) of their artwork and work with different clients or companies.
