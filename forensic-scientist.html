Astronomer

Who is an Astronomer?

An astronomer is a scientist who studies stars, planets, galaxies, and the universe. They try to understand how space works and how celestial objects are formed.

What Does an Astronomer Do?

  • Studies stars, planets, black holes, and galaxies.
  • Uses telescopes and space data to observe space.
  • Analyzes space images and research data.
  • Develops theories about the universe.

You May Enjoy This Career If:

  • You are curious about space and the universe.
  • You enjoy Physics and Mathematics.
  • You like research and discovery.
  • You enjoy solving complex scientific problems.

Real Life Examples:

  • An astronomer studies images from space telescopes to discover new planets.
  • An astronomer analyzes data from satellites to understand black holes.

Courses to Become an Astronomer

B.Sc Physics (3 years)

Eligibility: 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics

Minimum Marks: Usually 50–60%

Admission: Merit-based or entrance exams like CUET

M.Sc Physics / Astrophysics (2 years)

Eligibility: Bachelor’s degree in Physics

Minimum Marks: Usually 50–60%

Admission: University entrance exams, IIT-JAM, or institute-level exams

Ph.D. in Astronomy / Astrophysics (3–5 years)

Eligibility: Master’s degree in Physics / Astrophysics

Admission: Exams like JEST, CSIR-NET, or institute-specific Ph.D entrance exams

Advanced & Research-Focused Programs

Joint M.Sc–Ph.D (Physics with Astrophysics)

Offered by select research institutes for students aiming for advanced research careers.

Integrated M.Tech–Ph.D in Astronomical Instrumentation

Focused on building telescopes and space instruments for advanced scientific research.

Where Do Astronomers Work?

  • Research Scientist – in Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO)
  • Astrophysicist – in Indian Institute of Astrophysics (IIA)
  • Research Fellow – in Tata Institute of Fundamental Research (TIFR)
  • Professor / Lecturer – in universities and colleges
  • Observatory Scientist – in astronomical observatories

🌍 Global Opportunities

  • Research Scientist – in international space agencies like NASA and ESA
  • Astrophysicist – in global research institutes and observatories
  • Professor / Researcher – in universities abroad

Important Note:

To become a professional astronomer, higher studies (Master’s and Ph.D.) are usually required. Strong knowledge in Physics and Mathematics is very important in this field.
