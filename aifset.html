AIFSET (All India Forensic Science Entrance Test)

AIFSET is a national-level entrance examination conducted for admission to B.Sc. and M.Sc. Forensic Science courses offered by participating universities and institutes across India.

The examination is also used to award scholarships to eligible students based on their performance.

A student writes the AIFSET examination and receives a score.

Based on the score, students may become eligible for admission and scholarships in participating universities and institutes.

Conducted by: AIFSET Examination Board.

📌 Quick Facts

Eligibility:

Class 12 pass - B.Sc. Forensic Science

Bachelor's degree - M.Sc. Forensic Science.

Exam Pattern: Computer-Based Test (CBT) consisting of objective-type questions.

Mode: Online Computer-Based Test (CBT).

Total Questions: 100 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs).

Total Marks: 100 Marks.

Duration: 60 Minutes.

Frequency: Conducted annually in multiple phases.

Qualification:

  • For Undergraduate (UG) courses: Candidates must have passed Class 12 (10+2) with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology or Mathematics.
  • For Postgraduate (PG) courses: Candidates must have a Bachelor's degree in Forensic Science or a Science-related subject.
  • Appearing Candidates: Students appearing for their final Class 12 or Graduation examinations can also apply.

Age Limit:

  • There is no upper or lower age limit for appearing in AIFSET.

Minimum Marks:

  • For Undergraduate (UG) courses: Candidates should have at least 50% aggregate marks in Class 12. Minimum marks requirements may vary depending on the participating university or institute.
  • For Postgraduate (PG) courses: Candidates should have at least 50% aggregate marks in their Bachelor's degree. Minimum marks requirements may vary depending on the participating university or institute.

Attempts:

  • Candidates can appear for the AIFSET examination up to three times during one admission cycle.
  • Example: If a student applies for AIFSET in 2026, they can take the examination up to three times during the 2026 admission cycle. If the student applies again in 2027, they can again take the examination up to three times during the 2027 admission cycle.

Mode: Online Remote-Proctored Test (can be taken from home using a mobile phone, laptop, or desktop with an internet connection).

Frequency: Conducted annually in multiple phases.

Duration: 60 Minutes.

Total Questions: 100 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs).

Total Marks: 100 Marks.

Question Type: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs).

Question Paper Structure:

The question paper varies depending on whether the candidate is applying for an Undergraduate (UG) or Postgraduate (PG) course.

  • UG Courses: Questions are based on Physics, Chemistry, Biology/Mathematics, English, and General Knowledge.
  • PG Courses: Questions are based on Forensic Science and subjects studied during the Bachelor's degree.

Marking Scheme:

  • Each correct answer carries 1 mark.
  • No Negative Marking: No marks are deducted for incorrect answers.

Important: AIFSET scores are used for admission and scholarship selection in participating universities and institutes.

AIFSET scores are used for admission and merit-based scholarships in forensic science courses offered by participating universities and institutes across India.

Courses may include:

  • Undergraduate (UG) Programmes
    • B.Sc. Forensic Science
    • Integrated B.Sc. + M.Sc. Forensic Science
  • Postgraduate (PG) Programmes
    • M.Sc. Forensic Science
    • M.Sc. Cyber Forensics
    • M.Sc. Forensic Toxicology
    • M.Sc. Digital Forensics

Note: The courses and specializations offered may vary depending on the participating university or institute.
