Nutritionist / Dietitian

A nutritionist or dietitian helps people improve their health by guiding them on food, diet, and eating habits. They work with healthy individuals as well as people with medical conditions.

You may enjoy this career if you…

  • Like helping people improve their health through food
  • Are interested in how diet affects the body
  • Enjoy explaining things in a simple way
  • Are patient and comfortable working with people

Courses to become a Nutritionist / Dietitian

BSc Nutrition / Dietetics

Duration: 3 years

Eligibility:

  • 12th standard completed
  • Biology required
  • Minimum marks: 50–60%

Admission process: Merit-based

Merit-based admission Admission is given based on your 12th standard marks, without a separate entrance exam.

Example: If a college has 50 seats and 200 students apply, a merit list is prepared using marks. Students with higher marks get priority, and the top 50 students are admitted.

BSc Clinical Nutrition

Duration: 3 years

Eligibility:

  • Completed 12th standard
  • Biology is usually required
  • Minimum marks: 50–60% (varies by college)

Admission process: Mostly merit-based

Merit-based admission Admission is given based on your 12th standard marks, without a separate entrance exam.

Example: If a college has 50 seats and 200 students apply, a merit list is prepared using marks. Students with higher marks get priority, and the top 50 students are admitted.

Focus of this course:
Clinical nutrition focuses on diet planning for people with illnesses such as diabetes, heart disease, obesity, and digestive disorders.

BSc Home Science (Nutrition specialisation)

Duration: 3 years

Eligibility:

  • Completed 12th standard
  • Science stream preferred
  • Biology is recommended
  • Minimum marks: 50–60% (varies by college)

Admission process: Merit-based or university-level entrance exam

Eligibility note Home Science is a broad course. Students interested in nutrition should ensure that the college offers a nutrition or dietetics specialisation.

Focus of this course:
This course includes nutrition along with related areas such as food science, health, and human development.

MSc Nutrition / Dietetics

Duration: 2 years

Eligibility:

  • BSc Nutrition / Dietetics / Clinical Nutrition / Home Science
  • Minimum marks: 50–55%

Admission process: University-level entrance exam or merit-based

University-level entrance exam An entrance exam conducted by a specific university for admission to its own courses.

Diploma in Nutrition & Dietetics

Duration: 6 months – 1 year

Eligibility:

  • 12th standard pass (science preferred)

Admission process: Direct admission

Direct admission Students can apply directly to the college without an entrance exam. This does not mean low quality—it simply means the college uses its own selection criteria.

Where do Nutritionists / Dietitians work?

  • Hospitals and clinics
  • Wellness centres and gyms
  • Schools and colleges
  • Sports and fitness centres
  • Private practice (after experience)

Important note

Clinical dietitians working in hospitals usually need a formal bachelor’s or master’s degree along with practical training. Short-term courses are useful but have limitations.
