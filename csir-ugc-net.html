CSIR UGC NET (Council of Scientific and Industrial Research - University Grants Commission - National Eligibility Test)

A student writes the CSIR UGC NET examination after completing a master's degree in a Science or Technology subject.

This examination is mainly conducted to determine eligibility for three opportunities:

  • Junior Research Fellowship (JRF) - Research
  • Assistant Professor - Teaching
  • Ph.D. Admission - Higher studies

The opportunities a candidate receives depend on their performance in the examination.

Candidates with higher scores may become eligible for JRF, others may qualify for Assistant Professor positions and Ph.D. admissions.

Conducted by: National Testing Agency (NTA) on behalf of CSIR and UGC.

📌 Quick Facts

Who Can Apply? Students with eligible Science qualifications such as M.Sc., B.Tech., B.E., B.S., B.Pharm., MBBS, and related degrees.

Mode: Computer Based Test (CBT)

Questions: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

Frequency: Conducted twice a year (Usually June & December)

Qualification: Relevant Science qualification from a recognised university.

Eligible Degrees:

  • M.Sc.
  • Integrated BS-MS
  • B.E. / B.Tech.
  • B.S.
  • B.Pharm.
  • MBBS
  • Other equivalent degrees

Age:

  • JRF: Maximum 30 years (relaxation as per government rules)
  • Assistant Professor / Ph.D. Admission: No upper age limit

Minimum Marks:

  • 55% - General / EWS / OBC
  • 50% - SC / ST / PwD

Attempts: No restriction on the number of attempts.

Mode: Computer Based Test (CBT)

Question Type: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

Exam Frequency: Conducted twice a year (June & December)

Duration: 3 Hours

Subjects: Depends on the subject selected by the candidate

Total Questions: 200

Total Marks: 200 Marks

Question Paper Structure:

  • Part A: General Aptitude
  • Part B: Subject-related Questions
  • Part C: Advanced Subject-related Questions

Marking Scheme: Varies depending on the subject paper.

CSIR UGC NET is conducted in the following subjects:

  • Chemical Sciences
  • Earth, Atmospheric, Ocean and Planetary Sciences
  • Life Sciences
  • Mathematical Sciences
  • Physical Sciences

Candidates who qualify CSIR UGC NET may become eligible for the following opportunities:

  • Junior Research Fellowship (JRF): Receive financial support while pursuing research or a Ph.D.
  • Assistant Professor: Become eligible to apply for teaching positions in colleges and universities.
  • Ph.D. Admission: Apply for Ph.D. programmes in universities and research institutions.

The opportunities available to a candidate depend on their performance in the examination.

Generally, candidates with higher scores may become eligible for JRF, while others may qualify for Assistant Professor positions and Ph.D. admissions.
