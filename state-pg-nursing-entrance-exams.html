State PG Nursing Entrance Exams

What are State PG Nursing Entrance Exams?

State PG Nursing Entrance Exams are entrance examinations conducted by various state authorities, universities and nursing institutes for admission to postgraduate nursing courses in India.

There is no single national entrance examination for admission to M.Sc Nursing courses in India.

Conducted by: Various state authorities, universities and nursing institutes.

📌 Quick Facts

Eligibility: B.Sc Nursing or Post Basic B.Sc Nursing

Mode: Online or Offline (varies by state)

Duration: Varies by State

Questions: Varies by State

Frequency: Usually conducted once a year

Qualification: B.Sc Nursing or Post Basic B.Sc Nursing from a recognised institution.

Registration: Registered Nurse and Registered Midwife (RN/RM).

Experience: Some states and institutions may require clinical work experience.

Minimum Marks: Minimum qualifying marks may vary by state and institution.

Domicile: Some states may require candidates to be residents of that state.

Mode: Online or Offline (varies by state)

Duration: Varies by State

Total Questions: Varies by State

Question Type: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

Marking Scheme: Varies by State
