Physiotherapist

Physiotherapy is a healthcare profession where you help people to improve their daily functions after an injury,illness or other physical problems

What does a physiotherapist usually do?

  • Help patients recover after injury or surgery
  • Treat pain and stiffness in muscles or joints
  • Assist people with physical disabilities
  • Guide exercises for better movement and strength

You may enjoy this career if you

  • Like helping people recover and feel better
  • Are interested in how the human body moves
  • Prefer practical, hands-on work
  • Are comfortable working closely with people

Courses to become a Physiotherapist (India)

Bachelor of Physiotherapy (BPT)

Duration: 4 years + 6 months internship

Eligibility: 12th with Biology

Minimum marks: Usually 45–50%

Entrance exam: State-level entrance / NEET-UG (some colleges)

Diploma in Physiotherapy (DPT)

Duration: 2–3 years

Eligibility: 10+2 pass (Science preferred)

Minimum marks: Usually 40–45%

Entrance exam: Institute-level / merit-based

Diploma holders usually work under the supervision of a qualified physiotherapist. To work independently, a Bachelor of Physiotherapy (BPT) degree is required.

Where physiotherapists work

  • Hospitals and rehabilitation centres
  • Physiotherapy clinics
  • Sports centres and fitness clinics
  • Old-age care homes
  • Private practice or home care
