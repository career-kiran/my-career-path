🎓How to Become a Hardware Engineer

This is a good starting point if you want to enter the hardware field early and gain practical skills.

With a diploma, you can start working as a hardware technician.
To become a hardware engineer, after this diploma course you need to continue your studies through engineering course(usually via lateral entry).

Diploma in Electronics / Computer Hardware / Electrical

Duration: 3 years

Eligibility: 10th Pass with Science and Mathematics

Minimum Marks: Usually 35% – 50%

Age Limit: Minimum 14–16 years

Exam: State Polytechnic Entrance Exams

✏️ Exam Details

(Click on the exam name to see full details)

State Polytechnic Exams

Note: Some institutes also offer direct admission based on marks.

This is the most common path to become a hardware engineer.

B.Tech / B.E in Electronics & Communication / Computer Engineering

Duration: 4 years

Eligibility: 12th with Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics (PCM)

Minimum Marks: Usually 45% – 60%

Age Limit: Minimum 17 years

Exam: JEE Main / State CET / JEE Advanced /University Level Exam

✏️ Exam Details

(Click on the exam name to see full details)

JEE Main JEE Advanced State Engineering Entrance Exams University-Level Entrance Exams

Note: Some colleges offer admission based on marks without entrance exams.

This allows you to join directly into the 2nd year of engineering and move towards becoming a hardware engineer.

B.Tech (Lateral Entry) in Electronics / Computer Engineering

Duration: 3 years (Direct 2nd Year Entry)

Eligibility: 3-year Diploma in relevant field

Minimum Marks: Usually 45% – 60%

Age Limit: Usually up to 24–25 years

Exam: State Lateral Entry Entrance Exams

✏️ Exam Details

(Click on the exam name to see full details)

State Lateral Entry Exams

Note: This path helps diploma students become engineers by continuing their studies.

After completing your degree, you can specialize in advanced hardware fields and improve your career opportunities.

M.Tech in Electronics / Embedded Systems / VLSI

Duration: 2 years

Eligibility: B.E / B.Tech in relevant field

Minimum Marks: Usually 50% – 60%

Age Limit: No strict limit

Exam: GATE

  • Hardware Engineer – work in electronics companies designing and building devices like computers, chips, and circuit boards.
  • Embedded Systems Engineer – work in companies developing systems inside devices like smart appliances, cars, and IoT products.
  • Electronics Design Engineer – work in industries creating circuit designs and electronic systems.
  • Hardware Test Engineer – work in companies testing and checking if hardware devices work properly.
  • PCB Design Engineer – work in electronics companies designing circuit boards used in devices.
  • Field Service Engineer – work in companies installing, maintaining, and repairing hardware systems.
  • Electronics Manufacturing Companies – work in global companies producing electronic components.
  • Semiconductor Companies – work in companies designing and manufacturing chips.
  • Consumer Electronics Companies – work in brands creating TVs, smartphones, etc.
  • Automotive Companies – work in companies developing electronic systems for vehicles.
  • Embedded Systems & IoT Companies – build smart devices and connected systems.
  • Research & Development (R&D) Labs – develop new technologies and innovations.

Many hardware engineers start by learning how electronic devices and circuits work, along with basic programming and electronics.

They gain practical skills by working with tools, components, and systems through projects, labs, or internships.

With experience, they can specialize in areas like embedded systems, VLSI design, computer hardware, or electronics systems.

Experienced professionals can work in top tech companies, design advanced hardware systems, or work globally on innovative technology projects.
