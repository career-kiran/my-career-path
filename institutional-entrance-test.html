Institutional Entrance Test

Institutional Entrance Test is an entrance examination conducted by individual colleges, universities, or training institutes before giving admission to their own courses.

Institutional Entrance Test is valid only for admission to the institute that conducts the examination.

The purpose of the test is to assess whether a student has the knowledge and skills required for the chosen course.

The syllabus, exam pattern, and selection process vary from one institute to another.

Example:

A student applies for a Diploma in Web Designing & Development.

The institute conducts its own entrance examination to assess the student's basic knowledge and skills.

Based on the student's performance, the institute decides whether to offer admission or not.
