Medical Records Technician

A Medical Records Technician manages patient information in hospitals and clinics. They make sure medical records are accurate, organized, and safely stored so doctors and hospitals can use them when needed.

What does a Medical Records Technician usually do?

  • Maintain patient medical records (paper or computer-based)
  • Enter and update patient details in hospital computers
  • Keep reports like test results and prescriptions neatly organized
  • Make sure patient information is stored safely and not shared wrongly
  • Help doctors and hospital staff access correct records when required

You may enjoy this career if you:

  • Like working with computers and information
  • Prefer organized, desk-based work in healthcare
  • Pay attention to details and accuracy
  • Want to work in hospitals without direct patient care
  • Like working in an organized way and following clear instructions
Real-life example:
After a doctor sees a patient, the medical records technician enters all the patient details into the computer. This helps the doctor review the information during the patient’s next visit.

Courses Available

Diploma in Medical Records Technology

Trains students to manage hospital records, patient data, medical documentation, and basic medical coding.

  • Duration: 1 – 2 years
  • Eligibility: 12th pass (any stream)
  • Entrance exam: Usually not required
  • Admission: Merit-based or direct admission

Certificate Course in Medical Records / Health Information Management

A short-term course focusing on basic medical documentation, record handling, and hospital information systems.

  • Duration: 6 months – 1 year
  • Eligibility: 10th or 12th pass
  • Entrance exam: Not required
  • Admission: Direct admission

Bachelor’s Degree (Advanced Option)

Degree-level courses for students who wish to move into higher roles in health information and hospital administration.

  • Examples: BSc Health Information Management, BSc Medical Records Science
  • Duration: 3 years
  • Eligibility: 12th pass (science preferred in many colleges)

Where do Medical Records Technicians work?

  • Hospitals
  • Clinics
  • Diagnostic centers
  • Health insurance companies
  • Medical colleges and hospitals
Important note:
Course names, eligibility criteria, and admission rules may vary by institute and state. Always check the official college or hospital website before enrolling.
