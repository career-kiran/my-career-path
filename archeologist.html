Who is an Archaeologist?

An archaeologist studies the past by examining old objects, buildings, bones, tools, and ancient sites. They help us understand how people lived thousands of years ago.

What does an Archaeologist usually do?

  • Excavates (carefully digs) historical sites
  • Studies ancient tools, pottery, bones, and monuments
  • Records and preserves historical findings
  • Writes reports about discoveries

You may enjoy this career if you:

  • Are curious about history and ancient civilizations
  • Enjoy exploring old places and discovering hidden stories
  • Like research and careful observation

Real Life Examples:

  • An archaeologist discovers ancient coins or pottery during a temple excavation.
  • An archaeologist studies old human bones to understand how people lived long ago.

Courses to Become an Archaeologist

B.A / B.Sc in Archaeology (3 years)

Eligibility: 10+2 (Arts or Science stream)

Minimum Marks: Usually 50–60% (varies by college)

Admission:

  • Merit-based in many colleges
  • CUET-UG (Common University Entrance Test – Undergraduate) for central universities
  • University-level entrance exams (varies by institution)

M.A / M.Sc in Archaeology (2 years)

Eligibility: Bachelor’s degree in Archaeology / History / Anthropology or related subject

Minimum Marks: Usually 50–60%

Admission:

  • University entrance exams
  • CUET-PG (for some universities)

Ph.D in Archaeology (3–5 years)

Eligibility: Master’s degree in Archaeology or related subject

Admission:

  • UGC-NET (University Grants Commission – National Eligibility Test)
  • University-level Ph.D entrance exams

Advanced Specialised Courses:

  • Post Graduate Diploma in Archaeology – Offered by the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI). This is useful for working in government archaeology departments.
  • Professional Diploma in Forensic Archaeology – Focuses on excavation techniques used in criminal and forensic investigations.

Where Do Archaeologists Work?

  • Archaeologist – in the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI)
  • Site Officer / Excavation Officer – in state archaeology departments
  • Museum Curator / Research Assistant – in museums
  • Heritage Consultant – in heritage and tourism organisations
  • Professor / Lecturer – in universities and colleges

Important Note:

For senior research roles or professor positions, a Master’s degree or Ph.D is usually required. Fieldwork experience is also very important in this profession.
