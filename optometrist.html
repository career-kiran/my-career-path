Optometrist

An optometrist is a healthcare professional who checks people’s eyesight, detects vision problems, and helps improve eye health using glasses, contact lenses, and basic eye care.

What does an optometrist usually do?

  • Checks eyesight and tests vision
  • Identifies vision problems like short-sightedness or long-sightedness
  • Prescribes glasses or contact lenses
  • Advises on eye care and eye hygiene
  • Refers patients to eye doctors if serious problems are found
Real-life example
A person cannot see the board clearly in class or has trouble reading small letters. An optometrist checks their eyesight and prescribes suitable glasses so they can see clearly.

You may enjoy this career if you:

  • Like helping people improve their vision
  • Enjoy working with precision and attention to detail
  • Are comfortable working one-to-one with patients
  • Are interested in eye health and vision care
  • Prefer practical and focused healthcare work

Courses to become an Optometrist (India)

Bachelor of Optometry (B.Optom)

Duration: 4 years (3 years study + 1 year internship)

Eligibility:

  • 12th standard with Physics, Chemistry, Biology
  • Minimum marks: usually 45–50%

Admission process: Merit-based or institute/state-level entrance exam (for example: KCET,KEAM,TS/AP EAMCET - depending on the state and institution)

Note: Admission process and entrance exams may vary by college. Always check the official college or university website for exact details.

Master of Optometry (M.Optom) (optional)

Duration: 2 years

Eligibility: Bachelor of Optometry (B.Optom)

Focus: Advanced training in areas such as clinical optometry, contact lenses, or vision therapy.

Where do optometrists work?

  • Eye hospitals and clinics
  • Optical showrooms
  • Vision care centres
  • Hospitals and healthcare institutions
  • Private practice

Important note

Optometrists focus on vision testing and eye care, but they do not perform eye surgeries. For surgical or complex eye conditions, patients are referred to ophthalmologists.
