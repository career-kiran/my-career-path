GAT-B (Graduate Aptitude Test – Biotechnology)

GAT-B is a national-level entrance examination conducted for admission to postgraduate programmes in Biotechnology and allied areas.

Students can use their GAT-B score to apply for postgraduate biotechnology programmes offered by participating institutions in India.

Conducted by: National Testing Agency (NTA) on behalf of the Department of Biotechnology (DBT), Government of India.

📌 Quick Facts

Eligibility: Bachelor's Degree or Appearing in Final Year

Mode: Computer Based Test (CBT)

Questions: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

Frequency: Conducted once a year (April or May)

Qualification: Bachelor's degree from a recognised university or appearing in final year .

Appearing = currently in final year (writing exams this year)

Eligible Degrees:

  • B.Sc.
  • B.Tech. / B.E.
  • B.V.Sc.
  • B.Pharm.
  • MBBS
  • BDS
  • Other equivalent degrees

Age: No age limit.

Minimum Marks:

  • 55%–60% aggregate marks for General/OBC/EWS categories (may vary by institution)
  • 50%–55% aggregate marks for SC/ST/PwD categories (may vary by institution)

Attempts: No restriction on the number of attempts.

Mode: Computer Based Test (CBT)

Question Type: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

Exam Frequency: Conducted once a year (April or May)

Duration: 3 Hours

Subjects: Biotechnology and Life Sciences

Total Questions: 160

Total Marks: 240 Marks

Marking Scheme:

  • +3 - for every correct answer
  • -1 - for every wrong answer
  • 0 - for unattempted questions

GAT-B scores may be used for admission to postgraduate programmes in Biotechnology and related fields.

Courses may include:
  • M.Sc. Biotechnology
  • M.Sc. Medical Biotechnology
  • M.Sc. Marine Biotechnology
  • M.Sc. Industrial Biotechnology
  • M.Sc. Agricultural Biotechnology
  • M.Sc. Molecular Biology and Biotechnology
  • M.Sc. Molecular and Human Genetics
  • M.Sc. Bioresources Biotechnology
  • M.Tech. Biotechnology
  • M.Tech. Computational Biology
  • M.Tech. Industrial Biotechnology
  • M.V.Sc. Animal Biotechnology
