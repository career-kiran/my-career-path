Scientist / Researcher

Scientist

A scientist usually works in science fields like Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Mathematics, Environmental Science and related subjects.

If you enjoy thinking deeply about one topic for a long time, this path might suit you.

Real Life Examples (India)

  • Scientists at ISRO working on satellite missions.
  • Environmental scientists studying pollution and climate change.

Course Path

Step 1 – Bachelor’s Degree (3 years)

BSc in Physics / Chemistry / Biology / Mathematics etc.
Eligibility: 12th Science (PCM/PCB)
Minimum marks: Usually 50–60%

Entrance exams (if required):
  • CUET (UG)
  • NEST (for NISER)
  • IISER Aptitude Test (IAT)
  • State-level university exams
Step 2 – Master’s Degree (2 years)

MSc in related subject
Eligibility: BSc in relevant field

Entrance exams:
  • IIT JAM
  • CUET-PG
  • University-level entrance exams
Step 3 – PhD (3–5 years)

Research specialization in chosen topic
Eligibility: MSc

Entrance exams / Qualification tests:
  • UGC-NET
  • CSIR-NET
  • GATE (some fields)
  • Institute-level PhD entrance exams

Where do Scientists Work?

  • ISRO (Indian Space Research Organisation)
  • DRDO (Defence Research and Development Organisation)
  • ICMR (Indian Council of Medical Research)
  • Universities and research institutes
  • Private research labs and companies

Researcher

Research means trying to find new knowledge. You are not just studying what already exists — you are asking deeper questions and discovering something new.

Researcher (Any Field)

A researcher can work in any field — not only science. Research means studying a topic deeply and discovering new understanding.

You can do research in Psychology, Economics, History, Public Policy, Education, Commerce, Law, Media and many other fields.

Real Life Examples

  • A psychology researcher studying mental health among teenagers.
  • An economist researching poverty and development in rural India.

Course Path

Step 1 – Bachelor’s Degree (3 years)

BA / BSc / BCom or relevant undergraduate degree
Eligibility: 12th (varies by course)

Entrance exams (if required):
  • CUET (UG)
  • State-level university entrance exams
Step 2 – Master’s Degree (2 years)

MA / MSc / MCom depending on field
Eligibility: Bachelor’s degree in related subject

Entrance exams:
  • CUET-PG
  • University-level entrance exams
Step 3 – PhD (3–5 years)

Research specialization in chosen field
Eligibility: Master’s degree

Entrance exams / Qualification tests:
  • UGC-NET
  • JRF (Junior Research Fellowship)
  • Institute-level PhD entrance exams
  • State-level eligibility tests

Where do Researchers Work?

  • Universities and colleges
  • Research institutes
  • Government organisations
  • NGOs
  • Think tanks
  • Private companies
Important to Know

Research careers require patience. It may take 8–10 years of higher studies. If you truly enjoy deep learning and discovery, this path can feel meaningful and fulfilling.
