Institute Drawing Test

Institute Drawing Test is an admission test conducted by individual colleges and training institutes before giving admission to their creative courses.

It is mainly conducted for courses such as Fine Arts, Applied Arts, Animation, Graphic Design, Fashion Design, Interior Design, and other creative programmes.

The purpose of the test is to assess a student's drawing skills, observation, creativity, imagination, and visual thinking.

Students may be asked to draw simple objects, sketch a scene, complete an unfinished picture, create a poster, or illustrate a given topic.

Example:

A student applies for a Diploma in Applied Arts.

The college asks the student to draw a simple object, such as a flower vase, a house, or a landscape.

The college evaluates the student's drawing skills, observation, creativity, and neatness.

Based on the student's performance, the college decides whether to offer admission or not.
