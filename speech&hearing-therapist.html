Speech and Hearing Therapist

A Speech and Hearing Therapist helps people with difficulties in speech, language, communication, hearing, and swallowing. They work with both children and adults to improve communication and daily functioning.

Real-life example A 4-year-old child is not able to pronounce words clearly and struggles to speak in sentences. A Speech and Hearing Therapist uses simple exercises and play-based activities to improve speech clarity and guides parents on how to practise at home. Over time, the child’s communication improves.

You may enjoy this career if you…

  • Like helping people communicate better
  • Are willing to listen patiently and work one-to-one
  • Enjoy working with children, adults, or elderly people
  • Are interested in speech, hearing, and communication

Courses to become a Speech and Hearing Therapist (India)

Bachelor in Audiology and Speech-Language Pathology (BASLP)

Duration: 4 years (including internship)

Eligibility:

  • 12th standard with Physics, Chemistry, Biology
  • Minimum marks: 45–50% (varies by college)

Admission process: Merit-based or institute/state-level entrance exam

Entrance exam information There is no single national-level entrance exam for BASLP. Admission is usually based on 12th marks or entrance exams conducted by individual colleges or universities.

Master in Audiology and Speech-Language Pathology (MASLP)

Duration: 2 years

Eligibility:

  • Completed BASLP
  • Minimum marks: 50–55%

Admission process: Institute-level or university-level entrance exam

Where do Speech and Hearing Therapists work?

  • Hospitals and rehabilitation centres
  • Speech therapy clinics
  • Schools and special education centres
  • Hearing aid centres
  • NGOs and community health programs
  • Private practice

Important note

To practise as a Speech and Hearing Therapist in India, registration with the Rehabilitation Council of India (RCI) is required.
