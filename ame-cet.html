AME CET | Aircraft Maintenance Engineering Common Entrance Test

AME CET is a national-level entrance examination conducted for admission to various aviation courses offered by participating institutes and universities across India.

The examination is also used to award scholarships to eligible students based on their performance.

A student writes the AME CET examination and receives an All India Rank (AIR).

Based on the All India Rank (AIR), students may become eligible for admission and scholarships in participating aviation institutes and universities.

Conducted by: Udai Aviation Private Limited.

📌 Quick Facts

Eligibility: Class 10, Class 12, Diploma, or Graduation (varies depending on the course).

Mode: Computer-Based Test (CBT) or Offline Pen-and-Paper Test.

Total Questions: 90 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs).

Total Marks: 360 Marks.

Duration: 90 Minutes.

Frequency: Conducted in three phases every year (usually in May, June, and July).

Qualification:

  • For Aircraft Maintenance Engineering (AME): Candidates must have passed Class 12 (10+2) with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM) or hold a 3-year Engineering Diploma in a relevant stream.
  • For Aeronautical and Aerospace Engineering: Candidates must have passed Class 12 (10+2) with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (PCM).
  • For Commercial Pilot Licence (CPL): Candidates must have passed Class 12 (10+2) with Physics and Mathematics.
  • For Other Aviation Courses: Eligibility varies depending on the course. Some courses require Class 12, while others may accept Class 10 or Diploma qualifications.
  • Appearing Candidates: Students appearing for their final Class 10, Class 12, Diploma, or Graduation examinations can also apply, depending on the course.

Age Limit:

  • Vary depending on the course and the participating institute.

Minimum Marks:

  • Vary depending on the course and the participating institute.
  • Medical Fitness: Some aviation courses require candidates to meet the prescribed medical fitness standards.

Attempts:

  • Candidates can appear for AME CET a maximum of 3 times.
  • AME CET is usually conducted in three phases every year. Students can choose the phase that suits them.
  • Example: If a student appears in Phase 1, it counts as one attempt. If they are not satisfied with their rank, they can appear again in Phase 2 or Phase 3. Each appearance counts as one attempt.
  • Best Rank: If a student appears in more than one phase, the best rank is generally considered during the admission process.

Mode: Online (Computer-Based Test) and Offline (Pen-and-Paper Test).

Frequency: Conducted in three phases every year (usually in May, June, and July).

Duration: 90 Minutes.

Total Questions: 90 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs).

Total Marks: 360 Marks.

Question Type: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs).

Question Paper Structure:

The question paper depends on the candidate's educational qualification.

  • For 12th PCM Students: Questions from Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, English, and General Awareness.
  • For 12th Non-PCM Students: Questions from English, General Awareness, and General Intelligence & Reasoning.
  • For Diploma or Other Qualifications: The question paper varies depending on the candidate's qualification and the course selected.

Marking Scheme:

  • Correct Answer: +4 marks.
  • Incorrect Answer: −1 mark (Negative Marking).
  • Unanswered Questions: 0 marks.

Important:

The exam pattern may vary depending on the candidate's qualification and the course selected.

Candidates should always check the official AME CET notification for the latest exam pattern and syllabus.

AME CET scores are used for admission and scholarships in various aviation courses offered by participating institutes and universities across India.

  • Aircraft Maintenance Engineering Programmes

  • Pilot Training Programmes

  • Aviation Management & Other Programmes
    • BBA in Aviation

    • Airport Management

Note:

The courses offered may vary depending on the participating institute or university.

Students should always check whether the course is approved by the relevant authority before taking admission.
