Scientist / Researcher

Research means trying to find new knowledge. You are not just studying what already exists — you are asking deeper questions and trying to discover something new.

Scientist

A scientist usually works in science fields like Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Mathematics, Environmental Science and related subjects.

If you enjoy going deep into one topic and thinking about it for a long time, this path might suit you.

Real Life Examples (India)

  • Scientists at ISRO working on satellite missions.
  • Environmental scientists studying pollution and climate change.

Course Path

Step 1 – Bachelor’s Degree (3 years)
BSc in Physics / Chemistry / Biology / Mathematics etc.
Eligibility: 12th Science stream (PCM/PCB)
Minimum marks: Usually 50–60%
Step 2 – Master’s Degree (2 years)
MSc in related subject
Eligibility: BSc in relevant field
Step 3 – PhD (3–5 years)
Research degree in a specific topic
Eligibility: MSc or equivalent
Entrance Exams (May Vary)

• CUET (UG / PG)
• IIT JAM (for MSc Science)
• NEST (for NISER)
• UGC-NET / CSIR-NET
• State-level university entrance exams

Exact exams depend on university and field.

Where do Scientists Work?

  • ISRO, DRDO, ICMR and other government labs
  • Research institutes and universities
  • Private research organizations

Researcher (Any Field)

A researcher can work in any field — not only science. Research means studying a topic deeply and trying to find new understanding.

You can do research in Psychology, Economics, History, Public Policy, Education, Commerce, Law, Media and many other fields.

Real Life Examples

  • A psychology researcher studying mental health among teenagers.
  • An economist researching poverty and development in rural India.

Course Path

Step 1 – Bachelor’s Degree (3 years)
BA / BSc / BCom or relevant undergraduate degree
Eligibility: 12th (any stream, depending on course)
Step 2 – Master’s Degree (2 years)
MA / MSc / MCom / MBA depending on field
Step 3 – PhD (3–5 years)
Research specialization in chosen subject
Entrance Exams for Research Careers

• CUET (UG / PG)
• UGC-NET
• JRF (Junior Research Fellowship)
• CSIR-NET (Science fields)
• GATE (Engineering research)
• State-level eligibility tests

Requirements vary by university and discipline.

Where do Researchers Work?

  • Universities and colleges
  • Think tanks and policy institutes
  • Government research bodies
  • NGOs and international organizations
  • Private companies
Important to Know

Research careers require patience. It may take 8–10 years of higher studies. If you truly enjoy deep learning and discovery, this path can feel meaningful and fulfilling.
