Who is an Astronomer?

An astronomer is a scientist who studies space — stars, planets, galaxies, and the universe.

What does an Astronomer do?

  • Studies stars, planets, and other objects in space.
  • Uses telescopes and computers to analyze space data.
  • Tries to understand how the universe works.
  • Publishes research and shares discoveries.

You may enjoy this career if you:

  • Are curious about space and the universe.
  • Enjoy science and asking big questions.
  • Like observing and analyzing data.

Real Life Examples:

  • An astronomer studies how stars are formed in distant galaxies.
  • An astronomer works with space agencies to study planets like Mars.

Courses to Become an Astronomer

B.Sc in Physics / Astronomy (3 years)

Eligibility: 10+2 with Physics and Mathematics

Minimum Marks: Usually 50–60%

Admission:

  • Merit-based in many colleges
  • CUET-UG (Common University Entrance Test – Undergraduate)
  • State-level or university entrance exams

M.Sc in Physics / Astronomy / Astrophysics (2 years)

Eligibility: Bachelor’s degree in Physics or related field

Minimum Marks: Usually 50–60%

Admission:

  • University entrance exams
  • CUET-PG (Common University Entrance Test – Postgraduate)

Ph.D in Astronomy / Astrophysics (3–5 years)

Eligibility: Master’s degree in Physics / Astronomy

Admission:

  • CSIR-NET (Council of Scientific and Industrial Research – National Eligibility Test)
  • GATE (Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering)
  • JEST (Joint Entrance Screening Test)
  • University-level Ph.D entrance exams

Advanced Research Pathways:

  • Joint M.Sc–Ph.D in Physics with Astrophysics – Offered by institutes like IUCAA.
  • Integrated M.Tech–Ph.D in Astronomical Instrumentation – Offered by Indian Institute of Astrophysics (IIA).
  • Research programs at institutes like TIFR (Tata Institute of Fundamental Research) and PRL (Physical Research Laboratory).

Where Do Astronomers Work?

  • Research Scientist – in Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO)
  • Astrophysicist – in Indian Institute of Astrophysics (IIA)
  • Research Fellow – in Tata Institute of Fundamental Research (TIFR)
  • Professor / Lecturer – in universities and colleges
  • Observatory Scientist – in astronomical observatories

🌍 Global Opportunities

  • Research Scientist – in international space agencies like NASA (National Aeronautics and Space Administration) and ESA (European Space Agency)
  • Astrophysicist – in global research institutes and observatories
  • Professor / Researcher – in universities abroad

Important Note:

To become a professional astronomer, higher studies (Master’s and Ph.D) are usually required. Strong knowledge in Physics and Mathematics is very important in this field.
