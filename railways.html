🚆 Railway Jobs

🚆 What are Railway Jobs?

Railway jobs are government jobs in Indian Railways. They include technical, administrative, operational and support roles across the country.

💖 You may enjoy this path if You:

  • want a stable government job
  • are ready to prepare for competitive exams
  • are interested in technical, clerical, or field work
  • like structured and disciplined work environments

🛤 How to Get Railway Jobs?

You can enter Indian Railways through competitive exams conducted by the Railway Recruitment Board (RRB).

There are three main entry levels:

  • After 10th – Group D level posts
  • After 12th – NTPC 12th level posts
  • After Graduation – NTPC Graduate level, JE, ALP and other technical posts

🎓 Jobs After 10th

Track Maintainer

Eligibility: 10th pass

Age Limit: 18–33 years

Exam to Pass: RRB Group D

Helper / Assistant

Eligibility: 10th pass

Age Limit: 18–33 years

Exam to Pass: RRB Group D

Pointsman

Eligibility: 10th pass

Age Limit: 18–33 years

Exam to Pass: RRB Group D

Track Maintainer:

Maintain and inspect railway tracks to ensure safe train movement.

Helper / Assistant:

Help senior staff with repair and maintenance work

Pointsman:

Control railway tracks and help trains move safely.

Exam to Pass: RRB NTPC

Junior Clerk / Typist

Eligibility: 12th pass

Age Limit: 18–30/33years

Exam to Pass: RRB NTPC

Eligibility: 12th pass

Age Limit: 18–30/33 years

Exam to Pass: RRB NTPC

Ticket Collector (TC):

Check passenger tickets and make sure travels rules are followed.

Junior Clerk / Typist:

🎓 Jobs After Graduation

Station Master

Eligibility: Graduate (any stream)

Age Limit: 18–36 years

Exam to Pass: RRB NTPC

Goods Guard

Eligibility: Graduate

Age Limit: 18–36 years

Exam to Pass: RRB NTPC

Junior Engineer (JE)

Eligibility: Diploma / B.E / B.Tech

Age Limit: 18–36 years

Exam to Pass: RRB JE

Assistant Loco Pilot (ALP)

Eligibility: ITI / Diploma

Age Limit: 18–36 years

Exam to Pass: RRB ALP

Promotion: Can become Loco Pilot after experience and departmental exams.

Station Master:

Manage train timings and overall work at the railway stations.

Goods Guard:

Ensure safe movement and monitoring of goods trains.

Junior Engineer:

Supervise railway infrastructure maintenance and technical operations.

Assistant Loco Pilot (ALP):

Assists the loco pilot in driving the train and ensuring safe travel

Loco Pilot:

A train driver who operates and controls the train.

You usually become a Loco Pilot after working as an Assistant Loco Pilot and gaining experience.

Note: Age limits may vary depending on the official RRB notification and category-wise relaxation rules.

🧾 Selection Process

Railway jobs are filled through exams conducted by the Railway Recruitment Board (RRB). The selection process usually includes the following stages:

    1) Computer Based Test (CBT): An online exam that tests subjects like General Awareness, Mathematics, and Reasoning.

    2) Skill / Physical Test: Required for certain posts such as typing jobs or physically demanding jobs.

    3) Document Verification: Verification of educational certificates and identity documents.

    4) Medical Examination: Candidates must meet medical standards required for railway duties.

⭐ Career Insight

Railway jobs offer job stability, regular salary, promotions and long-term benefits. Consistent preparation and patience are important to succeed.
