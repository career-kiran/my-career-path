University Ph.D. Entrance Exams

University Ph.D. Entrance Exams are examinations conducted by individual universities for admission to Ph.D. and research programmes.

These examinations are usually taken after completing a postgraduate degree.

Examples include: Ph.D. entrance examinations conducted by universities such as Delhi University, Jawaharlal Nehru University (JNU), Banaras Hindu University (BHU), University of Hyderabad, and many other universities across India.

Conducted by: Individual Universities across India.

📌 Quick Facts

Who Can Apply? Students with a postgraduate degree in the relevant subject.

Mode: Varies according to the university

Questions: MCQs, Subjective Questions, or Interview-Based Assessment

Frequency: Depends on the university

Qualification: Postgraduate degree from a recognised university in the relevant subject.

Age: Vary according to the university.

Minimum Marks:

  • Depends on the university and research programme.

Attempts: Vary according to the university.

Note: Candidates must satisfy the eligibility requirements prescribed by the university where they are seeking admission.

Mode: Usually Computer-Based Test (CBT) or Written Examination

Question Type: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs), Subjective Questions, or Interview-Based Assessment

Duration: Varies according to the university

Total Questions: Varies according to the university

Total Marks: Varies according to the university

  • Interview / Viva Voce: Some universities may conduct an interview after the written examination.

    • Marking Scheme:

    • May vary according to the university.

    Medium of Paper: Depends on the university and programme.

    Note: The exam pattern, syllabus, and selection process may vary from one university to another. Candidates should always check the official notification of the university they are applying to.

    Students who qualify a University Ph.D. Entrance Exam may become eligible for admission to Ph.D. and research programmes offered by that university.

    Programmes may include:

    • Ph.D. in Science
    • Ph.D. in Engineering and Technology
    • Ph.D. in Life Sciences
    • Ph.D. in Medical and Health Sciences
    • Ph.D. in Commerce and Management
    • Ph.D. in Humanities and Social Sciences
    • Ph.D. in Education
    • Ph.D. in Law
    • Ph.D. in Agriculture and Allied Sciences
    • Other Research Programmes offered by the university

    Note: The available Ph.D. programmes may vary from one university to another.
