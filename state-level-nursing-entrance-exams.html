State-Level Nursing Entrance Exams

State-Level Nursing Entrance Exams are entrance examinations conducted by individual state governments and institutions for admission to nursing courses in India.

Examples: KEAM (Kerala), KCET (Karnataka), JENPAS UG (West Bengal), MHT CET (Maharashtra), AP EAPCET (Andhra Pradesh) and TS EAMCET (Telangana).

Conducted by: Various state governments, universities and examination authorities.

📌 Quick Facts

Eligibility: 12th Pass with Physics, Chemistry and Biology

Mode: Online or Offline (varies by state)

Duration: Varies by Exam

Questions: Varies by Exam

Frequency: Usually conducted once a year

Qualification: Passed or appearing in 12th with Physics, Chemistry, Biology and English.

Age: Minimum 17 years as on or before 31st December of the admission year.

Minimum Marks:

  • 45% - General/EWS Category
  • 40% - SC/ST/OBC Category

Domicile: Some states may require candidates to be residents of that state.

Mode: Online or Offline (varies by state)

Duration: Varies by Exam

Total Questions: Varies by Exam

Question Type: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

Marking Scheme: Varies by Exam

Questions are generally asked from Physics, Chemistry, Biology and English, though the exact syllabus may vary by examination.

After qualifying in a state-level nursing entrance examination, students can get admission to various nursing courses offered by colleges and universities in their respective states.

Courses include:-
  • B.Sc Nursing
